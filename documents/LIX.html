LIX

LIX is a readability measure indicating the difficulty of reading a text1 developed by Swedish scholar Carl-Hugo Björnsson. It is computed as follows:

LIX = A B + C ⋅ 100 A LIX A B normal-⋅ C 100 A \text{LIX}=\frac{A}{B}+\frac{C\cdot 100}{A} , where

A A A is the number of words,

B B B is the number of periods (defined by period, colon or capital first letter), and

C C C is the number of long words (more than 6 letters).2

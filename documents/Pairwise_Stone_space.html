Pairwise Stone space

In mathematics and particularly in topology, pairwise Stone space is a bitopological space ( X , τ 1 , τ 2 ) X subscript τ 1 subscript τ 2 \scriptstyle(X,\tau_{1},\tau_{2}) which is pairwise compact, pairwise Hausdorff, and pairwise zero-dimensional.

Pairwise Stone spaces are a bitopological version of the Stone spaces.

Pairwise Stone spaces are closely related to spectral spaces.

Conversely, if ( X , τ 1 , τ 2 ) X subscript τ 1 subscript τ 2 \scriptstyle(X,\tau_{1},\tau_{2}) is a pairwise Stone space, then both ( X , τ 1 ) X subscript τ 1 \scriptstyle(X,\tau_{1}) and ( X , τ 2 ) X subscript τ 2 \scriptstyle(X,\tau_{2}) are spectral spaces.

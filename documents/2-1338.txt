It is a non-standard positional numeral system which uses the imaginary number 2 i as its base . It is able to ( almost ) uniquely represent every complex number using only the digits 0, 1, 2, and 3. 1 (Numbers less than zero, which are ordinarily represented with a minus sign, are representable as digit strings in quater-imaginary; for example, the number âˆ’1 is represented as "103" in quater-imaginary notation.) Every complex number (every number of the form a + bi ) has a quater-imaginary representation. Most numbers have a unique quater-imaginary representation, but just as 1 has the two representations 1 = 0.999... in decimal notation, so 1 / 5 has the two quater-imaginary representations 1.(0300)â€¦ 2 i = 0.(0003)â€¦ 2 i .  To convert an arbitrary complex number to quater-imaginary, it is sufficient to split the number into its real and imaginary components, convert each of those separately, and then add the results by interleaving the digits. For example, since â€“1+4 i is equal to â€“1 plus 4 i , the quater-imaginary representation of â€“1+4 i is the quater-imaginary representation of â€“1 (namely, 103) plus the quater-imaginary representation of 4 i (namely, 20), which gives a final result of â€“1+4 i = 123 2 i .  To find the quater-imaginary representation of the imaginary component, it suffices to multiply that component by 2 i , which gives a real number; then find the quater-imaginary representation of that real number, and finally shift the representation by one place to the right (thus dividing by 2 i ). For example, the quater-imaginary representation of 6 i is calculated by multiplying 6 i â€¢ 2 i = â€“12, which is expressed as 300 2 i , and then shifting by one place to the right, yielding: 6 i = 30 2 i . Addition and subtraction  It is possible to add and subtract numbers in the quater-imaginary system. In doing this, there are two basic rules that have to be kept in mind:   Whenever a number exceeds 3, subtract 4 and "carry" âˆ’1 two places to the left.  Whenever a number drops below 0, add 4 and "carry" +1 two places to the left.   Or for short: "If you add four, carry +1 . If you subtract four, carry -1 ". This is the opposite of normal long addition, in which a "carry" in the current column requires adding 1 to the next column to the left, and a "borrow" requires subtracting. In quater-imaginary arithmetic, a "carry" subtracts from the next-but-one column, and a "borrow" adds .

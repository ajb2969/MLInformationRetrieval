   Lie algebroid      Lie algebroid   In mathematics , Lie algebroids serve the same role in the theory of Lie groupoids that Lie algebras serve in the theory of Lie groups : reducing global problems to infinitesimal ones. Just as a Lie groupoid can be thought of as a "Lie group with many objects", a Lie algebroid is like a "Lie algebra with many objects". Examples   Every Lie algebra is a Lie algebroid over the one point manifold.    The tangent bundle    T  M      T  M    TM   of a manifold   M   M   M   is a Lie algebroid for the Lie bracket of vector fields and the identity of    T  M      T  M    TM   as an anchor.    Every integrable subbundle of the tangent bundle ‚Äî that is, one whose sections are closed under the Lie bracket ‚Äî also defines a Lie algebroid.    Every bundle of Lie algebras over a smooth manifold defines a Lie algebroid where the Lie bracket is defined pointwise and the anchor map is equal to zero.    To every Lie groupoid is associated a Lie algebroid, generalizing how a Lie algebra is associated to a Lie group (see also below). For example, the Lie algebroid    T  M      T  M    TM   comes from the pair groupoid whose objects are   M   M   M   , with one isomorphism between each pair of objects.

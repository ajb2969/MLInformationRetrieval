   Kirsch operator      Kirsch operator   The Kirsch operator or Kirsch compass kernel is a non-linear  edge detector that finds the maximum edge strength in a few predetermined directions. It is named after the computer scientist Russell A. Kirsch .  Mathematical description  The operator takes a single kernel mask and rotates it in 45 degree increments through all 8 compass directions: N, NW, W, SW, S, SE, E, and NE.

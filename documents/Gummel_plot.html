Gummel plot

In electronics, the Gummel plot is the combined plot of the base and collector electric currents, I c subscript I c I_{c} and I b subscript I b I_{b} , of a bipolar transistor vs. the base-emitter voltage, V b e subscript V b e V_{be} , on a semi-logarithmic scale. This plot is very useful in device characterization because it reflects on the quality of the emitter-base junction while the base-collector bias, V b c subscript V b c V_{bc} , is kept at a constant.

A number of other device parameters can be garnered either quantitatively or qualitatively directly from the Gummel plot:1

  • the common-emitter current gain, β β \beta , and the common-base current gain, α α \alpha
  • base and collector ideality factors, n n n
  • series resistances and leakage currents.

Sometimes the DC current gain, β β \beta , is plotted on the same figure as well.

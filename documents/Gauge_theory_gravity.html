Gauge theory gravity

'''Gauge theory gravity (GTG)''' is a theory of [[gravitation]] cast in the mathematical language of [[geometric algebra]]. To those familiar with [[general relativity]], it is highly reminiscent of the [[tetrad formalism]] although there are significant conceptual differences. Most notably, the background in GTG is flat, [[Minkowski spacetime]]. The [[equivalence principle]] is not assumed, but instead follows from the fact that the [[gauge covariant derivative]] is [[minimal coupling|minimally coupled]]. GTG was first proposed by Lasenby, Doran, and Gull in 19981 as a fulfillment of partial results presented in 1993.2 The theory has not been widely adopted by the rest of the physics community, who have mostly opted for differential geometry approaches like that of the related gauge gravitation theory.

Mathematical foundation

The foundation of GTG comes from two principles. First, position-gauge invariance demands that arbitrary local displacements of fields not affect the physical content of the field equations. Second, rotation-gauge invariance demands that arbitrary local rotations of fields not affect the physical content of the field equations. Relation to general relativity

For those more familiar with general relativity, it is possible to define a metric tensor from the position-gauge field in a manner similar to tetrads.

   Antiisomorphism      Antiisomorphism   In Category theory , a branch of formal mathematics, an antiisomorphism (or anti-isomorphism ) between structured sets  A and B is an isomorphism from A to the opposite of B (or equivalently from the opposite of A to B ). 1 If there exists an antiisomorphism between two structures, they are antiisomorphic.  Intuitively, to say that two mathematical structures are antiisomorphic is to say that they are basically opposites of one another.  The concept is particularly useful in an algebraic setting, as, for instance, when applied to rings .

   Berlekampâ€“Welch algorithm      Berlekampâ€“Welch algorithm   The Berlekampâ€“Welch algorithm , also known as the Welchâ€“Berlekamp algorithm , is named for Elwyn R. Berlekamp and Lloyd R. Welch . The algorithm efficiently corrects errors in BCH codes and Reedâ€“Solomon codes (which are a subset of BCH codes). Unlike many other decoding algorithms, and in correspondence with the code-domain Berlekampâ€“Massey algorithm that uses syndrome decoding and the dual of the codes, the Berlekampâ€“Welch decoding algorithm provides a method for decoding Reedâ€“Solomon codes using just the generator matrix and not syndromes.  History on decoding Reedâ€“Solomon codes   In 1960, Peterson came up with an algorithm for decoding BCH codes . 1 2 His algorithm solves the important second stage of the generalized BCH decoding procedure and is used to calculate the error locator polynomial coefficients that in turn provide the error locator polynomial. This is crucial to the decoding of BCH codes.  In 1963, Gorensteinâ€“Zierler saw that BCH codes and Reedâ€“Solomon codes have a common generalization and that the decoding algorithm extends to more general situation.  In 1968 / 69, Elwyn Berlekamp invented an algorithm for decoding BCH codes. James Massey recognized its application to linear feedback shift registers and simplified the algorithm. 3 4 Massey termed the algorithm the LFSR Synthesis Algorithm (Berlekamp Iterative Algorithm) but it is now known as the Berlekampâ€“Massey algorithm .  In 1986, The Welchâ€“Berlekamp algorithm was developed to solve the decoding equation of Reedâ€“Solomon codes , using a fast method to solve a certain polynomial equation.

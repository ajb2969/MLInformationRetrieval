   P-adic Hodge theory      P-adic Hodge theory   In mathematics , p -adic Hodge theory is a theory that provides a way to classify and study p -adic Galois representations of characteristic 0  local fields 1 with residual characteristic p (such as Q p ). The theory has its beginnings in Jean-Pierre Serre and John Tate 's study of Tate modules of abelian varieties and the notion of Hodgeâ€“Tate representation . Hodgeâ€“Tate representations are related to certain decompositions of p -adic cohomology theories analogous to the Hodge decomposition , hence the name p -adic Hodge theory.

   Theoretical gravity      Theoretical gravity   In geodesy and geophysics , theoretical gravity is a means to compare the true gravity on the Earth's surface with a physically smoothed model. The most common model of a smoothed Earth is the Earth ellipsoid .  Despite the fact that the exact density layers in the Earth's interior are still unknown, the theoretical gravity  g of its level surface can be computed quite easily by using the International Gravity Formula . This refers to a mean Earth ellipsoid , the parameters of which are set by international convention.

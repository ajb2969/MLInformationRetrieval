It is also known as the logistic normal distribution , 1 which often refers to a multinomial logit version (e.g. 2 3 4 5 ).  A variable might be modeled as logit-normal if it is a proportion, which is bounded by zero and one, and where values of zero and one never occur. It also has the potential to simplify statistical analyses of compositional data by allowing one to answer questions about log-ratios of the components of the data vectors. One is often interested in ratios rather than absolute component values.  The probability simplex is a bounded space, making standard techniques that are typically applied to vectors in    ℝ  n     superscript  ℝ  n    \mathbb{R}^{n}   less meaningful. Aitchison described the problem of spurious negative correlations when applying such methods directly to simplicial vectors. 9 However, mapping compositional data in    𝒮  D     superscript  𝒮  D    \mathcal{S}^{D}   through the inverse of the additive logistic transformation yields real-valued data in    ℝ   D  -  1      superscript  ℝ    D  1     \mathbb{R}^{D-1}   .

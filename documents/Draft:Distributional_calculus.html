Draft:Distributional calculus

In mathematics, distributional calculus is a generalized form of calculus that is based on the notion of distributions (generalized function), as opposed to classical calculus, which is based on functions. Its historical origin is calculus of variation and today it serves a foundation for such fields as the theory of partial differential equations and complex analytic geometry. Distributional calculus can give a rigorous meaning to the formal equations such as:

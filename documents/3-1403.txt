Therefore, coordinate transformations (also called point transformations ) are a type of canonical transformation. However, the class of canonical transformations is much broader, since the old generalized coordinates, momenta and even time may be combined to form the new generalized coordinates and momenta. Canonical transformations that do not include the time explicitly are called restricted canonical transformations (many textbooks consider only this type).  For clarity, we restrict the presentation here to calculus and classical mechanics .

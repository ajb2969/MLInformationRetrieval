   Pentation      Pentation   In mathematics, pentation is the operation of repeated tetration , just as tetration is the operation of repeated exponentiation . 1  History  The word "pentation" was coined by Reuben Goodstein in 1947 from the roots penta- (five) and iteration . It is part of his general naming scheme for hyperoperations . 2  Notation  Pentation can be written as a hyperoperation as    a   [  5  ]   b      a   delimited-[]  5   b    a[5]b   , or using Knuth's up-arrow notation as    a  ↑  ↑  ↑  b     fragments  a  normal-↑  normal-↑  normal-↑  b    a\uparrow\uparrow\uparrow b   or    a   ↑  3   b      superscript  normal-↑  3   a  b    a\uparrow^{3}b   .

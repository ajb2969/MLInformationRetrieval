Liftings are used to produce disintegrations of measures , for instance conditional probability distributions given continuous random variables, and fibrations of Lebesgue measure on the level sets of a function.  Existence of liftings   Theorem. Suppose ( X , Œ£, Œº) is complete. 5 Then ( X , Œ£, Œº) admits a lifting if and only if there exists a collection of mutually disjoint integrable sets in Œ£ whose union is X .  In particular, if ( X , Œ£, Œº) is the completion of a œÉ-finite 6 measure or of an inner regular Borel measure on a locally compact space, then ( X , Œ£, Œº) admits a lifting.   The proof consists in extending a lifting to ever larger sub-œÉ-algebras, applying Doob's martingale convergence theorem if one encounters a countable chain in the process.

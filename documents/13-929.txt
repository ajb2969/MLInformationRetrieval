   Blanuša snarks      Blanuša snarks   In the mathematical field of graph theory , the Blanuša snarks are two 3- regular graphs with 18 vertices and 27 edges. 1 They were discovered by Croatian  mathematician  Danilo Blanuša in 1946 and are named after him. 2 When discovered, only one snark was known—the Petersen graph .  As snarks , the Blanuša snarks are connected, bridgeless cubic graphs with chromatic index equal to 4. Both of them have chromatic number 3, diameter 4 and girth 5. They are non-hamiltonian but are hypohamiltonian . 3  Algebraic properties  The automorphism group of the first Blanuša snark is of order 8 and is isomorphic to the Dihedral group  D 4 , the group of symmetries of a square.  The automorphism group of the second Blanuša snark is an abelian group of order 4 isomorphic to the Klein four-group , the direct product of the Cyclic group  Z /2 Z with itself.

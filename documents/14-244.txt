   Structural acoustics      Structural acoustics   Structural acoustics is the study of the mechanical waves in structures and how they interact with and radiate into adjacent media. The field of structural acoustics is often referred to as vibroacoustics in Europe and Asia. People that work in the field of structural acoustics are known as structural acousticians. The field of structural acoustics can be closely related to a number of other fields of acoustics including noise , transduction , underwater acoustics , and physical acoustics .  Vibrations in structures 1  Compressional and shear waves (isotropic, homogeneous material)  Compressional waves (often referred to as longitudinal waves ) expand and contract in the same direction (or opposite) as the wave motion. Bending waves in beams and plates  Most sound radiation is caused by bending (or flexural) waves, which deform the structure transversely as they propagate. Bending waves are more complicated than compressional or shear waves and depend on material properties as well as geometric properties. They are also dispersive since different frequencies travel as different speeds.  Modeling vibrations  Finite element analysis can be used to predict the vibration of complex structures. A finite element computer program will assemble the mass, stiffness, and damping matrices based on the element geometries and material properties, and solve for the vibration response based on the loads applied. This causes some of the energy from the structure to escape into the fluid, some of which radiates away as sound, some of which stays near the structure and does not radiate away. Structural wave radiation  Since most structures do not vibrate uniformly (as in the case of the baffled piston), but vibrate according as combinations of flexural, compressional and shear waves. The radiation characteristics actually depend strongly on whether the bending wave speed is slower than the sound speed in the fluid or faster. For subsonic bending waves, the radiation is weak, while supersonic bending waves radiate efficiently.

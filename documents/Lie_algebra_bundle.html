Lie algebra bundle

In Mathematics, a weak Lie algebra bundle

ξ = ( ξ , p , X , θ ) ξ ξ p X θ \xi=(\xi,p,X,\theta)\,

is a vector bundle ξ ξ \xi\, over a base space X together with a morphism

θ : ξ ⊗ ξ → ξ normal-: θ normal-→ tensor-product ξ ξ ξ \theta:\xi\otimes\xi\rightarrow\xi

which induces a Lie algebra structure on each fibre ξ x subscript ξ x \xi_{x}\, .

A Lie algebra bundle ξ = ( ξ , p , X ) ξ ξ p X \xi=(\xi,p,X)\, is a vector bundle in which each fibre is a Lie algebra and for every x in X, there is an open set U U U containing x, a Lie algebra L and a homeomorphism

ϕ : U × L → p - 1 ( U ) normal-: ϕ normal-→ U L superscript p 1 U \phi:U\times L\to p^{-1}(U)\,

such that

ϕ x : x × L → p - 1 ( x ) normal-: subscript ϕ x normal-→ x L superscript p 1 x \phi_{x}:x\times L\rightarrow p^{-1}(x)\,

is a Lie algebra isomorphism.

Any Lie algebra bundle is a weak Lie algebra bundle but the converse need not be true in general.

As an example of a weak Lie algebra bundle that is not a strong Lie algebra bundle, consider the total space 𝔰 𝔬 ( 3 ) × ℝ 𝔰 𝔬 3 ℝ \mathfrak{so}(3)\times\mathbb{R} over the real line ℝ ℝ \mathbb{R} . Let [.,.] denote the Lie bracket of 𝔰 𝔬 ( 3 ) 𝔰 𝔬 3 \mathfrak{so}(3) and deform it by the real parameter as:

[ X , Y ] x = x ⋅ [ X , Y ] subscript X Y x normal-⋅ x X Y [X,Y]_{x}=x\cdot[X,Y] for X , Y ∈ 𝔰 𝔬 ( 3 ) X Y 𝔰 𝔬 3 X,Y\in\mathfrak{so}(3) and x ∈ ℝ x ℝ x\in\mathbb{R} .

Lie's third theorem states that every bundle of Lie algebras can locally be integrated to a bundle of Lie groups. However globally the total space might fail to be Hausdorff.1

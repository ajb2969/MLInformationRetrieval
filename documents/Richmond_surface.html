Richmond surface

In differential geometry, a Richmond surface is a minimal surface first described by Herbert William Richmond in 1904. 1 It is a family of surfaces with one planar end and one Enneper surface-like self-intersecting end.

It has Weierstrass–Enneper parameterization f ( z ) = 1 / z m , g ( z ) = z m formulae-sequence f z 1 superscript z m g z superscript z m f(z)=1/z^{m},g(z)=z^{m} . This allows a parametrization based on a complex parameter as

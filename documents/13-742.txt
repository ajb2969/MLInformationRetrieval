   Lectionary 184      Lectionary 184   | type = | cat = | hand = | note = }}  Lectionary 184 , designated by siglum  ℓ  184 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering) is a Greek manuscript of the New Testament , on parchment. Westcott and Hort labelled it by 39 e , 1  Scrivener by 259 e . 2 It is dated by a colophon to the year 1319. 3  Description  The codex contains Lessons from the Gospels of John , Matthew , Luke  lectionary ( Evangelistarium ). 4 Part of the first leaf (John 1:11-13) is on paper and was supplied by later hand. 5 It is written in Greek minuscule letters, on 248 parchment leaves ({{×|30|22.5|cm}}), in two columns per page, 27-28 lines per page. 6 According to Gregory it has "good text". 7  It is remarkable for its departures from the received text, and for that reason was often cited by Tischendorf , Alford , 8 and Aland on the Gospels. It contains text of Luke 22:43-44 but with doubtful mark.  In Matt 23:38 word  omitted like in Codex Vaticanus , Codex Regius , ff 2 , syr c , cop sa, bo mss . 9  In Luke 14:5 it reads υἱὸς ὑμῶν (as mss. 1344 , ℓ  1579 ) for υἱὸς ἢ βοῦς (    𝔓   𝔓   \mathfrak{P}    45 ,    𝔓   𝔓   \mathfrak{P}    75 , B, W, Δ , 28 , 565, 700); 10  In John 6:1 it reads της θαλασσης της Τιβεριαδος for της θαλασσης της Γαλιλαιας της Τιβεριαδος, the reading of the codex is supported by 0210 , 1242 , 1344, 2174; 11  History  The manuscript once belonged to Charles Burney , along with codices 480 , 481 , 482 , 484 , and 485 . 12  It was examined by Scrivener and Gregory . Scrivener gave its collation in 1852 (along with the codex ℓ  183 ).  The manuscript is cited in the critical editions of the Greek New Testament (UBS3, 13 UBS4 14 ).  Currently the codex is located in the British Library (Burney 22) at London . 15  See also   List of New Testament lectionaries  Biblical manuscript  Textual criticism   Notes and references  Bibliography    Westcott in Smith's Dictionary of the Bible .

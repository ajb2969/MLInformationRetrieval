Papyrus 108

Papyrus 108 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by ğ”“ ğ”“ \mathfrak{P} 108, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of John, containing verses 17:23-24 & 18:1-5 in a fragmentary condition. The manuscript has been paleographically assigned to the late 2nd or early 3rd Century CE.1 The manuscript is currently housed at the Papyrology Rooms (P.

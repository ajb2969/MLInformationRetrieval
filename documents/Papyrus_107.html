Papyrus 107

Papyrus 107 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 107, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of John, containing verses 17:1-2 & 17:11 in a fragmentary condition. The manuscript has been paleographically assigned to the early 3rd Century CE.1 The manuscript currently is housed at the Sackler Library (Papyrology Rooms, P. Oxy. 4446) at Oxford.2

Description

The original manuscript would've had around 33 lines per page. The extant portion is too small to determine height and width. The handwriting script is either documentary or common. The text is erratic, and doesn't really agree with any major text-type, bearing most resemblance with Codex Washingtonianus (W).3

   Laplace principle (large deviations theory)      Laplace principle (large deviations theory)   In mathematics , Laplace's principle is a basic theorem in large deviations theory , similar to Varadhan's lemma . It gives an asymptotic expression for the Lebesgue integral of exp(− θφ ( x )) over a fixed set A as θ becomes large. Such expressions can be used, for example, in statistical mechanics to determining the limiting behaviour of a system as the temperature tends to absolute zero .

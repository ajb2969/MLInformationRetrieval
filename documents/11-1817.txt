   Gabor–Wigner transform      Gabor–Wigner transform   The Gabor transform , named after Dennis Gabor , and the Wigner distribution function, named after Eugene Wigner , are both tools for time-frequency analysis . Since the Gabor transform does not have high clarity, and the Wigner distribution function has a "cross term problem" (i.e. is non-linear), a 2007 study by S. C. Pei and J. J. Ding proposed a new combination of the two transforms that has high clarity and no cross term problem. [2 ] Since the cross term does not appear in the Gabor transform, the time frequency distribution of the Gabor transform can be used as a filter to filter out the cross term in the output of the Wigner distribution function.

   Fermat quotient      Fermat quotient   In number theory , the Fermat quotient of an integer a with respect to an odd prime  p is defined as: 1 2 3 4         q  p    (  a  )    =     a   p  -  1    -  1   p    .         subscript  q  p   a        superscript  a    p  1    1   p     q_{p}(a)=\frac{a^{p-1}-1}{p}.     or        δ  p    (  a  )    =    a  -   a  p    p          subscript  δ  p   a       a   superscript  a  p    p     \delta_{p}(a)=\frac{a-a^{p}}{p}   .  This article is about the former. For the latter see p-derivation .  If the base a is coprime to the exponent p then Fermat's little theorem says that q p ( a ) will be an integer. The quotient is named after Pierre de Fermat . In general they are called Wieferich primes base a.

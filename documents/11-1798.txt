Transdiode configuration  A necessary condition for successful operation of a log amplifier is that the input voltage, V in , is always positive. This may be ensured by using a rectifier and filter to condition the input signal before applying it to the log amp input. As V in is positive, V out is obliged to be negative (since the op amp is in the inverting configuration) and is large enough to forward bias the emitter-base junction of the BJT keeping it in the active mode of operation.

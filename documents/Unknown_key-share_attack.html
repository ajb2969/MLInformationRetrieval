Unknown key-share attack

As defined by , an unknown key-share (UKS) attack on an authenticated key agreement (AK) or authenticated key agreement with key confirmation (AKC) protocol is an attack whereby an entity A A A ends up believing she shares a key with B B B , and although this is in fact the case, B B B mistakenly believes the key is instead shared with an entity E ≠ A E A E\neq A .

Papyrus 68

Papyrus 68 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by 
 
 
68, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the First Epistle to the Corinthians. The surviving texts of 1 Corinthians are verses 4:12-17; 4:19-5:3. The manuscript palaeographically has been assigned to the 7th century.1
Text
The Greek text of this codex is mixed. Aland placed it in Category III.2
Location
It is currently housed at the Russian National Library (Gr. 258B) in Saint Petersburg.34

An estimate is suitable if replacing it with the unknown parameter does not cause major damage in next computations. In algorithmic inference , suitability of an estimate reads in terms of compatibility with the observed sample.  In turn, parameter compatibility is a probability measure that we derive from the probability distribution of the random variable to which the parameter refers. In this way we identify a random parameter Œò compatible with an observed sample.

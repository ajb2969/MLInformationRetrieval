   Variance gamma process      Variance gamma process   In the theory of stochastic processes , a part of the mathematical theory of probability , the variance gamma process (VG) , also known as Laplace motion , is a L√©vy process determined by a random time change. The process has finite moments distinguishing it from many L√©vy processes. There is no diffusion component in the VG process and it is thus a pure jump process . The increments are independent and follow a Variance-gamma distribution , which is a generalization of the Laplace distribution .  There are several representations of the VG process that relate it to other processes. As such the variance gamma model allows to consistently price options with different strikes and maturities using a single set of parameters. Madan and Seneta present a symmetric version of the variance gamma process. 4 Madan, Carr and Chang 5 extend the model to allow for an asymmetric form and present a formula to price European options under the variance gamma process.  Hirsa and Madan show how to price American options under variance gamma. 6 Fiorani presents numerical solutions for European and American barrier options under variance gamma process. 7 He also provides computer programming code to price vanilla and barrier European and American barrier options under variance gamma process.  Lemmens et al. 8 construct bounds for arithmetic Asian options for several L√©vy models including the variance gamma model.  Applications to Credit Risk Modeling  The variance gamma process has been successfully applied in the modeling of credit risk in structural models. The pure jump nature of the process and the possibility to control skewness and kurtosis of the distribution allow the model to price correctly the risk of default of securities having a short maturity, something that is generally not possible with structural models in which the underlying assets follow a Brownian motion. Fiorani, Luciano and Semeraro 9 model credit default swaps under variance gamma. In an extensive empirical test they show the overperformance of the pricing under variance gamma, compared to alternative models presented in literature.

   Ideal norm      Ideal norm   In commutative algebra , the norm of an ideal is a generalization of a norm of an element in the field extension. It is particularly important in number theory since it measures the size of an ideal of a complicated number ring in terms of an ideal in a less complicated ring. When the less complicated number ring is taken to be the ring of integers , Z , then the norm of a nonzero ideal I of a number ring R is simply the size of the finite quotient ring  R / I .

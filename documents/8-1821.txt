The values of elements diminish toward zero away from the diagonal, where all elements have value 1.  Interestingly, the inverse of a Lehmer matrix is a tridiagonal matrix , where the superdiagonal and subdiagonal have strictly negative entries. Consider again the n×n  A and m×m  B Lehmer matrices, where m > n . A rather peculiar property of their inverses is that A −1 is nearly a submatrix of B −1 , except for the A n,n element, which is not equal to B m,m .  A Lehmer matrix of order n has trace  n .

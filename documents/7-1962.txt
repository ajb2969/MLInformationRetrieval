Applications  The classic applications of bipolar coordinates are in solving partial differential equations , e.g., Laplace's equation or the Helmholtz equation , for which bipolar coordinates allow a separation of variables (in 2D). A typical example would be the electric field surrounding two parallel cylindrical conductors.

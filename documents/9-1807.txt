   Harmonic measure      Harmonic measure  In [[mathematics]], especially [[potential theory]], '''harmonic measure''' is a concept related to the theory of [[harmonic function]]s that arise s from the solution of the classical Dirichlet problem .  In probability theory , harmonic measure of a subset of the boundary of a bounded domain in Euclidean space     R  n     superscript  R  n    R^{n}   ,    n  â‰¥  2      n  2    n\geq 2   is the probability that a Brownian motion started inside a domain hits that subset of the boundary. More generally, harmonic measure of an ItÅ diffusion  X describes the distribution of X as it hits the boundary of D . In the complex plane , harmonic measure can be used to estimate the modulus of an analytic function inside a domain D given bounds on the modulus on the boundary of the domain; a special case of this principle is Hadamard's three-circle theorem . On simply connected planar domains, there is a close connection between harmonic measure and the theory of conformal maps .

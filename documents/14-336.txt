   Concomitant (statistics)      Concomitant (statistics)   In statistics , the concept of a concomitant , also called the induced order statistic , arises when one sorts the members of a random sample according to corresponding values of another random sample.  Let ( X i , Y i ), i = 1, . . ., n be a random sample from a bivariate distribution. If the sample is ordered by the X i , then the Y -variate associated with X r : n will be denoted by Y [ r : n ] and termed the concomitant of the r th  order statistic .

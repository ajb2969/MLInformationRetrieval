Circuit minimization for Boolean functions

In Boolean algebra, circuit minimization is the problem of obtaining the smallest logic circuit (Boolean formula) that represents a given Boolean function or truth table. The unbounded circuit minimization problem was long-conjectured to be Σ 2 P superscript subscript normal-Σ 2 P \Sigma_{2}^{P} -complete, a result finally proved in 2008,1 but there are effective heuristics such as Karnaugh maps and the Quine–McCluskey algorithm that facilitate the process.

Purpose

The problem with having a complicated circuit (i.e. one with many elements, such as logical gates) is that each element takes up physical space in its implementation and costs time and money to produce in itself. Circuit minimization may be one form of logic optimization used to reduce the area of complex logic in integrated circuits.

Plus construction

In mathematics, the plus construction is a method for simplifying the fundamental group of a space without changing its homology and cohomology groups. It was introduced by , and was used by Daniel Quillen to define algebraic K-theory. This operation generally changes the homology of the space, but these changes can be reversed by the addition of three-cells.

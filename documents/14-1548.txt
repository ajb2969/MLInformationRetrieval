   Conjugate depth      Conjugate depth   In fluid dynamics , the conjugate depth s refer to the depth ( y 1 ) upstream and the depth ( y 2 ) downstream of the hydraulic jump whose momentum fluxes are equal for a given discharge (volume flux) q . The depth upstream of a hydraulic jump is always supercritical . It is important to note that the conjugate depth is different from the alternate depths for flow which are used in energy conservation calculations.

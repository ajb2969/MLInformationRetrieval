   Exceptional isomorphism      Exceptional isomorphism   In mathematics , an exceptional isomorphism , also called an accidental isomorphism , is an isomorphism between members a i and b j of two families (usually infinite) of mathematical objects, that is not an example of a pattern of such isomorphisms. 1 These coincidences are at times considered a matter of trivia, 2 but in other respects they can give rise to other phenomena, notably exceptional objects . 3 In the below, coincidences are listed in all places they occur.

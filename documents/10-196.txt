   Kuratowski convergence      Kuratowski convergence   In mathematics , Kuratowski convergence is a notion of convergence for sequences (or, more generally, nets ) of compact subsets of metric spaces , named after the Polish  mathematician  Kazimierz Kuratowski . Intuitively, the Kuratowski limit of a sequence of sets is where the sets " accumulate ". Related Concepts  For metric spaces X we have the following:   Kuratowski convergence coincides with convergence in Fell topology .  Kuratowski convergence is weaker than convergence in Vietoris topology .  Kuratowski convergence is weaker than convergence in Hausdorff metric .  For compact metric spaces X , Kuratowski convergence coincides with both convergence in Hausdorff metric and Vietoris topology.

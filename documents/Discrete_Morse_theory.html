Discrete Morse theory

'''Discrete Morse theory''' is a [[comb

inatorial]] adaptation of Morse theory developed by Robin Forman. The theory has various practical applications in diverse fields of applied mathematics and computer science, such as configuration spaces,1 homology computation,2 denoising,3 and mesh compression.4

Discrete Morse functions

A real-valued function μ : 𝒳 → ℝ normal-: μ normal-→ 𝒳 ℝ \mu:\mathcal{X}\to\mathbb{R} is a discrete Morse function if it satisfies the following two properties:

  1. For any cell σ ∈ 𝒳 σ 𝒳 \sigma\in\mathcal{X} , the number of cells τ ∈ 𝒳 τ 𝒳 \tau\in\mathcal{X} in the boundary of σ σ \sigma which satisfy μ ( σ ) ≤ μ ( τ ) μ σ μ τ \mu(\sigma)\leq\mu(\tau) is at most one.
  2. For any cell σ ∈ 𝒳 σ 𝒳 \sigma\in\mathcal{X} , the number of cells τ ∈ 𝒳 τ 𝒳 \tau\in\mathcal{X} containing σ σ \sigma in their boundary which satisfy μ ( σ ) ≥ μ ( τ ) μ σ μ τ \mu(\sigma)\geq\mu(\tau) is at most one.

It can be shown6 that the cardinalities in the two conditions cannot both be one simultaneously for a fixed cell σ σ \sigma , provided that 𝒳 𝒳 \mathcal{X} is a regular CW complex. In this case, each cell σ ∈ 𝒳 σ 𝒳 \sigma\in\mathcal{X} can be paired with at most one exceptional cell τ ∈ 𝒳 τ 𝒳 \tau\in\mathcal{X} : either a boundary cell with larger μ μ \mu value, or a co-boundary cell with smaller μ μ \mu value. The cells which have no pairs, i.e., their function values are strictly higher than their boundary cells and strictly lower than their co-boundary cells are called critical cells. Thus, a discrete Morse function partitions the CW complex into three distinct cell collections 𝒳 = 𝒜 ⊔ 𝒦 ⊔ 𝒬 𝒳 square-union 𝒜 𝒦 𝒬 \mathcal{X}=\mathcal{A}\sqcup\mathcal{K}\sqcup\mathcal{Q} , where:

  1. 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} denotes the critical cells which are unpaired,
  2. 𝒦 𝒦 \mathcal{K} denotes cells which are paired with boundary cells, and
  3. 𝒬 𝒬 \mathcal{Q} denotes cells which are paired with co-boundary cells.

By construction, there is a bijection of sets between k k k -dimensional cells in 𝒦 𝒦 \mathcal{K} and the ( k - 1 ) k 1 (k-1) -dimensional cells in 𝒬 𝒬 \mathcal{Q} , which can be denoted by p k : 𝒦 k → 𝒬 k - 1 normal-: superscript p k normal-→ superscript 𝒦 k superscript 𝒬 k 1 p^{k}:\mathcal{K}^{k}\to\mathcal{Q}^{k-1} for each natural number k k k . For instance, over the integers ℤ ℤ \mathbb{Z} , the only allowed values are ± 1 plus-or-minus 1 \pm 1 . The fundamental result of discrete Morse theory establishes that the CW complex 𝒳 𝒳 \mathcal{X} is isomorphic on the level of homology to a new complex 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} consisting of only the critical cells.

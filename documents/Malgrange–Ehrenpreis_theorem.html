The original proofs of Malgrange and Ehrenpreis were non-constructive as they used the Hahn‚ÄìBanach theorem. Since then several constructive proofs have been found.

By taking Fourier transforms the Malgrange‚ÄìEhrenpreis theorem is equivalent to the fact that every non-zero polynomial P has a distributional inverse. By replacing P by the product with its complex conjugate, one can also assume that P is non-negative.

   Natural Earth projection      Natural Earth projection   The Natural Earth projection is a pseudocylindrical  map projection designed by Tom Patterson . It is neither conformal nor equal-area .  Definition  The Natural Earth is defined by the following formulas:      x  =     0.8707  ×  l    (  φ  )    ×  λ       x        0.8707  l   φ   λ     x=0.8707\times l(\varphi)\times\lambda   ,      y  =     0.8707  ×  0.52  ×  d    (  φ  )    ×  π       y        0.8707  0.52  d   φ   π     y=0.8707\times 0.52\times d(\varphi)\times\pi   ,  where      x   x   x   ,   y   y   y   are the Cartesian coordinates;     λ   λ   \lambda   is the longitude from the central meridian;     φ   φ   \varphi   is the latitude;      l   (  φ  )       l  φ    l(\varphi)   is the length of the parallel at latitude   φ   φ   \varphi   ;      d   (  φ  )       d  φ    d(\varphi)   is the distance of the parallel from the equator at latitude   φ   φ   \varphi   .       l   (  φ  )       l  φ    l(\varphi)   and    d   (  φ  )       d  φ    d(\varphi)   are given in this table:         φ   φ   \varphi   (degrees)       l   (  φ  )       l  φ    l(\varphi)          d   (  φ  )       d  φ    d(\varphi)          0   1   0     5   0.9988   0.062     10   0.9953   0.124     15   0.9894   0.186     20   0.9811   0.248     25   0.9703   0.31     30   0.957   0.372     35   0.9409   0.434     40   0.9222   0.4958     45   0.9006   0.5571     50   0.8763   0.6176     55   0.8492   0.6769     60   0.8196   0.7346     65   0.7874   0.7903     70   0.7525   0.8435     75   0.716   0.8936     80   0.6754   0.9394     85   0.627   0.9761     90   0.563   1     The values of the above functions for other latitudes are calculated by interpolation . The values for the southern hemisphere are calculated by changing the sign of the corresponding values for the northern hemisphere.

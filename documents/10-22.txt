   Toroidal and poloidal      Toroidal and poloidal   The earliest use of these terms cited by the Oxford English Dictionary (OED) is by Walter M. Elsasser (1946) in the context of the generation of the Earth's magnetic field by currents in the core, with "toroidal" being parallel to lines of latitude and "poloidal" being in the direction of the magnetic field (i.e. towards the poles).  The OED also records the later usage of these terms in the context of toroidally confined plasmas, as encountered in magnetic confinement fusion .

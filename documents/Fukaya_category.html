Fukaya category

In symplectic topology, a discipline within mathematics, a Fukaya category of a symplectic manifold ( M , ω ) M ω (M,\omega) is a category ℱ ( M ) ℱ M \mathcal{F}(M) whose objects are Lagrangian submanifolds of M M M , and morphisms are Floer chain groups Hom ( L 0 , L 1 ) = F C ( L 0 , L 1 ) Hom subscript L 0 subscript L 1 F C subscript L 0 subscript L 1 \mathrm{Hom}(L_{0},L_{1})=FC(L_{0},L_{1}) . Its finer structure can be described in the language of quasi categories as an A∞-category.

They are named after Kenji Fukaya who introduced the A ∞ subscript A A_{\infty} language first in the context of Morse homology, and exist in a number of variants. As Fukaya categories are A∞-categories, they have associated derived categories, which are the subject of a celebrated conjecture of Maxim Kontsevich: the homological mirror symmetry. This conjecture has been verified by computations for a variety of comparatively simple examples.

   Concentration inequality      Concentration inequality   In mathematics , concentration inequalities provide probability bounds on how a random variable deviates from some value (e.g., its expectation ). The laws of large numbers of classical probability theory state that sums of independent random variables are, under very mild conditions, close to their expectation with a large probability. Such sums are the most basic examples of random variables concentrated around their mean . Recent results shows that such behavior is shared by other functions of independent random variables.

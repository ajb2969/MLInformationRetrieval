   Vaidya metric      Vaidya metric   In general relativity , the Vaidya metric describes the non-empty external spacetime of a spherically symmetric and nonrotating star which is either emitting or absorbing null dusts . It is named after the Indian physicist Prahalad Chunnilal Vaidya and constitutes a simplest non-static generalization of the non-radiative Schwarzschild solution to Einstein's field equation , and therefore is also called the "radiating(/shining) Schwarzschild metric".

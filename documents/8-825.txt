   Tunnell's theorem      Tunnell's theorem   In number theory , Tunnell's theorem gives a partial resolution to the congruent number problem , and under the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture , a full resolution.  Congruent number problem  The congruent number problem asks which positive integers can be the area of a right triangle with all three sides rational. Tunnell's theorem relates this to the number of integral solutions of a few fairly simple Diophantine equations . History  The theorem is named for Jerrold B. Tunnell , a number theorist at Rutgers University , who proved it in 1983.  Importance  The importance of Tunnell's theorem is that the criterion it gives is testable by a finite calculation.

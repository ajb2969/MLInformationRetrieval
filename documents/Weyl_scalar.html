Physical interpretation

Szekeres (1965)8 gave an interpretation of the different Weyl scalars at large distances:

Ψ 2 subscript normal-Ψ 2 \Psi_{2} is a "Coulomb" term, representing the gravitational monopole of the source;

Ψ 1 subscript normal-Ψ 1 \Psi_{1} & Ψ 3 subscript normal-Ψ 3 \Psi_{3} are ingoing and outgoing "longitudinal" radiation terms;

Ψ 0 subscript normal-Ψ 0 \Psi_{0} & Ψ 4 subscript normal-Ψ 4 \Psi_{4} are ingoing and outgoing "transverse" radiation terms.

For a general asymptotically flat spacetime containing radiation (Petrov Type I), Ψ 1 subscript normal-Ψ 1 \Psi_{1} & Ψ 3 subscript normal-Ψ 3 \Psi_{3} can be transformed to zero by an appropriate choice of null tetrad. Thus these can be viewed as gauge quantities.

A particularly important case is the Weyl scalar Ψ 4 subscript normal-Ψ 4 \Psi_{4} . It can be shown to describe outgoing gravitational radiation (in an asymptotically flat spacetime) as

Ψ 4 = 1 2 ( h ¨ θ ^ θ ^ - h ¨ ϕ ^ ϕ ^ ) + i h ¨ θ ^ ϕ ^ = - h ¨ + + i h ¨ × . subscript normal-Ψ 4 1 2 subscript normal-¨ h normal-^ θ normal-^ θ subscript normal-¨ h normal-^ ϕ normal-^ ϕ i subscript normal-¨ h normal-^ θ normal-^ ϕ subscript normal-¨ h i subscript normal-¨ h \Psi_{4}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\ddot{h}_{\hat{\theta}\hat{\theta}}-\ddot{h}_{\hat{% \phi}\hat{\phi}}\right)+i\ddot{h}_{\hat{\theta}\hat{\phi}}=-\ddot{h}_{+}+i% \ddot{h}_{\times}\ . Here, h + subscript h h_{+} and h × subscript h h_{\times} are the "plus" and "cross" polarizations of gravitational radiation, and the double dots represent double time-differentiation.

There are, however, certain examples in which the interpretation listed above fails.9 These are exact vacuum solutions of the Einstein field equations with cylindrical symmetry. For instance, a static (infinitely long) cylinder can produce a gravitational field which has not only the expected "Coulomb"-like Weyl component Ψ 2 subscript normal-Ψ 2 \Psi_{2} , but also non-vanishing "transverse wave"-components Ψ 0 subscript normal-Ψ 0 \Psi_{0} and Ψ 4 subscript normal-Ψ 4 \Psi_{4} . Furthermore, purely outgoing Einstein-Rosen waves have a non-zero "incoming transverse wave"-component Ψ 0 subscript normal-Ψ 0 \Psi_{0} .

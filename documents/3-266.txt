Farey conjectured, without offering proof, that each new term in a Farey sequence expansion is the mediant of its neighbours. Farey's letter was read by Cauchy , who provided a proof in his Exercices de mathÃ©matique , and attributed this result to Farey. In fact, another mathematician, Charles Haros , had published similar results in 1802 which were not known either to Farey or to Cauchy. 3 Thus it was a historical accident that linked Farey's name with these sequences. This is an example of Stigler's law of eponymy .

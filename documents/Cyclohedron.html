Cyclohedron

In geometry, the cyclohedron or Bott–Taubes polytope is a certain (n − 1)-dimensional polytope that is useful in studying knot invariants.

The configuration space of n distinct points on the circle S1 is an n-dimensional manifold, which can be compactified into a manifold with corners by allowing the points to approach each other. This compactification can be factored as S 1 × W n superscript S 1 subscript W n S^{1}\times W_{n} , where Wn is the cyclohedron.

Geometric measure of entanglement

The geometric measure of entanglement 1 is a means to quantify the entanglement in a multi-partite system.

For a system consisting of N N N subsystems, the full Hilbert space ℋ ℋ \mathcal{H} is a tensor product of those of the subsystems, i.e., ℋ = ℋ 1 ⊗ ℋ 2 … ⊗ ℋ N ℋ tensor-product tensor-product subscript ℋ 1 subscript ℋ 2 normal-… subscript ℋ N \mathcal{H}=\mathcal{H}_{1}\otimes\mathcal{H}_{2}\ldots\otimes\mathcal{H}_{N} . This approach works for distinguishable particles or the spin systems. For identical or indistinguishable fermions or bosons, the full Hilbert space is not the tensor product of those of each individual particle. Therefore, a simple modification is necessary. For example, for identical fermions, since the full wave function ψ ψ \psi is now completely anti-symmetric, so is required for ϕ ϕ \phi . This means, the ϕ ϕ \phi taken to approximate ψ ψ \psi should be a Slater determinant wave function. 2

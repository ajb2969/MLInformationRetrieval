   Extender (set theory)      Extender (set theory)   In set theory , an extender is a system of ultrafilters which represents an elementary embedding witnessing large cardinal properties. A nonprincipal ultrafilter is the most basic case of an extender.  A (κ, λ)-extender can be defined as an elementary embedding of some model M of ZFC − (ZFC minus the power set axiom ) having critical point κ ε M , and which maps κ to an ordinal at least equal to λ. It can also be defined as a collection of ultrafilters, one for each n - tuple drawn from λ.  Formal definition of an extender  Let κ and λ be cardinals with κ≤λ.

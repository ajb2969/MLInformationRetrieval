Identric mean

The identric mean of two positive real numbers x, y is defined as:1

I ( x , y ) I x y \displaystyle I(x,y)

It can be derived from the mean value theorem by considering the secant of the graph of the function x ↦ x ⋅ ln x maps-to x normal-⋅ x x x\mapsto x\cdot\ln x . It can be generalized to more variables according by the mean value theorem for divided differences. The identric mean is a special case of the Stolarsky mean.

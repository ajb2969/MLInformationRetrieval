Blade solidity

Blade solidity is an important design parameter for the axial flow impeller and is defined as the ratio of blade chord length to pitch.

  • Blade Solidity = c/s

Where

  • s = 2 π r m / Z b s 2 π subscript r m subscript Z b s=2\pi r_{m}/Z_{b}
  • r m subscript r m r_{m} is the mean radius
  • Z b subscript Z b Z_{b} is blade number
  • Cord length c is the length of the chord line
  • s is pitch

In case of an axial flow impeller mean radius is defined in terms of hub ( r h subscript r h r_{h} ,inner radius) and tip radius ( r t subscript r t r_{t} ,outer radius) as :

  • r m = [ ( r t 2 + r h 2 ) / 2 ] 0.5 subscript r m superscript delimited-[] superscript subscript r t 2 superscript subscript r h 2 2 0.5 r_{m}=[(r_{t}^{2}+r_{h}^{2})/2]^{0.5}

Blade solidity affects various turbomachinery parameters. So to vary those parameters one needs to vary blade solidity but there are some limitations imposed by Aspect ratio (wing) (span/chord), pitch. If an impeller has few blades i.e high pitch it will result in less lift force and in a similar manner for more blades i.e. very low pitch, there will be high drag force.

Blade solidity should not be confused with rotor solidity, which is the ratio of the total area of the rotor blades to the swept area of the rotor.

Preliminary design procedure

Design of the impeller depends on specific speed, hub-tip ratio and solidity ratio. To illustrate the dependence, an expression for axial flow pump and fan is shown

c s = 10 ( D h / D t ) ( N s / 1000 ) 1.5 c s 10 subscript D h subscript D t superscript subscript N s 1000 1.5 \frac{c}{s}=\frac{10}{(D_{h}/D_{t})(N_{s}/1000)^{1.5}}

where

  • D h D t subscript D h subscript D t \frac{D_{h}}{D_{t}} is ratio of hub to tip diameter
  • N s subscript N s N_{s} is the specific speed

Cordier diagram can be used to determine specific speed and impeller tip diameter D t subscript D t D_{t} . Accordingly solidity ratio and hub-tip ratio (range 0.3-0.7) can be adjusted.

Solidity ratio generally falls in the range of 0.4-1.1

Zodi

The zodi is a unit of zodiacal dust.1 One zodi is the amount of interplanetary dust in the inner solar system.2 This dust absorbs light from the Sun and re-radiates it as thermal radiation. The luminosity of the zodiacal dust in the Solar System is about 10 - 7 superscript 10 7 10^{-7} relative to the luminosity of the Sun,3 in The Astrophysical Journal, 713, p. 816 and in practice, this is the observable characteristic defining one zodi.

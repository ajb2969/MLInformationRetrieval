Almost integer

In recreational mathematics an almost integer is any number that is very close to an integer. Well-known examples of almost integers are high powers of the golden ratio ϕ = 1 + 5 2 ≈ 1.618 ϕ 1 5 2 1.618 \phi=\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}\approx 1.618\, , for example:

  • ϕ 17 = 3571 + 1597 5 2 ≈ 3571.00028 superscript ϕ 17 3571 1597 5 2 3571.00028 \phi^{17}=\frac{3571+1597\sqrt{5}}{2}\approx 3571.00028\,
  • ϕ 18 = 2889 + 1292 5 ≈ 5777.999827 superscript ϕ 18 2889 1292 5 5777.999827 \phi^{18}=2889+1292\sqrt{5}\approx 5777.999827\,
  • ϕ 19 = 9349 + 4181 5 2 ≈ 9349.000107 superscript ϕ 19 9349 4181 5 2 9349.000107 \phi^{19}=\frac{9349+4181\sqrt{5}}{2}\approx 9349.000107\,

The fact that these powers approach integers is non-coincidental, which is trivially seen because the golden ratio is a Pisot-Vijayaraghavan number.

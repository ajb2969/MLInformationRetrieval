Hydraulic retention time

The hydraulic retention time (HRT), also known as hydraulic residence time or t (tau), is a measure of the average length of time that a soluble compound remains in a constructed bioreactor.

Hydraulic retention time is the volume of the aeration tank divided by the influent flowrate:

H R T = V o l u m e o f a e r a t i o n t a n k i n f l u e n t f l o w r a t e H R T V o l u m e o f a e r a t i o n t a n k i n f l u e n t f l o w r a t e HRT=\frac{Volume\;of\;aeration\;tank}{influent\;flowrate}

where using SI Units Volume is in [m3] and Influent flowrate is in [m3/h]. HRT is usually expressed in hours (or sometimes days).

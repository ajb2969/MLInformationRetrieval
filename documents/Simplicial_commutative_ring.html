Simplicial commutative ring

In algebra, a simplicial commutative ring is a commutative monoid in the category of simplicial abelian groups, or, equivalently, a simplicial object in the category of commutative rings. If A is a simplicial commutative ring, then it can be shown that π 0 A subscript π 0 A \pi_{0}A is a commutative ring and π i A subscript π i A \pi_{i}A are modules over that ring (in fact, π * A subscript π A \pi_{*}A is a graded ring over π 0 A subscript π 0 A \pi_{0}A .)

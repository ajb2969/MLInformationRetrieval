The Chebyshev functions, especially the second one ψ ( x ), are often used in proofs related to prime numbers , because it is typically simpler to work with them than with the prime-counting function , π ( x ) (See the exact formula , below.) Both Chebyshev functions are asymptotic to x , a statement equivalent to the prime number theorem .  Both functions are named in honour of Pafnuty Chebyshev . This proves that the primorial x # is asymptotically equal to exp((1+o(1)) x ), where "o" is the little-o notation (see Big O notation ) and together with the prime number theorem establishes the asymptotic behavior of p n #.

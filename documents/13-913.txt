   Averaging argument      Averaging argument   In computational complexity theory and cryptography , averaging argument is a standard argument for proving theorems. It usually allows us to convert probabilistic  polynomial-time algorithms into non-uniform polynomial-size circuits .  Example  To simplify, let's first consider an example.

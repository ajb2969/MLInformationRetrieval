This sequence of inclusions expresses the PrÃ¼fer p -group as the direct limit of its finite subgroups. As there is no maximal subgroup of a PrÃ¼fer p -group, it is its own Frattini subgroup .  Given this list of subgroups, it is clear that the PrÃ¼fer p -groups are indecomposable (cannot be written as a direct sum of proper subgroups). More is true: the PrÃ¼fer p -groups are subdirectly irreducible . An abelian group is subdirectly irreducible if and only if it is isomorphic to a finite cyclic p -group or to a PrÃ¼fer group.  The PrÃ¼fer p -group is the unique infinite p -group which is locally cyclic (every finite set of elements generates a cyclic group). As seen above, all proper subgroups of Z ( p âˆ ) are finite. The PrÃ¼fer p -groups are the only infinite abelian groups with this property. 1  The PrÃ¼fer p -groups are divisible . They play an important role in the classification of divisible groups; along with the rational numbers they are the simplest divisible groups. More precisely: an abelian group is divisible if and only if it is the direct sum of a (possibly infinite) number of copies of Q and (possibly infinite) numbers of copies of Z ( p âˆ ) for every prime p .

Pointed sets may be regarded as a rather simple algebraic structure. In the sense of universal algebra, they are structures with a single nullary operation which picks out the basepoint.10

The class of all pointed sets together with the class of all based maps form a category. In this category the pointed singleton set ( { a } , a ) a a (\{a\},a) is an initial object and a terminal object,11 i.e. a zero object.12 There is a faithful functor from usual sets to pointed sets, but it is not full and these categories are not equivalent.13 In particular, the empty set is not a pointed set, for it has no element that can be chosen as base point.14

The category of pointed sets and pointed maps has both products and co-products, but it is not a distributive category.19

Many algebraic structures are pointed sets in a rather trivial way. For example, groups are pointed sets by choosing the identity element as the basepoint, so that group homomorphisms are point-preserving maps.20 This observation can be restated in category theoretic terms as the existence of a forgetful functor from groups to pointed sets.21

A pointed set may be seen as a pointed space under the discrete topology or as a vector space over the field with one element.22

As "rooted set" the notion naturally appears in the study of antimatroids23 and transportation polytopes.24

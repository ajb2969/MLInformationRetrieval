Wehrl entropy

In quantum information theory, the Wehrl entropy,1 named after A. Wehrl, is a type of quasi-entropy defined for the Husimi Q representation Q Q Q 2 of the phase-space quasiprobability distribution. It is defined as

Such a definition of entropy relies on the fact that the Husimi Q representation remains non-negative definite,3 unlike other representations of quantum quasiprobability distributions in phase space.

The Wehrl entropy, then, may be thought of as a type of heuristic semiclassical approximation to the full quantum von Neumann entropy, since it retains some ħ ħ ħ dependence (through Q) but not all of it.

Like all entropies, it reflects some measure of non-localization,5 as the Gauss transform involved in generating Q Q Q and the sacrifice of the star operators have effectively discarded information. In general, for the same state, the Wehrl entropy exceeds the von Neumann entropy (which vanishes for pure states).

In magnetostatics , the force of attraction or repulsion between two current-carrying wires (see first figure below) is often called AmpÃ¨re's force law . The physical origin of this force is that each wire generates a magnetic field, as defined by the Biotâ€“Savart law , and the other wire experiences a magnetic force as a consequence, as defined by the Lorentz force .

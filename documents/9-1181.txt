History and properties  J 2 is one of the 26 Sporadic groups and is also called Hallâ€“Jankoâ€“Wales group . In 1969 Zvonimir Janko predicted J 2 as one of two new simple groups having 2 1+4 :A 5 as a centralizer of an involution (the other is the Janko group J3 ). It was constructed by  as a rank 3 permutation group on 100 points.  Both the Schur multiplier and the outer automorphism group have order 2.  J 2 is the only one of the 4 Janko groups that is a subquotient of the monster group ; it is thus part of what Robert Griess calls the Happy Family. Since it is also found in the Conway group Co1 , it is therefore part of the second generation of the Happy Family.

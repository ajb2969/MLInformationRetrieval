   Stochastic ordering      Stochastic ordering   In probability theory and statistics , a stochastic order quantifies the concept of one random variable being "bigger" than another. These are usually partial orders , so that one random variable   A   A   A   may be neither stochastically greater than, less than nor equal to another random variable   B   B   B   . Many different orders exist, which have different applications.

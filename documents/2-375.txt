   Constant factor rule in integration      Constant factor rule in integration   The constant factor rule in integration is a dual of the constant factor rule in differentiation , and is a consequence of the linearity of integration . It states that a constant factor within an integrand can be separated from the integrand and instead multiplied by the integral.

History  In 1984, Vaughan Jones introduced a new polynomial invariant of link isotopy types which is called the Jones polynomial . The invariants are related to the traces of irreducible representations of Hecke algebras associated with the symmetric groups . In 1986,  showed that the Kauffman polynomial can also be interpreted as a function   F   F   F   on a certain associative algebra. In 1989,  constructed a two-parameter family of algebras C n ( ‚Ñì , m ) with the Kauffman polynomial K n ( ‚Ñì , m ) as trace after appropriate renormalization.

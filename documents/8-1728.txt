Such an identity only holds when AB = BA (i.e. when the matrices A and B  commute ). In the construction of symmetric Pascal matrices like that above, the sub- and superdiagonal matrices do not commute, so the (perhaps) tempting simplification involving the addition of the matrices cannot be made.  A useful property of the sub- and superdiagonal matrices used in the construction is that both are nilpotent ; that is, when raised to a sufficiently high integer power, they degenerate into the zero matrix . (See shift matrix for further details.) As the n Ã— n generalised shift matrices we are using become zero when raised to power n , when calculating the matrix exponential we need only consider the first n +Â 1 terms of the infinite series to obtain an exact result.

   Methanation      Methanation  '''Methanation''' is a physical-chemical process to generate methane from a mixture of various gases out of biomass fermentation or thermo-chemical gasification . 1 The main components are carbon  monoxide and hydrogen . The main catalysts used for this reaction are ruthenium, cobalt, nickel and iron. 2 The following main process describes the methanation:        CO   +    3    H  2     →    CH  4   +    H  2   O       normal-→    CO    3   subscript  normal-H  2        subscript  CH  4      subscript  normal-H  2   normal-O      \mathrm{\,CO+3\,H_{2}\rightarrow CH_{4}+\,H_{2}O}   This process is used for the generation of biogenous natural gas substitute, which can be fed into the gas grid. Methanation is the reverse reaction of steam methane reforming , which converts methane into synthesis gas .

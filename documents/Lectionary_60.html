History

The manuscript was written by Helias, a priest and monk, "in castro Colonia", for the use of the French monastery of St. Denys.11 It belonged to the monastery Dionysius, then to de Thou, then to Colbert. It was examined by Moutfaucon.12 The manuscript was added to the list of New Testament manuscripts by Scholz.13

It was examined and described by Henri Omont14 and Paulin Martin.15

The manuscript is cited in the critical editions of the Greek New Testament (UBS3),16 it was used for the Editio Critica Maior.17

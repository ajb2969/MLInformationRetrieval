   Modulation space      Modulation space   Modulation spaces 1 are a family of Banach spaces defined by the behavior of the short-time Fourier transform with respect to a test function from the Schwartz space . They were originally proposed by Hans Georg Feichtinger and are recognized to be the right kind of function spaces for time-frequency analysis . Feichtinger's algebra , while originally introduced as a new Segal algebra , 2 is identical to a certain modulation space and has become a widely used space of test functions for time-frequency analysis.  Modulation spaces are defined as follows.

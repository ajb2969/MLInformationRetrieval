Gregory dated it to the 9th or 10th century. Currently it is dated by the INTF to the 9th century. 25  It was called "the queen of the cursives" by J. G. Eichhorn (1752-1827), 26 but now it has several rivals (81, 892, 1175, 1739). 27 The manuscript was examined by many scholars, such as Griesbach , 28 who collated its text in Matthew 1-18. It was also studied by Birch and others. The text of the codex was fully collated by S. P. Tregelles in 1850. Tregelles said that, of all the manuscripts he collated (presumably excluding palimpsests), it was the hardest to read.  It was examined and described by Paulin Martin . 29  C. R. Gregory saw the manuscript twice, in 1884 and in 1885. 30  The manuscript was included to a critical apparatus by Kurt Aland in his 25th edition of Novum Testamentum Graece (1963). 31  The codex is located now at the National Library of France (Cod.

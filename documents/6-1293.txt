In nuclear physics the g-factor of a given system includes the effect of the nucleon spins, their orbital angular momenta, and their couplings. Crucially, the Larmor frequency is independent of the polar angle between the applied magnetic field and the magnetic moment direction. This is what makes it a key concept in fields such as nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) and electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR), since the precession rate does not depend on the spatial orientation of the spins. H. E. Griffiths (UK) and E. K. Zavoiskij (USSR) in 1946.  Larmor precession is important in nuclear magnetic resonance , magnetic resonance imaging , electron paramagnetic resonance and muon spin resonance . It is also important for the alignment of cosmic dust grains, which is a cause of the polarization of starlight .  To calculate the spin of a particle in a magnetic field, one must also take into account Thomas precession .  Precession direction  The spin angular momentum of an electron precesses counter-clockwise about the direction of the magnetic field. An electron has a negative charge, so the direction of magnetic moment is opposite to that of its spin.

In contrast, the usual torus is the Cartesian product of two circles only.  The triple torus is a three-dimensional compact  manifold with no boundary . It can be obtained by "gluing" the three pairs of opposite faces of a cube , where being "glued" can be intuitively understood to mean that when a particle moving by inertia in the interior of the cube reaches a point on a face, it goes through it and appears to come forth from the corresponding point on the opposite face, in the same direction.

   Isentropic expansion waves      Isentropic expansion waves   Isentropic expansion waves are created when a supersonic flow is redirected along a curved surface. These waves are studied to obtain a relation between deflection angle and Mach number . Each wave in this case is a Mach wave , so it is at an angle    α  =   s  i   n   -  1     (   1  M   )        α    s  i   superscript  n    1      1  M      \alpha=sin^{-1}(\frac{1}{M})   , where M is the Mach number immediately before the wave.

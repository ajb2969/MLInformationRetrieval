   Concentration dimension      Concentration dimension   In mathematics — specifically, in probability theory — the concentration dimension of a Banach space -valued random variable is a numerical measure of how “spread out” the random variable is compared to the norm on the space.  Definition  Let ( B , || ||) be a Banach space and let X be a Gaussian random variable taking values in B . That is, for every linear functional ℓ in the dual space  B ∗ , the real-valued random variable 〈 ℓ , X 〉 has a normal distribution .

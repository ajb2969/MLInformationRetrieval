Usually, non Kählerian nearly Kähler manifolds are called "strict nearly Kähler manifolds". Nearly Kähler manifolds, also known as almost Tachibana manifolds, were studied by Shun-ichi Tachibana in 19592 and then by Alfred Gray from 1970 on.3 For example, it was proved that any 6-dimensional strict nearly Kähler manifold is an Einstein manifold and has vanishing first Chern class (in particular, this implies spin).

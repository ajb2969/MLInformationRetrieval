HD 221525

HD 221525 is a subgiant star near the north celestial pole in the constellation Cepheus.1 At an apparent magnitude of 5.6, so that it can be seen with the naked eye under dark skies.2 It is about 30 times fainter than the star Polaris.3
(Figure)
HR 8938 is located directly below Polaris on this chart.

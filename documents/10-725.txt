   Jacobi–Anger expansion      Jacobi–Anger expansion   In mathematics , the Jacobi–Anger expansion (or Jacobi–Anger identity ) is an expansion of exponentials of trigonometric functions in the basis of their harmonics. It is useful in physics (for example, to convert between plane waves and cylindrical waves ), and in signal processing (to describe FM signals). This identity is named after the 19th-century mathematicians Carl Jacobi and Carl Theodor Anger .

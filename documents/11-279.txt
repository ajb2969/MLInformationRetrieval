   Autoregressive conditional duration      Autoregressive conditional duration   In financial econometrics , an autoregressive conditional duration ( ACD , Engle and Russell (1998)) model considers irregularly spaced and autocorrelated intertrade durations. ACD is analogous to GARCH . Indeed, in a continuous double auction (a common trading mechanism in many financial markets ) waiting times between two consecutive trades vary at random.

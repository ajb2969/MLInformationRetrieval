Polynomial-time algorithm for approximating the volume of convex bodies

The paper is a joint work by Martin Dyer, Alan M. Frieze and Ravindran Kannan.1

The main result of the paper is a randomized algorithm for finding an ϵ ϵ \epsilon approximation to the volume of a convex body K K K in n n n -dimensional Euclidean space by assuming the existence of a membership oracle. The algorithm takes time bounded by a polynomial in n n n , the dimension of K K K and 1 / ϵ 1 ϵ 1/\epsilon .

The algorithm is a sophisticated usage of the so-called Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) method. The basic scheme of the algorithm is a nearly uniform sampling from within K K K by placing a grid consisting n n n -dimensional cubes and doing a random walk over these cubes. By using the theory of rapidly mixing Markov chains, they show that it takes a polynomial time for the random walk to settle down to being a nearly uniform distribution.

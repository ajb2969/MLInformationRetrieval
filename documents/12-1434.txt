Papyrus 26

Papyrus 26 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by 

26, is an early copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Epistle to the Romans, it contains only Romans 1:1-16. The manuscript paleographically has been assigned to the late 6th or early 7th century.
The Greek text of this codex is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type. Aland placed it in Category I.1
It is currently housed at the Southern Methodist University in Dallas, Texas.23

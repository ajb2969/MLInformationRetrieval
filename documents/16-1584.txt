   Cokurtosis      Cokurtosis   In probability theory and statistics , cokurtosis is a measure of how much two random variables change together. Cokurtosis is the fourth standardized cross central moment . 1 If two random variables exhibit a high level of cokurtosis they will tend to undergo extreme positive and negative deviations at the same time. The cokurtosis between variables X and Y does not depend on the scale on which the variables are expressed. If we are analyzing the relationship between X and Y , the cokurtosis between X and Y will be the same as the cokurtosis between a + bX and c + dY , where a , b , c and d are constants.

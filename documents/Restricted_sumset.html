Cauchy-Davenport theorem

The Cauchy–Davenport theorem named after Augustin Louis Cauchy and Harold Davenport asserts that for any prime p and nonempty subsets A and B of the prime order cyclic group Z/pZ we have the inequality12

| 2 ∧ A | ≥ min { p , 2 | A | - 3 } superscript 2 A p 2 A 3 |2^{\wedge}A|\geq\min\{p,2|A|-3\}

We may use this to deduce the Erdős–Ginzburg–Ziv theorem: given any 2n−1 elements of Z/n, there is a non-trivial subset that sums to zero modulo n. (Here n does not need to be prime.)34

A direct consequence of the Cauchy-Davenport theorem is: Given any set S of p−1 or more elements, not necessarily distinct, of Z/pZ, every element of Z/pZ can be written as the sum of the elements of some subset (possibly empty) of S.5

Kneser's theorem generalises this to finite abelian groups.6

   Hattendorf's theorem      Hattendorf's theorem   Hattendorf's Theorem , attributed to K. Hattendorf (1868), is a theorem in actuarial science that describes the allocation of the variance or risk of the loss random variable over the lifetime of an actuarial reserve . In other words, Hattendorf's theorem demonstrates that the variation in the present value of the loss of an issued insurance policy can be allocated to the future years during which the insured is still alive.

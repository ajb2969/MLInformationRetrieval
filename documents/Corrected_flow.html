Corrected flow

Corrected Flow is the mass flow that would pass through a device (e.g. compressor, bypass duct, etc.) if the inlet pressure and temperature corresponded to ambient conditions at Sea Level, on a Standard Day (e.g. 14.696 lbf/in², 518.7R).

w θ / δ = w T / 518.7 / ( P / 14.696 ) w θ δ w T 518.7 P 14.696 w\sqrt{\theta}/{\delta}=w\sqrt{T/518.7}/(P/14.696)

The equivalent equations for Preferred SI Units are: (101.325kPa, 288.15K)

w θ / δ = w T / 288.15 / ( P / 101.325 ) w θ δ w T 288.15 P 101.325 w\sqrt{\theta}/{\delta}=w\sqrt{T/288.15}/(P/101.325)

w T / P = w θ / δ * 288.15 / 101.325 w T P w θ δ 288.15 101.325 w\sqrt{T}/{P}=w\sqrt{\theta}/{\delta}*\sqrt{288.15}/{101.325}

Nomenclature:

  • P P P Stagnation (or Total) Pressure
  • T T T Stagnation (or Total) Temperature
  • w w w Real Mass Flow
  • δ δ {\delta} Referred Pressure
  • θ θ {\theta} Referred Temperature

In relative form, Corrected Flow, Referred Flow and Non-Dimensional Flow are all measures of axial Mach number.

   Fusion frame      Fusion frame   In mathematics, a fusion frame of a vector space is a natural extension of a frame . It is an additive construct of several, potentially "overlapping" frames. The motivation for this concept comes from the event that a signal can not be acquired by a single sensor alone (a constraint found by limitations of hardware or data throughput), rather the partial components of the signal must be collected via a network of sensors, and the partial signal representations are then fused into the complete signal.  By construction, fusion frames easily lend themselves to parallel or distributed processing 1 of sensor networks consisting of arbitrary overlapping sensor fields.

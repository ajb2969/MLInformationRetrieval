Formerly it was labelled by 25a, 31p, and 7r.34 The manuscript is lacunose.

Description

According to the colophon it was written in 1087.6 The headpieces with geometric decorations. The initial letters in red.

Text

The Greek text of the codex is an eclectic, in the Epistles it is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type and the Byzantine in the Acts and Apocalypse. Aland placed it in Category III in epistles, and in Category V in the Acts and Book of Revelation.9

History

The manuscript was written by a scribe named Ioannes Tzoutzounas and was held in Asia Minor.31 It was purchased by John Covel, chaplain of the Levant Company at Constantinople 1670-1676.

It was examined by Mill, Griesbach, Bloomfield, Henri Omont, and F. H. A. Scrivener.32

Formerly it was labelled by 25a, 31p, and 7r. In 1908 C. R. Gregory gave the number 104 to it.33

It was cited in 27th edition of Nestle-Aland Novum Testamentum Graece only once (1 Cor 11:24).34

It is currently housed at the British Library (Harley 5537), at London.35

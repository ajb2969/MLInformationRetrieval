   Menger curvature      Menger curvature   In mathematics , the Menger curvature of a triple of points in n - dimensional  Euclidean space  R n is the reciprocal of the radius of the circle that passes through the three points. It is named after the Austrian - American  mathematician  Karl Menger .  Definition  Let x , y and z be three points in R n ; for simplicity, assume for the moment that all three points are distinct and do not lie on a single straight line. Let Π ⊆ R n be the Euclidean plane spanned by x , y and z and let C ⊆ Π be the unique Euclidean circle in Π that passes through x , y and z (the circumcircle of x , y and z ). Let R be the radius of C . Then the Menger curvature  c ( x , y , z ) of x , y and z is defined by        c   (  x  ,  y  ,  z  )    =   1  R    .        c   x  y  z      1  R     c(x,y,z)=\frac{1}{R}.     If the three points are collinear , R can be informally considered to be +∞, and it makes rigorous sense to define c ( x , y , z ) = 0. If any of the points x , y and z are coincident, again define c ( x , y , z ) = 0.

   Interpretability logic      Interpretability logic   Interpretability logics comprise a family of modal logics that extend provability logic to describe interpretability and/or various related metamathematical properties and relations such as weak interpretability , Π 1 -conservativity, cointerpretability , tolerance , cotolerance , and arithmetic complexities.  Main contributors to the field are Alessandro Berarducci, Petr Hájek, Konstantin Ignatiev, Giorgi Japaridze , Franco Montagna, Vladimir Shavrukov, Rineke Verbrugge, Albert Visser, and Domenico Zambella. Logic TOL : The language of TOL extends that of classical propositional logic by adding the modal operator   ◇   normal-◇   \Diamond   which is allowed to take any nonempty sequence of arguments.

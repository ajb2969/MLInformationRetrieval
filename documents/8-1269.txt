Overview of the proof  Green and Tao's proof has three main components:   SzemerÃ©di's theorem , which asserts that subsets of the integers with positive upper density have arbitrarily long arithmetic progressions. It does not a priori apply to the primes because the primes have density zero in the integers.  A transference principle that extends SzemerÃ©di's theorem to subsets of the integers which are pseudorandom in a suitable sense. Such a result is now called a relative SzemerÃ©di theorem.  A pseudorandom subset of the integers containing the primes as a dense subset.

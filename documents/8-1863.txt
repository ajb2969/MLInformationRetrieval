It is possible to prove that the notion of fuzzy subgroup is strictly related with the notions of fuzzy equivalence . In fact, assume that S is a set, G a group of transformations in S and (G,s) a fuzzy subgroup of G. Then, by setting   e(x,y) = Sup{s(h) : h is an element in G such that h(x) = y}   we obtain a fuzzy equivalence. Conversely, let e be a fuzzy equivalence in S and, for every transformation h of S, set   s(h)= Inf{e(x,h(x)): x‚ààS}.   Then s defines a fuzzy sub group of transformation in S. In a similar way we can relate the fuzzy submonoids with the fuzzy orders.

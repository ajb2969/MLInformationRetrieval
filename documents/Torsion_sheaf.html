Torsion sheaf

In mathematics, a torsion sheaf is a sheaf ℱ ℱ \mathcal{F} on a site for which, for every quasi-compact U, the space of sections Γ ( U , ℱ ) normal-Γ U ℱ \Gamma(U,\mathcal{F}) is a torsion group. Similarly, for a prime number l, we say a sheaf ℱ ℱ \mathcal{F} is l-torsion if every section over any quasi-compact is killed by a power of l.

A torsion sheaf on an étale site is the union of its constructible subsheaves.1

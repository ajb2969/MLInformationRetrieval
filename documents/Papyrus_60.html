Papyrus 60

Papyrus 60 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), signed by ùîì ùîì \mathfrak{P} 60, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of John, it contains John 16:29-19:26.

The manuscript paleographically has been assigned to the 6th or 7th century.

The Greek text of this codex is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type. Aland placed it in Category III.1

It is currently housed at the The Morgan Library & Museum (P. Colt 4) in New York City.23

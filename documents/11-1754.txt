   6-Acetyl-2,3,4,5-tetrahydropyridine      6-Acetyl-2,3,4,5-tetrahydropyridine   6-Acetyl-2,3,4,5-tetrahydropyridine , is an aroma compound and flavor that gives baked goods such as white bread , popcorn , and tortillas their typical smell, together with its structural homolog  2-acetyl-1-pyrroline .  6-Acetyl-2,3,4,5-tetrahydropyridine and 2-acetyl-1-pyrroline are usually formed by Maillard reactions during heating of food. Both compounds have odor thresholds below 0.06 ng / l . 1  2  Structure and properties  6-Acetyl-2,3,4,5-tetrahydropyridine is a substituted tetrahydro pyridine and a cyclic imine as well as a ketone .

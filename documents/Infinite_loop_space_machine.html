Infinite loop space machine

In topology, a branch of mathematics, given a topological monoid X up to homotopy (in a nice way), an infinite loop space machine produces a group completion of X together with infinite loop space structure. For example, one can take X to be the classifying space of a symmetric monoidal category S; that is, X = B S X B S X=BS . Then the machine produces the group completion B S → K ( S ) normal-→ B S K S BS\to K(S) . The space K ( S ) K S K(S) may be described by the K-theory spectrum of S.

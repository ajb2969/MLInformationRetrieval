In queueing theory, a discipline within the mathematical theory of probability, the M/M/‚àû queue is a multi-server queueing model where every arrival experiences immediate service and does not wait.1 In Kendall's notation it describes a system where arrivals are governed by a Poisson process, there are infinitely many servers, so jobs do not need to wait for a server. Each job has an exponentially distributed service time. It is a limit of the M/M/c queue model where the number of servers c becomes very large.

The model can be used to model bound lazy deletion performance.2

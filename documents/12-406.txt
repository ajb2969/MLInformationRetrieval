   Tripadi      Tripadi   Tripadi ( Kannada , Sanskrit lit. tri : three, pad or "adi": feet) is a native metre in the Kannada language dating back to c. 700 CE.  Definition  The tripadi consists of three lines, each differing from the others in the number of feet and moras ( Sanskrit  matras ), 1 but in accordance with the following rules:   The first line has 4 feet, each with 5 moras, and a caesura at the end of the second foot. 2  The 6th and 10th feet of the tripadi are each required to have the metrical pattern of a Brahma foot:       -  ⌣   or   ⌣  ⌣  ⌣   or   -  -   or   ⌣  ⌣  -     fragments   normal-⌣  or  normal-⌣  normal-⌣  normal-⌣  or    or  normal-⌣  normal-⌣     -\smile\ \mathrm{or}\ \smile\smile\smile\ \mathrm{or}\ --\ \mathrm{or}\ \smile%

The remaining feet have either 5 moras or 4, chosen to satisfy the rules of Nagavarma II : 3   Line 1 20 moras in four feet  Line 2 17 moras in four feet  Line 3 13 moras in three feet.   There is alliteration of the second letter of each line.

   De Donder–Weyl theory      De Donder–Weyl theory   In mathematical physics , the De Donder–Weyl theory is a formalism in the calculus of variations over spacetime which treats the space and time coordinates on equal footing. In this framework, a field is represented as a system that varies both in space and in time. The theory is a formulation of a covariant Hamiltonian field theory and for n = 1 it reduces to Hamiltonian mechanics (see also action principle in the calculus of variations ).

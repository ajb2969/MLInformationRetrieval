On a K√§hler manifold, the associated K√§hler form and metric are called K√§hler‚ÄìEinstein (or sometimes Einstein‚ÄìK√§hler) if its Ricci tensor is proportional to the metric tensor, R = Œª g R Œª g R=\lambda g , for some constant Œª. Originally the K√§hler condition is independent on the Einstein condition, in which Ricci tensor is proportional to Riemannian metric with constant real number. The important point is that if X is K√§hler then Christoffel symbols Œì Œ≤ Œ≥ Œ± subscript superscript normal-Œì Œ± Œ≤ Œ≥ \Gamma^{\alpha}_{\beta\gamma} vanish and Ricci curvature is much simplified. In fact Aubin and Yau proved the Calabi conjecture using the fact that on a compact K√§hler manifold with the first Chern class c1=0 there is a unique Ricci-flat K√§hler metric in each K√§hler class. But in non-compact case the situation turns to be more complicated and the final solution might not be reached.

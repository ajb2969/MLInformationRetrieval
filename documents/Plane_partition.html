Symmetries

Plane partitions may be classified according to various symmetries.

When viewed as three-dimensional arrays of blocks, however, more symmetries become evident: any permutation of the axes corresponds to a reflection or rotation of the plane partition. A plane partition that is invariant under all of these symmetries is called totally symmetric.

An additional symmetry is complementation: given a plane partition inside an a √ó b √ó c a b c a\times b\times c box, the complement is simply the result of removing the boxes of the plane partition from the box and reindexing appropriately. Totally symmetric plane partitions that are equal to their own complements are known as totally symmetric self-complementary plane partitions; they are known to be equinumerous with alternating sign matrices and so with numerous other combinatorial objects.

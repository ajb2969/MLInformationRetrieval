The Johnson S U subscript S U S_{U} distribution is a four-parameter family of probability distributions first investigated by N. L. Johnson in 1949.1 Johnson proposed it as a transformation of the normal distribution:2 z = γ + δ sinh - 1 ( x - ξ λ ) z γ δ superscript 1 x ξ λ z=\gamma+\delta\sinh^{-1}(\frac{x-\xi}{\lambda})

Generation of random variables

Let U be a random variable that is uniformly distributed on the unit interval [0, 1]. Johnson S U subscript S U S_{U} random variables can be generated from U as follows:

x = λ sinh ( Φ - 1 ( U ) - γ δ ) + ξ x λ superscript normal-Φ 1 U γ δ ξ x=\lambda\sinh\left(\frac{\Phi^{-1}(U)-\gamma}{\delta}\right)+\xi

where Φ is the cumulative distribution function of the normal distribution.

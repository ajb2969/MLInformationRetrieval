   Three subgroups lemma      Three subgroups lemma   In mathematics , more specifically group theory , the three subgroups lemma is a result concerning commutators . It is a consequence of the Hall–Witt identity .  Notation  In that which follows, the following notation will be employed:   If H and K are subgroups of a group G , the commutator of H and K will be denoted by [ H , K ]; if L is a third subgroup, the convention that [ H , K , L ] = [[ H , K ], L ] will be followed.  If x and y are elements of a group G , the conjugate of x by y will be denoted by    x  y     superscript  x  y    x^{y}   .  If H is a subgroup of a group G , then the centralizer of H in G will be denoted by C G ( H ).

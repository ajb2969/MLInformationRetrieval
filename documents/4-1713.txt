More generally for R an integral domain contained in a field K we define   ğ’ª   ğ’ª   \mathcal{O}   to be an R -order in a K -algebra A if it is a subring of A which is a full R -lattice. 1  When A is not a commutative ring , the idea of order is still important, but the phenomena are different. For example, the Hurwitz quaternions form a maximal order in the quaternions with rational co-ordinates; they are not the quaternions with integer coordinates in the most obvious sense. Maximal orders exist in general, but need not be unique: there is in general no largest order, but a number of maximal orders. An important class of examples is that of integral group rings .

Further generalizations  As with the two-dimensional Gaussâ€“Bonnet Theorem, there are generalizations when M is a manifold with boundary .  The Gaussâ€“Bonnet Theorem can be seen as a special instance in the theory of characteristic classes . The Gaussâ€“Bonnet integrand is the Euler class . Since it is a top-dimensional differential form, it is closed. The naturality of the Euler class means that when you change the Riemannian metric, you stay in the same cohomology class. That means that the integral of the Euler class remains constant as you vary the metric, and so is an invariant of smooth structure.

Dimension  The dimension of the row space is called the rank of the matrix. This is the same as the maximum number of linearly independent rows that can be chosen from the matrix, or equivalently the number of pivots. For example, the 3â€¯Ã—â€¯3 matrix in the example above has rank two. 4  The rank of a matrix is also equal to the dimension of the column space . The row space and null space are two of the four fundamental subspaces associated with a matrix A (the other two being the column space and left null space ).  Relation to coimage  If V and W are vector spaces , then the kernel of a linear transformation  T : V â†’ W is the set of vectors v âˆˆ V for which T ( v )Â = 0 . The kernel of a linear transformation is analogous to the null space of a matrix.  If V is an inner product space , then the orthogonal complement to the kernel can be thought of as a generalization of the row space. This is sometimes called the coimage of T . The transformation T is one-to-one on its coimage, and the coimage maps isomorphically onto the image of T .  When V is not an inner product space, the coimage of T can be defined as the quotient space  V /Â ker( T ).

P²-irreducible

In mathematics, a 3-manifold is P2-irreducible if it is irreducible and contains no 2-sided ℝ P 2 ℝ superscript P 2 \mathbb{R}P^{2} (real projective plane). An orientable manifold is P2-irreducible if and only if it is irreducible.1 Every non-orientable P2-irreducible manifold is a Haken manifold.2

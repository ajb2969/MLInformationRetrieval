While the Kelvin functions are defined as the real and imaginary parts of Bessel functions with x taken to be real, the functions can be analytically continued for complex arguments With the exception of Ber n ( x ) and Bei n ( x ) for integral n , the Kelvin functions have a branch point at x = 0.

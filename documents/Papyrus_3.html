Papyrus 3

Papyrus 3, designated by ğ”“ ğ”“ \mathfrak{P} 3 (in the numbering Gregory-Aland), is a small fragment of fifteen verses from the Gospel of Luke dating to the 6th/7th century.1 It is formed part of a lectionary. It is dated palaeographically to the 6th or 7th century.2

The Greek text-type of this codex is a mixed. Aland placed it in Category III.3

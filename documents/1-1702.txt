Examples  Temperature of the Sun  With his law Stefan also determined the temperature of the Sun 's surface. He learned from the data of Charles Soret (1854â€“1904) that the energy flux density from the Sun is 29 times greater than the energy flux density of a certain warmed metal lamella (a thin plate). A round lamella was placed at such a distance from the measuring device that it would be seen at the same angle as the Sun. Soret estimated the temperature of the lamella to be approximately 1900 Â°C to 2000Â Â°C. Stefan surmised that â…“ of the energy flux from the Sun is absorbed by the Earth's atmosphere , so he took for the correct Sun's energy flux a value 3/2 times greater than Soret's value, namely 29 Ã— 3/2 = 43.5.  Precise measurements of atmospheric absorption were not made until 1888 and 1904. The temperature Stefan obtained was a median value of previous ones, 1950Â Â°C and the absolute thermodynamic one 2200Â K. As 2.57 4 = 43.5, it follows from the law that the temperature of the Sun is 2.57 times greater than the temperature of the lamella, so Stefan got a value of 5430Â Â°C or 5700Â K (the modern value is 5778Â K 2 ). This was the first sensible value for the temperature of the Sun. Before this, values ranging from as low as 1800Â Â°C to as high as 13,000,000Â Â°C were claimed. The lower value of 1800Â Â°C was determined by Claude Servais Mathias Pouillet (1790â€“1868) in 1838 using the Dulong-Petit law .

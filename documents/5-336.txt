   Butterfly theorem      Butterfly theorem   (Figure)  Butterfly theorem.svg   The butterfly theorem is a classical result in Euclidean geometry , which can be stated as follows: 1  Let M be the midpoint of a chord  PQ of a circle , through which two other chords AB and CD are drawn; AD and BC intersect chord PQ at X and Y correspondingly. Then M is the midpoint of XY .

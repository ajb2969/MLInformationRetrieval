Order of accuracy

In numerical analysis, order of accuracy quantifies the rate of convergence of a numerical approximation of a differential equation to the exact solution. A numerical solution to a differential equation is said to be n n n th-order accurate if the error, E E E , is proportional to the step-size h h h to the n n n th power;1

The size of the error of a first-order accurate approximation is directly proportional to h h h .

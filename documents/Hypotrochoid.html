Hypotrochoid

A hypotrochoid is a roulette traced by a point attached to a circle of radius r rolling around the inside of a fixed circle of radius R, where the point is a distance d from the center of the interior circle.

The parametric equations for a hypotrochoid are:1

x ( θ ) = ( R - r ) cos θ + d cos ( R - r r θ ) x θ R r θ d R r r θ x(\theta)=(R-r)\cos\theta+d\cos\left({R-r\over r}\theta\right)

y ( θ ) = ( R - r ) sin θ - d sin ( R - r r θ ) . y θ R r θ d R r r θ y(\theta)=(R-r)\sin\theta-d\sin\left({R-r\over r}\theta\right).

Where θ θ \theta is the angle formed by the horizontal and the center of the rolling circle (note that these are not polar equations because θ θ \theta is not the polar angle).

Special cases include the hypocycloid with d = r and the ellipse with R = 2r.

The classic Spirograph toy traces out hypotrochoid and epitrochoid curves.

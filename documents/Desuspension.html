Desuspension

In topology, a field within mathematics, desuspension is an operation inverse to suspension.1

Definition

In general, given an n-dimensional space X X X , the suspension Σ X normal-Σ X \Sigma{X} has dimension n + 1. Thus, the operation of suspension creates a way of moving up in dimension. In the 1950s, to define a way of moving down, mathematicians introduced an inverse operation Σ - 1 superscript normal-Σ 1 \Sigma^{-1} , called desuspension.2 Therefore, given an n-dimensional space X X X , the desuspension Σ - 1 X superscript normal-Σ 1 X \Sigma^{-1}{X} has dimension n – 1.

Reasons

The reasons to introduce desuspension:

  1. Desuspension makes the category of spaces a triangulated category.
  2. If arbitrary coproducts were allowed, desuspension would result in all cohomology functors being representable.

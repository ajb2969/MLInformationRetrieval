Any monoid with involution is a dagger category with only one object. In fact, every endomorphism  hom-set in a dagger category is not simply a monoid , but a monoid with involution, because of the dagger.    A discrete category is trivially a dagger category.    A groupoid (and as trivial corollary a group ) also has a dagger structure with the adjoint of a morphism being its inverse. In this case, all morphisms are unitary.

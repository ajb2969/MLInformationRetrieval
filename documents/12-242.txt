   Landau–Lifshitz model      Landau–Lifshitz model   In solid-state physics , the Landau–Lifshitz equation ( LLE ), named for Lev Landau and Evgeny Lifshitz , is a partial differential equation describing time evolution of magnetism in solids, depending on 1 time variable and 1, 2, or 3 space variables.  Landau–Lifshitz equation  The LLE describes an anisotropic magnet. The equation is described in  as follows: It is an equation for a vector field  S , in other words a function on R 1+ n taking values in R 3 .

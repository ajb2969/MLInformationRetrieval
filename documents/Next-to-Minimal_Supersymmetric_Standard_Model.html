Next-to-Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model

In particle physics, NMSSM is an acronym for Next-to-Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model. 12345 It is a supersymmetric extension to the Standard Model that adds an additional singlet chiral superfield to the MSSM and can be used to dynamically generate the μ μ \mu term, solving the μ μ \mu -problem. Articles about the NMSSM are available for review.67

The Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model does not explain why the μ μ \mu parameter in the superpotential term μ H u H d μ subscript H u subscript H d \mu H_{u}H_{d} is at the electroweak scale. The idea behind the Next to Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model is to promote the μ μ \mu term to a gauge singlet, chiral superfield S S S . Phenomenology

Due to the additional singlet S S S the NMSSM alters in general the phenomenology of both the Higgs sector and the neutralino sector compared to the MSSM.

Higgs phenomenology

In the Standard Model we have one physical Higgs boson. In the MSSM we encounter five physical Higgs bosons. Due to the additional singlet S ^ normal-^ S \hat{S} in the NMSSM we have two more Higgs bosons, that is, in total seven physical Higgs bosons. The Higgs sector is therefore much richer compared to the MSSM. In particular, the Higgs potential is in general no longer invariant under CP transformations; see CP violation. Typically, the Higgs bosons in the NMSSM are denoted in an order with increasing masses, that is, by H 1 , H 2 , … , H 7 subscript H 1 subscript H 2 normal-… subscript H 7 H_{1},H_{2},...,H_{7} with H 1 subscript H 1 H_{1} the lightest Higgs boson. In the special case of a CP conserving Higgs potential we have three CP even Higgs bosons, H 1 , H 2 , H 3 subscript H 1 subscript H 2 subscript H 3 H_{1},H_{2},H_{3} , two CP odd ones, A 1 , A 2 subscript A 1 subscript A 2 A_{1},A_{2} and a pair of charged Higgs bosons, H + , H - superscript H superscript H H^{+},H^{-} . In the MSSM, the lightest Higgs boson is always Standard Model-like, and therefore its production and decays are roughly known. In the NMSSM, the lightest Higgs can be very light (even of the order of 1 GeV) and may have escaped detection so far. In addition, in the CP-conserving case, the lightest CP-even Higgs boson turns out to have an enhanced lower bound compared to the MSSM. This is one of the reasons why the NMSSM deserves much attraction in recent years.

Neutralino phenomenology

The spin-1/2 singlino S ~ normal-~ S \tilde{S} gives a fifth neutralino, compared to the four neutralinos of the MSSM. The singlino does not couple to gauge bosons, gauginos (the superpartners of the gauge bosons), leptons, sleptons (the superpartners of the leptons), quarks or squarks (the superpartners of the quarks). Suppose that a supersymmetric partner particle is produced at a collider, for instance at the LHC, the singlino is omitted in cascade decays and therefore escapes detection. However in case the singlino is the lightest supersymmetric particle (LSP) all supersymmetric partner particles eventually decay into the singlino. Due to R parity conservation this LSP is stable. In this way the singlino could be detected via missing transversal energy in the detector.

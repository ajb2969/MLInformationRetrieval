Quasi-fibration

In algebraic topology, a branch of mathematics, a quasi-fibration, introduced by Dold and Thom, is a continuous map of topological spaces

 
  such that the fibers 
 
 
 
  are homotopy equivalent to the homotopy fiber of f via the canonical map. A fibration is a quasi-fibration.

   Sector mass spectrometer      Sector mass spectrometer   A sector instrument is a general term for a class of mass spectrometer that uses a static electric or magnetic sector or some combination of the two (separately in space) as a mass analyzer. 1 A popular combination of these sectors has been the BEB (magnetic-electric-magnetic). Most modern sector instruments are double-focusing instruments (first developed by A.Â Dempster , K.Â Bainbridge and J.Â Mattauch in 1936 2 ) in that they focus the ion beams both in direction and velocity. 3  Theory  The behavior of ions in a homogeneous, linear, static electric or magnetic field (separately) as is found in a sector instrument is simple. The physics are described by a single equation called the Lorentz force law.

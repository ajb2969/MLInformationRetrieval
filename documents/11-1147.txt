The converse of Casey's theorem is also true. 5 That is, if equality holds, the circles are tangent.  Applications  Casey's theorem and its converse can be used to prove a variety of statements in Euclidean geometry . For example, the shortest known proof 6 of Feuerbach's theorem uses the converse theorem.

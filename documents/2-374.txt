   Sum rule in integration      Sum rule in integration   In calculus , the sum rule in integration states that the integral of a sum of two functions is equal to the sum of their integrals. It is of particular use for the integration of sums , and is one part of the linearity of integration .  As with many properties of integrals in calculus, the sum rule applies both to definite integrals and indefinite integrals . For indefinite integrals, the sum rule states       ∫    (   f  +  g   )   d  x    =    ∫    f   d  x    +   ∫    g   d  x               f  g   d  x          f  d  x        g  d  x       \int\left(f+g\right)\,dx=\int f\,dx+\int g\,dx     Application to indefinite integrals  For example, if you know that the integral of exp(x) is exp(x) from calculus with exponentials and that the integral of cos(x) is sin(x) from calculus with trigonometry then:       ∫    (    e  x   +   cos  x    )   d  x    =    ∫     e  x    d  x    +   ∫   cos    x   d  x      =    e  x   +   sin  x   +  C                superscript  e  x     x    d  x           superscript  e  x   d  x          x  d  x              superscript  e  x     x   C      \int\left(e^{x}+\cos{x}\right)\,dx=\int e^{x}\,dx+\int\cos{x}\ \,dx=e^{x}+\sin%

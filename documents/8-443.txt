 \frac{1}{p^{ks}}+\ldots     When     ℜ   (  s  )    >  1        s   1    \Re(s)>1   , we have | p − s | \left|\zeta(s) - \prod_{p \le q}\left(\frac{1}{1-p^{-s}}\right)\right|  where σ is the real part of s . By the fundamental theorem of arithmetic , the partial product when expanded out gives a sum consisting of those terms n − s where n is a product of primes less than or equal to q . The inequality results from the fact that therefore only integers larger than q can fail to appear in this expanded out partial product. Since the difference between the partial product and ζ( s ) goes to zero when σ > 1, we have convergence in this region.

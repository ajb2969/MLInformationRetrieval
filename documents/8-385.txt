The principle of least constraint is qualitatively similar to Hamilton's principle , which states that the true path taken by a mechanical system is an extremum of the action . However, Gauss's principle is a true (local) minimal principle, whereas the other is an extremal principle.  Hertz's principle of least curvature  Hertz's principle of least curvature is a special case of Gauss's principle, restricted by the two conditions that there be no applied forces and that all masses are identical. (Without loss of generality, the masses may be set equal to one.)

   Compound Poisson distribution      Compound Poisson distribution   In probability theory , a compound Poisson distribution is the probability distribution of the sum of a number of independent identically-distributed random variables , where the number of terms to be added is itself a Poisson-distributed variable. In the simplest cases, the result can be either a continuous or a discrete distribution . The compound Poisson distribution is obtained by marginalising the joint distribution of ( Y , N ) over N , where this joint distribution is obtained by combining the conditional distribution Y | N with the marginal distribution of N .

Baumslag–Solitar group

In the mathematical field of group theory, the Baumslag–Solitar groups are examples of two-generator one-relator groups that play an important role in combinatorial group theory and geometric group theory as (counter)examples and test-cases. They are given by the group presentation

⟨ a , b : b a m b - 1 = a n ⟩ . delimited-⟨⟩ normal-: a b b superscript a m superscript b 1 superscript a n \left\langle a,b\ :\ ba^{m}b^{-1}=a^{n}\right\rangle.

For each integer m m m and n n n , the Baumslag–Solitar group is denoted B S ( m , n ) B S m n BS(m,n) . The relation in the presentation is called the Baumslag–Solitar relation.

Some of the various B S ( m , n ) B S m n BS(m,n) are well-known groups. B S ( 1 , 1 ) B S 1 1 BS(1,1) is the free abelian group on two generators, and B S ( 1 , − 1 ) B S 1 normal-− 1 BS(1,−1) is the fundamental group of the Klein bottle.

The groups were defined by Gilbert Baumslag and Donald Solitar in 1962 to provide examples of non-Hopfian groups. The groups contain residually finite groups, Hopfian groups that are not residually finite, and non-Hopfian groups.

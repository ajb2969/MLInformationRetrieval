Burau representation

In mathematics the Burau representation is a representation of the braid groups, named after and originally studied by the German mathematician Werner Burau1 during the 1930s. The Burau representation has two common and near-equivalent formulations, the reduced and unreduced Burau representations.

Geometry

Squier showed that the Burau representation preserves a sesquilinear form.10 Moreover, when the variable t t t is chosen to be a transcendental unit complex number near 1 1 1 it is a positive-definite Hermitian pairing, thus the Burau representation can be thought of as a map into the Unitary group.

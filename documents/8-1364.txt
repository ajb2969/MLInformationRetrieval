In chemistry, a contact number (CN) is a simple solvent exposure measure that measures residue burial in proteins.1 The definition of CN varies between authors, but is generally defined as the number of either C
 
 
 
  or C
 
 
 
  atoms within a sphere around the C
 
 
 
 
  or C
 
 
 
  atom of the residue. The radius of the sphere is typically chosen to be between 8 and 14Å.

Note that the unitarity and tracelessness of the two Pauli matrices is preserved, but not Hermiticity in dimensions higher than two. Since Pauli matrices describe Quaternions , Sylvester dubbed the higher-dimensional analogs "nonions", "sedenions", etc.  These two matrices are also the cornerstone of quantum mechanical dynamics in finite-dimensional vector spaces 3 4 5 as formulated by Hermann Weyl , and find routine applications in numerous areas of mathematical physics. 6 The clock matrix amounts to the exponential of position in a "clock" of d hours, and the shift matrix is just the translation operator in that cyclic vector space, so the exponential of the momentum. They are (finite-dimensional) representations of the corresponding elements of the Heisenberg group on a d -dimensional Hilbert space.

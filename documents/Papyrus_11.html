Papyrus 11

Papyrus 11 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), signed by ğ”“ ğ”“ \mathfrak{P} 11, is a copy of a part of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the First Epistle to the Corinthians. It contains fragments 1 Corinthians 1:17-22; 2:9-12.14; 3:1-3,5-6; 4:3; 5:5-5.7-8; 6:5-9.11-18; 7:3-6.10-11.12-14. Only some portions of the codex can be read.1

The manuscript palaeographically had been assigned to the 7th century.2

The Greek text of this codex is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type. Aland placed it in Category II.3

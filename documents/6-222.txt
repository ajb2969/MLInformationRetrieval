Finite variation processes  A process X is said to have finite variation if it has bounded variation over every finite time interval (with probability 1). Such processes are very common including, in particular, all continuously differentiable functions. The quadratic variation exists for all continuous finite variation processes, and is zero.  This statement can be generalized to non-continuous processes. Any cÃ dlÃ g finite variation process X has quadratic variation equal to the sum of the squares of the jumps of X .

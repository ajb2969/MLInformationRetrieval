Sparse activation: For example, in a randomly initialized networks, only about 50% of hidden units are activated (having a non-zero output).  Efficient gradient propagation: No vanishing gradient problem or exploding effect.  Efficient computation: Only comparison, addition and multiplication.   For the first time in 2011, 10 the use of the rectifier as a non-linearity has been shown to enable training deep supervised neural networks without requiring unsupervised pre-training. Rectified linear units, compared to sigmoid function or similar activation functions, allow for faster and effective training of deep neural architectures on large and complex datasets.  Potential problems   Non-differentiable at zero: however it is differentiable at any point arbitrarily close to 0.

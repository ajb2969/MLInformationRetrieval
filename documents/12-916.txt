Model parameters and experimental data from PolymerFEM.com  The Yeoh  hyperelastic material model 1 is a phenomenological model for the deformation of nearly incompressible , nonlinear  elastic materials such as rubber . The model is based on Ronald Rivlin's observation that the elastic properties of rubber may be described using a strain energy density function which is a power series in the strain invariants      I  1   ,   I  2   ,   I  3       subscript  I  1    subscript  I  2    subscript  I  3     I_{1},I_{2},I_{3}   . 2 The Yeoh model for incompressible rubber is a function only of    I  1     subscript  I  1    I_{1}   . For compressible rubbers, a dependence on    I  3     subscript  I  3    I_{3}   is added on. Since a polynomial form of the strain energy density function is used but all the three invariants of the left Cauchy-Green deformation tensor are not, the Yeoh model is also called the reduced polynomial model .

Kantorovich theorem

The Kantorovich theorem is a mathematical statement on the convergence of Newton's method. It was first stated by Leonid Kantorovich in 1940.

Newton's method constructs a sequence of points that‚Äîwith good luck‚Äîwill converge to a solution x x x of an equation f ( x ) = 0 f x 0 f(x)=0 or a vector solution of a system of equation F ( x ) = 0 F x 0 F(x)=0 . The Kantorovich theorem gives conditions on the initial point of this sequence. If those conditions are satisfied then a solution exists close to the initial point and the sequence converges to that point.

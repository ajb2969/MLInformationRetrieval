 In any interval I_n$ we have at most countable many points of discontinuity, and since a countable union of at most countable sets is at most countable, it follows that the set of all discontinuities is at most countable.  Remark  One can prove 6 7 that all points of discontinuity of a monotone real-valued function defined on an interval are jump discontinuities and hence, by our definition, of the first kind. With this remark Froda's theorem takes the stronger form:  Let f be a monotone function defined on an interval  $I$ . Then the set of discontinuities is at most countable .

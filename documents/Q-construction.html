Q-construction

In algebra, Quillen's Q-construction associates to an exact category (e.g., an abelian category) an algebraic K-theory. The construction is widely applicable and is used to define an algebraic K-theory in a non-classical context. For example, one can define equivariant K-theory as π * subscript π \pi_{*} of B + superscript B B^{+} of the category of equivariant sheaves on a scheme.

Waldhausen's S-construction generalizes the Q-construction in a stable sense; in fact, the former, which uses a more general Waldhausen category, produces a spectrum instead of a space. Grayson's binary complex also gives a construction of algebraic K-theory for exact categories.1 See also module spectrum#K-theory for a K-theory of a ring spectrum.

   Saddlepoint approximation method      Saddlepoint approximation method   The saddlepoint approximation method, initially proposed by Daniels (1954) is a specific example of the mathematical saddlepoint technique applied to statistics . It provides a highly accurate approximation formula for any PDF or probability mass function of a distribution, based on the moment generating function . There is also a formula for the CDF of the distribution, proposed by Lugannani and Rice (1980).

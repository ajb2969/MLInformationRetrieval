The implicit function theorem describes conditions, under which an equation F ( x , y , z ) = 0 F x y z 0 F(x,y,z)=0 can be solved (theoretically) for x, y or z. But in general the solution may not be feasible. This theorem is the key for the computation of essential geometric features of the surface: tangent planes, surface normals, curvatures (s. below). But they have an essential drawback: their visualization is difficult.

Visualization of implicit surfaces

The visualization of implicit surfaces needs great efforts. Essentially there are two ideas visualizing an implicit surface. One generates a net of polygons which is visualized (see surface triangulation) and the second relies on ray tracing which determines intersection points of rays with the surface.

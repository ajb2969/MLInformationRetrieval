   Hjorth parameters      Hjorth parameters   Hjorth Parameters are indicators of statistical properties used in signal processing in the time domain introduced by Bo Hjorth in 1970. 1 The parameters are Activity, Mobility, and Complexity. They are commonly used in the analysis of electroencephalography signals for feature extraction. 2 The Hjorth parameters are normalized slope descriptors (NSDs) usually used in sleep EEG processing for data reducing and/or automatic sleep stage scoring. 3  Parameters  Hjorth Activity  The activity parameter represents the signal power, the variance of a time function. This can indicate the surface of power spectrum in the frequency domain. This is represented by the following equation:        A  c  t  i  v  i  t  y   =   v  a  r   (   y   (  t  )    )     .        A  c  t  i  v  i  t  y     v  a  r    y  t      Activity=var(y(t)).     Where y(t) represents the signal.  Hjorth Mobility  The mobility parameter represents the mean frequency, or the proportion of standard deviation of the power spectrum. This is defined as the square root of variance of the first derivative of the signal y(t) divided by the signal y(t)        M  o  b  i  l  i  t  y   =     v  a  r   (   y   (  t  )     d  y    d  t     )     v  a  r   (   y   (  t  )    )       .        M  o  b  i  l  i  t  y         v  a  r    y  t      d  y     d  t        v  a  r    y  t        Mobility=\sqrt{\frac{{var(y(t)\frac{dy}{dt})}}{var(y(t))}}.     Hjorth Complexity  The Complexity parameter represents the change in frequency. The parameter compares the signal's similarity to a pure sine wave , where the value converges to 1 if the signal is more similar.        C  o  m  p  l  e  x  i  t  y   =    M  o  b  i  l  i  t  y   (   y   (  t  )     d  y    d  t     )     M  o  b  i  l  i  t  y   (   y   (  t  )    )      .        C  o  m  p  l  e  x  i  t  y       M  o  b  i  l  i  t  y    y  t      d  y     d  t        M  o  b  i  l  i  t  y    y  t       Complexity=\frac{{Mobility(y(t)\frac{dy}{dt})}}{Mobility(y(t))}.

Stiefelâ€“Whitney classes are indexed from 0 to n , where n is the dimension of the vector space fiber of the vector bundle. If the Stiefelâ€“Whitney class of index i is nonzero, then there cannot exist ( n âˆ’ i +1) everywhere linearly independent sections of the vector bundle. A nonzero n th Stiefelâ€“Whitney class indicates that every section of the bundle must vanish at some point. A nonzero first Stiefelâ€“Whitney class indicates that the vector bundle is not orientable . For example, the first Stiefelâ€“Whitney class of the MÃ¶bius strip , as a line bundle over the circle, is not zero, whereas the first Stiefelâ€“Whitney class of the trivial line bundle over the circle, S 1 Ã— R is zero.  The Stiefelâ€“Whitney class was named for Eduard Stiefel and Hassler Whitney and is an example of a Z /2 Z - characteristic class associated to real vector bundles.  In algebraic geometry one can also define analogous Stiefelâ€“Whitney classes for vector bundles with a non-degenerate quadratic form, taking values in etale cohomology groups or in Milnor K-theory . As a special case one can define Stiefelâ€“Whitney classes for quadratic forms over fields, the first two cases being the discriminant and the Hasseâ€“Witt invariant .

modal depth of a formula indicates 'how far' one needs to look in a Kripke model when checking the validity of the formula. For each modal operator, one needs to transition from a world in the model to a world that is accessible through the accessibility relation . The modal depth indicates the longest 'chain' of transitions from a world to the next that is needed to verify the validity of a formula.  For example, to check whether     M  ,  w   ⊧   ◇  ◇  φ      models   M  w     normal-◇  normal-◇  φ     M,w\models\Diamond\Diamond\varphi   , one needs to check whether there exists an accessible world   v   v   v   for which     M  ,  v   ⊧   ◇  φ      models   M  v     normal-◇  φ     M,v\models\Diamond\varphi   . If that is the case, one needs to check whether there is also a world   u   u   u   such that     M  ,  u   ⊧  φ     models   M  u   φ    M,u\models\varphi   and   u   u   u   is accessible from   v   v   v   . We have made two steps from the world   w   w   w   (from   w   w   w   to   v   v   v   and from   v   v   v   to   u   u   u   ) in the model to determine whether the formula holds; this is, by definition, the modal depth of that formula.

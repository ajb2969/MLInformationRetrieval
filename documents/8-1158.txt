Weak convergence of measures  In mathematics and statistics , weak convergence (also known as narrow convergence or weak-* convergence , which is a more appropriate name from the point of view of functional analysis , but less frequently used) is one of many types of convergence relating to the convergence of measures . It depends on a topology on the underlying space and thus is not a purely measure theoretic notion.  There are several equivalent definitions of weak convergence of a sequence of measures, some of which are (apparently) more general than others.

The Exner equation is a statement of conservation of mass that applies to sediment in a fluvial system such as a river . 1 It was developed by the Austrian meteorologist and sedimentologist Felix Maria Exner , from whom it derives its name. 2  The equation  The Exner equation describes conservation of mass between sediment in the bed of a channel and sediment that is being transported . It states that bed elevation increases (the bed aggrades ) proportionally to the amount of sediment that drops out of transport, and conversely decreases (the bed degrades ) proportionally to the amount of sediment that becomes entrained by the flow.

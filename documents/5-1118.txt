Any hyperbolic fixed point (or equilibrium point ) of a continuous dynamical system is locally asymptotically stable if and only if the Jacobian of the dynamical system is Hurwitz stable at the fixed point.  The Hurwitz stability matrix is in crucial part on control theory . A system is stable if its control matrix is a Hurwitz matrix. The negative real components of the eigenvalues of the matrix represent negative feedback . Similarly, a system is inherently unstable if any of the eigenvalues have positive real components, representing positive feedback .

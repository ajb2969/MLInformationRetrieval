   Draft:Adaptive filtering by optimal projection      Draft:Adaptive filtering by optimal projection     Adaptive filtering by optimal projection (AFOP) is a mathematical procedure for filtering a multivariate signal. It is a linear, spatial, instantaneous and adaptive filter. The method consists on constructing a filtering matrix which will reduce artifact components and preserve at most main signal components. The construction of the matrix requires two steps:   The first one to determine the distribution of artifacts using the common spatial pattern (CSP).    The second one to determine the distribution of main signals using a linear regression .

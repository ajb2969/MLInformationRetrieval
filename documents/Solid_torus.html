Solid torus

In mathematics, a solid torus is the topological space formed by sweeping a disk around a circle.1 It is homeomorphic to the Cartesian product S 1 × D 2 superscript S 1 superscript D 2 S^{1}\times D^{2} of the disk and the circle,2 endowed with the product topology. A standard way to visualize a solid torus is as a toroid, embedded in 3-space. However, it should be distinguished from a torus, which has the same visual appearance: the torus is the two-dimensional space on the boundary of a toroid, while the solid torus includes also the interior space surrounded by the torus.

Topological properties

The solid torus is a connected, compact, orientable 3-dimensional manifold with boundary. The boundary is homeomorphic to S 1 × S 1 superscript S 1 superscript S 1 S^{1}\times S^{1} , the ordinary torus.

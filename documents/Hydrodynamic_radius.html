Hydrodynamic radius

The hydrodynamic radius of a macromolecule or colloid particle has two meanings. Some books use it as a synonym for the Stokes radius. 1

Others books define a theoretical hydrodynamic radius R hyd subscript R hyd R_{\rm hyd} . They consider the macromolecule or colloid particle to be a collection of N N N subparticles. This is done most commonly for polymers; the subparticles would then be the units of the polymer. R hyd subscript R hyd R_{\rm hyd} is defined by

The theoretical hydrodynamic radius R hyd subscript R hyd R_{\rm hyd} arises in the study of the dynamic properties of polymers moving in a solvent. It is often similar in magnitude to the radius of gyration.

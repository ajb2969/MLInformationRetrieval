   Carminati–McLenaghan invariants      Carminati–McLenaghan invariants   In general relativity , the Carminati–McLenaghan invariants or CM scalars are a set of 16 scalar curvature invariants for the Riemann tensor . This set is usually supplemented with at least two additional invariants.  Mathematical definition  The CM invariants consist of 6 real scalars plus 5 complex scalars, making a total of 16 invariants.

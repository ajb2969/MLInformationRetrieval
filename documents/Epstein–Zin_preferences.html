Epstein–Zin preferences

In economics, Epstein–Zin preferences (or Epstein–Zin-Weil preferences) refers to a specification of recursive utility.

A recursive utility function can be constructed from two components: a time aggregator that characterizes preferences in the absence of uncertainty and a risk aggregator that defines the certainty equivalent function that characterizes preferences over static gambles and is used to aggregate the risk associated with future utility. With Epstein–Zin preferences, the time aggregator is a linearly homogenous CES aggregate of current consumption and the certainty equivalent of future utility.

   Legendre's equation      Legendre's equation   In mathematics, Legendre's equation is the Diophantine equation        a   x  2    +   b   y  2    +   c   z  2     =  0.          a   superscript  x  2      b   superscript  y  2      c   superscript  z  2     0.    ax^{2}+by^{2}+cz^{2}=0.     The equation is named for Adrien Marie Legendre who proved in 1785 that it is solvable in integers x , y , z , not all zero, if and only if − bc , − ca and − ab are quadratic residues modulo a , b and c , respectively, where a , b , c are nonzero, square-free , pairwise relatively prime integers , not all positive or all negative .

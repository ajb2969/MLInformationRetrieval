Papyrus 92

Papyrus 92 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 92, (PNarmuthis 69.39a/229a) is an early New Testament papyrus.1

Description

The writing is in 27 lines per page.2

The Greek text of this codex is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type. 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 92 shows strong affinity with 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 46, Codex Sinaiticus, and Vaticanus.3

It is currently housed at the Egyptian Museum (Inv. 69,39a + 69,229a) in Cairo.45

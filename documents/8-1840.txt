   Gårding's inequality      Gårding's inequality   In mathematics , Gårding's inequality is a result that gives a lower bound for the bilinear form induced by a real linear elliptic partial differential operator . The inequality is named after Lars Gårding .  Statement of the inequality  Let Ω be a bounded , open domain in n - dimensional  Euclidean space and let H k (Ω) denote the Sobolev space of k -times weakly differentiable functions u : Ω → R with weak derivatives in L 2 . Assume that Ω satisfies the k -extension property, i.e., that there exists a bounded linear operator  E : H k (Ω) → H k ( R n ) such that ( Eu )| Ω = u for all u in H k (Ω).

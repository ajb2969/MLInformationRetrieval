   Fundamental theorem of linear programming      Fundamental theorem of linear programming   In mathematical optimization , the fundamental theorem of linear programming states, in a weak formulation, that the maxima and minima of a linear function over a convex polygonal region occur at the region's corners. Further, if an extreme value occurs at two corners, then it must also occur everywhere on the line segment between them.

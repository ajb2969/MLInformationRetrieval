Wood method

The Wood method, also known as the Merchant–Rankine–Wood method, is a structural analysis method which was developed to determine estimates for the effective buckling length of a compressed member included in a building frames, both in sway and a non-sway buckling modes.12 It is named after R. H. Wood.

According to this method, the ratio between the critical buckling length and the real length of a column is determined based on two redistribution coefficients, η 1 subscript η 1 \eta_{1} and η 2 subscript η 2 \eta_{2} , which are mapped to a ratio between the effective buckling length of a compressed member and its real length.

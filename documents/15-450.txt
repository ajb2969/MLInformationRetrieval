   Bennett's laws      Bennett's laws   Bennett's laws of quantum information are:   1 qubit    ⩾     \geqslant   1 bit ( classical )  1 qubit   ⩾     \geqslant   1 ebit ( entanglement bit)  1 ebit + 1 qubit   ⩾     \geqslant   2 bits (i.e. superdense coding )  1 ebit + 2 bits   ⩾     \geqslant   1 qubit (i.e. quantum teleportation )   where   ⩾     \geqslant   indicates 'can do the job of.'  These principles were formulated around 1993 by Charles H. Bennett .

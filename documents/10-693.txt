   (R)-aminopropanol dehydrogenase      (R)-aminopropanol dehydrogenase   In enzymology , a (R)-aminopropanol dehydrogenase () is an enzyme that catalyzes the chemical reaction   (R)-1-aminopropan-2-ol + NAD +    ⇌   normal-⇌   \rightleftharpoons   aminoacetone + NADH + H +    Thus, the two substrates of this enzyme are (R)-1-aminopropan-2-ol and NAD + , whereas its 3 products are aminoacetone , NADH , and H + .  This enzyme belongs to the family of oxidoreductases , specifically those acting on the CH-OH group of donor with NAD + or NADP + as acceptor. The systematic name of this enzyme class is (R)-1-aminopropan-2-ol:NAD + oxidoreductase . Other names in common use include L-aminopropanol dehydrogenase , 1-aminopropan-2-ol-NAD + dehydrogenase , L( + )-1-aminopropan-2-ol:NAD + oxidoreductase , 1-aminopropan-2-ol-dehydrogenase , DL-1-aminopropan-2-ol: NAD + dehydrogenase , and L( + )-1-aminopropan-2-ol-NAD + /NADP + oxidoreductase . This enzyme participates in glycine , serine and threonine metabolism. It requires potassium as a cofactor .

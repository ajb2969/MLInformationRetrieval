   Casson invariant      Casson invariant   In 3-dimensional topology , a part of the mathematical field of geometric topology , the Casson invariant is an integer-valued invariant of oriented integral homology 3-spheres , introduced by Andrew Casson .  Kevin Walker (1992) found an extension to rational homology 3-spheres , called the Casson–Walker invariant , and Christine Lescop (1995) extended the invariant to all closed oriented 3-manifolds .  Definition  A Casson invariant is a surjective map λ from oriented integral homology 3-spheres to Z satisfying the following properties:   λ( S 3 ) = 0.  Let Σ be an integral homology 3-sphere.

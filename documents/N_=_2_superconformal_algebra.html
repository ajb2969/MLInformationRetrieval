N = 2 superconformal algebra

In mathematical physics, the N = 2 superconformal algebra is an infinite-dimensional Lie superalgebra, related to supersymmetry, that occurs in string theory and conformal field theory. It has important applications in mirror symmetry. It was introduced by as a gauge algebra of the U(1) fermionic string.

Definition

There are two slightly different ways to describe the N = 2 superconformal algebra, called the N = 2 Ramond algebra and the N = 2 Neveu–Schwarz algebra, which are isomorphic (see below) but differ in the choice of standard basis. Kazama–Suzuki supersymmetric coset construction

generalized the SU(2) coset construction to any pair consisting of a simple compact Lie group G G G and a closed subgroup H H H of maximal rank, i.e. containing a maximal torus T T T of G G G , with the additional condition that the dimension of the centre of H H H is non-zero. In this case the compact Hermitian symmetric space G / H G H G/H is a Kähler manifold, for example when H = T H T H=T . The physical states lie in a single orbit of the affine Weyl group, which again implies the Weyl–Kac character formula for the affine Kac–Moody algebra of G G G .3

Transitive models of set theory  Transitive classes are often used for construction of interpretations of set theory in itself, usually called inner models . The reason is that properties defined by bounded formulas are absolute for transitive classes.  A transitive set (or class) that is a model of a formal system of set theory is called a transitive model of the system. Transitivity is an important factor in determining the absoluteness of formulas.

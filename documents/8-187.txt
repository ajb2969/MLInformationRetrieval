The theorem was discovered by and named for Joseph Bertrand . 2  General preliminaries  All attractive central forces can produce circular orbits, which are naturally closed orbits . The only requirement is that the central force exactly equals the centripetal force , which determines the required angular velocity for a given circular radius. Non-central forces (i.e., those that depend on the angular variables as well as the radius) are ignored here, since they do not produce circular orbits in general.

Papyrus 21 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by siglum

21, is an early copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of Matthew, it contains only Matthew 12:24-26.32-33. The manuscript paleographically had been assigned to the early 3rd century.1
Description
The manuscript is written in large upright uncial letters.2
The Greek text of this codex probably is a mixture of text-types. Aland placed it in Category III.3
In Matthew 12:25 it has textual variant ιδων δε (instead of ειδως δε) in agreement with Codex Bezae, corrector b of the Codex Sinaiticus, 892*, the Latin text of Codex Bezae (itd), k, c, s, copbo.4 In 12:32 it lacks words αυτω ουτε.5
It is currently housed at the Muhlenberg College (Theol. Pap. 3) in Allentown (Pennsylvania).67

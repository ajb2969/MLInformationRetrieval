   Gromov product      Gromov product   In mathematics , the Gromov product is a concept in the theory of metric spaces named after the mathematician Mikhail Gromov . The Gromov product can also be used to define δ -hyperbolic metric spaces in the sense of Gromov.  Definition  Let ( X , d ) be a metric space and let x , y , z ∈ X . In Euclidean space, the Gromov product ( y , z ) x equals the distance between x and the point where the inscribed circle of the triangle xyz touches the edge xy .  Properties   The Gromov product is symmetric: ( y , z ) x = ( z , y ) x .    The Gromov product degenerates at the endpoints: ( y , z ) y = ( y , z ) z = 0. In this case. Gromov product measures how long geodesics remain close together. Namely, if x , y and z are three points of a δ -hyperbolic metric space then the initial segments of length ( y , z ) x of geodesics from x to y and x to z are no further than 2 δ apart (in the sense of the Hausdorff distance between closed sets).

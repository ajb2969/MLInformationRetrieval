Contact number

''see also Kissing number in the sphere packing problem

In chemistry, a contact number (CN) is a simple solvent exposure measure that measures residue burial in proteins.1 The definition of CN varies between authors, but is generally defined as the number of either C β β \beta or C α α \alpha atoms within a sphere around the C β β \beta or C α α \alpha atom of the residue. The radius of the sphere is typically chosen to be between 8 and 14Å.

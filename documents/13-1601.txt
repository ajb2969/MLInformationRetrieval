


Minuscule 623

Minuscule 623 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), α 173 (von Soden),1 is a Greek diglot minuscule manuscript of the New Testament, on parchment. It is dated by a colophon to the year 1037. The manuscript is lacunose.2 Tischendorf labeled it by 156a and 190p.3
Description
The codex contains the text of the Acts of the Apostles, Catholic epistles, Pauline epistles on 187 parchment leaves (size {{×|34.6|27.2}}) with one lacuna (Acts 1:1-5:4). The text is written in two columns per page, 43-44 lines per page.4
The text is divided according to the  (chapters), whose numbers are given at the margin, and the  (titles of chapters) at the top of the pages.5
It contains Prolegomena, tables of the  (tables of contents) before each book, lectionary markings (for liturgical use), subscriptions at the end of each book, , and Euthalian Apparatus. The Pauline epistles have a commentary.67
The order of books: Book of Acts, Catholic epistles, and Pauline epistles. On the list of the Pauline epistles the Hebrews is placed before First Epistle to Timothy.8
Text
The Greek text of the codex Aland placed it in Category III.9
Ending of the Epistle to the Romans has omitted verse 16:24 (as in codices Codex Sinaiticus A B C 5 81 263 1739 1838 1962 2127 itz vgww copsa,bo ethro Origenlat).10
In 1 Corinthians 3:3 it reads ἔρις διχοστασία for ἔρις καί διχοστασίαι (p46, D, 33, 88, 104, 181, 326, 330, 436, 451, 614, 629, 1241). Other manuscripts have reading ἔρις ($\mathfrak{P}$11, א, B, C, P, Ψ, 81, 181, 630, 1739, 1877, 1881, it, vg).11
History
The manuscript was written in the city Reggio for the wish Nicolas Archbishop of Calabria by the cleric Theodore from Sicily.1213 Formerly it was held in Grottaferrata.14
The manuscript was examined by Zacagni, Johann Jakob Wettstein, Johann Jakob Griesbach, and Johann Martin Augustin Scholz. It was added to the list of New Testament manuscripts by Scholz. C. R. Gregory saw the manuscript in 1886.15
Formerly it was labeled by 156a and 190p. In 1908 Gregory gave the number 623 to it.16
The manuscript currently is housed at the Vatican Library (Vat. gr. 1650), at Rome.17

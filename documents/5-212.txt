   Proper transfer function      Proper transfer function   In control theory , a proper transfer function is a transfer function in which the degree of the numerator does not exceed the degree of the denominator.  A strictly proper  transfer function is a transfer function where the degree of the numerator is less than the degree of the denominator.  If the degree of the numerator equals the degree of the denominator, the transfer function is biproper .  The difference between the degree of the denominator (number of poles) and degree of the numerator (number of zeros) is the relative degree of the transfer function.

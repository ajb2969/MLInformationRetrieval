While this case leads to infinitely many solutions of (1) (since we can pick any m for m>5), these solutions only give a single triplet of values ( a m , b n , c k ).  It is known by Faltings' theorem that for any fixed choice of positive integers m , n and k satisfying (2), only finitely many coprime triples ( a , b , c ) solving (1) exist, but of course the full Fermat–Catalan conjecture is a much stronger statement since it allows for an infinitude of sets of exponents m , n and k .

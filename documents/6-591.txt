   Groombridge 34      Groombridge 34   Groombridge 34 is a binary star system located about 11.7 light-years from the Sun . It consists of two red dwarfs in a nearly circular orbit with a separation of about 147 AU . Both stars in this pair exhibit variability due to random flares and they have been given variable star designations. (The brighter member Groombridge 34 A is designated GX And , and the other member is designated GQ And ).  Distance          Planetary system  In August 2014, a planet orbiting around Groombridge 34 A was reported. 1 The planet's existence was deduced from analysis of the radial velocities of the parent Star by the Eta-Earth Survey using HIRES at Keck Observatory .  The planet is thought to have a minimum mass of 5.35 ± 0.75 Earth masses , 2 and at its discovery was the sixth nearest known exoplanet .

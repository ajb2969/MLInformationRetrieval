   Upper-convected Maxwell model      Upper-convected Maxwell model   The upper-convected Maxwell (UCM) model is a generalisation of the Maxwell material for the case of large deformations using the upper-convected time derivative . The model was proposed by James G. Oldroyd . The concept is named after James Clerk Maxwell . Usually quadratic behavior of the first difference of normal stresses and no second difference of the normal stresses is a realistic behavior of polymer melts at moderated shear rates, but constant viscosity is unrealistic and limits usability of the model.

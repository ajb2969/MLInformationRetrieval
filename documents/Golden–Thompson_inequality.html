Golden–Thompson inequality

In physics and mathematics, the Golden–Thompson inequality, proved independently by and , says that for Hermitian matrices A and B,

tr e A + B ≤ tr ( e A e B ) tr superscript e A B tr superscript e A superscript e B \operatorname{tr}\,e^{A+B}\leq\operatorname{tr}\left(e^{A}e^{B}\right)

where tr is the trace, and eA is the matrix exponential. This trace inequality is of particular significance in statistical mechanics, and was first derived in that context.

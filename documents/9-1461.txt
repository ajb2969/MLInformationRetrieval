   Papyrus 115      Papyrus 115   Papyrus 115 ( P. Oxy. 4499, designated by   𝔓   𝔓   \mathfrak{P}    115 in the Gregory-Aland numbering) is a fragmented manuscript of the New Testament written in Greek on papyrus . It consists of 26 fragments of a codex containing parts of the Book of Revelation , and probably nothing more. 1 It dates to the 3rd century, ca. 225-275 AD. 2  Grenfell and Hunt discovered the papyrus in Oxyrhynchus, Egypt .     𝔓   𝔓   \mathfrak{P}    115 was not deciphered and published until the end of the 20th century. It is currently housed at the Ashmolean Museum . 3  Description  The original codex had 33-36 lines per page of 15.5 cm by 23.5 cm. The surviving text includes Revelation 2:1-3, 13-15, 27-29; 3:10-12; 5:8-9; 6:5-6; 8:3-8, 11-13; 9:1-5, 7-16, 18-21; 10:1-4, 8-11; 11:1-5, 8-15, 18-19; 12:1-5, 8-10, 12-17; 13:1-3, 6-16, 18; 14:1-3, 5-7, 10-11, 14-15, 18-20; 15:1, 4-7. 4  The manuscript has evidence of the following nomina sacra :     Ι  Η  Λ   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Ι  normal-Η  normal-Λ     \overline{ΙΗΛ}   ,     Α  Υ  Τ  Ο  Υ   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Α  normal-Υ  normal-Τ  normal-Ο  normal-Υ     \overline{ΑΥΤΟΥ}   ,     Π  Ρ  Σ   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Π  normal-Ρ  normal-Σ     \overline{ΠΡΣ}   ,     Θ  Ω   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Θ  normal-Ω     \overline{ΘΩ}   ,     Θ  Υ   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Θ  normal-Υ     \overline{ΘΥ}   ,     Α  Ν  Ω  Ν   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Α  normal-Ν  normal-Ω  normal-Ν     \overline{ΑΝΩΝ}   ,     Π  Ν  Α   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Π  normal-Ν  normal-Α     \overline{ΠΝΑ}   ,     Ο  Υ  Ν  Ο  Υ   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Ο  normal-Υ  normal-Ν  normal-Ο  normal-Υ     \overline{ΟΥΝΟΥ}   ,     Ο  Υ  Ν  Ο  Ν   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Ο  normal-Υ  normal-Ν  normal-Ο  normal-Ν     \overline{ΟΥΝΟΝ}   ,     Κ  Υ   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Κ  normal-Υ     \overline{ΚΥ}   ,     Θ  Ν   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Θ  normal-Ν     \overline{ΘΝ}   ,     Α  Ν  Ο  Υ   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Α  normal-Ν  normal-Ο  normal-Υ     \overline{ΑΝΟΥ}   ,     Ο  Υ  Ν  Ω   ¯     normal-¯    normal-Ο  normal-Υ  normal-Ν  normal-Ω     \overline{ΟΥΝΩ}   .

Fannes–Audenaert inequality

The Fannes–Audenaert inequality is a mathematical bound on the difference between the von Neumann entropies of two density matrices as a function of their trace distance. It was proved by Koenraad M. R. Audenaert in 20071 as an optimal refinement of Mark Fannes' original inequality, which was published in 1973.2 Mark Fannes is a Belgian physicist specialised in mathematical quantum mechanics. He works at the KU Leuven.

Fannes' inequality and Audenaert's refinement

The original inequality proved by Fannes was

| S ( ρ ) - S ( σ ) | ≤ 2 T log ( d ) - 2 T log 2 T S ρ S σ 2 T d 2 T 2 T |S(\rho)-S(\sigma)|\leq 2T\log(d)-2T\log 2T

when T ≤ 1 / 2 e T 1 2 e T\leq 1/2e . He also proved the weaker inequality

| S ( ρ ) - S ( σ ) | ≤ 2 T log ( d ) + 1 / ( e log 2 ) S ρ S σ 2 T d 1 e 2 |S(\rho)-S(\sigma)|\leq 2T\log(d)+1/(e\log 2)

which can be used for larger T.

Fannes proved this inequality as a means to prove the continuity of the von Neumann entropy, which did not require an optimal bound. The proof is very compact, and can be found in the textbook by Nielsen and Chuang.3 Audenaert's proof of the optimal inequality, on the other hand, is significantly more complicated.

Cocurvature

In mathematics in the branch of differential geometry, the cocurvature of a connection on a manifold is the obstruction to the integrability of the vertical bundle.

Definition

If M is a manifold and P is a connection on M, that is a vector-valued 1-form on M which is a projection on TM such that PabPbc = Pac, then the cocurvature R ¯ P subscript normal-¯ R P \bar{R}_{P} is a vector-valued 2-form on M defined by

R ¯ P ( X , Y ) = ( Id - P ) [ P X , P Y ] subscript normal-¯ R P X Y Id P P X P Y \bar{R}_{P}(X,Y)=(\operatorname{Id}-P)[PX,PY]

where X and Y are vector fields on M.

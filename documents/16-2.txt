It may seem strange to consider only real eigenvalues. After all, it is widely known in linear algebra that the eigenvalues of a matrix with all real entries can have complex eigenvalues. In geometric algebra, however, the blades of different orders inherit the complex structure. Since both vectors and pseudovectors can act as eigenblades, they may each have a set of eigenvalues matching the degrees of freedom of the complex eigenvalues that would be found in ordinary linear algebra.

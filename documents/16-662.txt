   Draft:Kane's Method      Draft:Kane's Method      Kane's method is developed by Thomas R. Kane for analyzing complex dynamics systems (originally called Lagrange form of d’Alembert’s principle). 1 This method is based on Newtonian dynamics and consequently is a vectorial method. Despite being based on Newtonian mechanics, it prevents reaction forces and unwanted torques to appear in equations of motion, therefore can produce simple dynamical equations even for multi DOF systems. One example of multi DOF systems is a robot. Using this method can eliminate difficulties and limitations related to arm dynamics. 2  3  4  5 This method focuses on motion of the system rather than general coordinates by introducing the concept of partial velocities 6  7 . Before giving the formal definition of partial velocities, first we need to review the concept of general coordinates. 8 9  General Coordinates  If we have a set of particles "   s   s   s   ", then the minimum set of parameters which completely specify the configuration of the system are called general coordinates denoted by    q  i     subscript  q  i    q_{i}   .

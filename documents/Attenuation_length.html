Attenuation length

In physics, the attenuation length or absorption length is the distance λ λ \lambda into a material when the probability has dropped to 1 / e 1 e 1/e that a particle has not been absorbed. Alternatively, if there is a beam of particles incident on the material, the attenuation length is the distance where the intensity of the beam has dropped to 1 / e 1 e 1/e , or about 63% of the particles have been stopped.

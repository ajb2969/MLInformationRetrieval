Isoenthalpic–isobaric ensemble

The isoenthalpic-isobaric ensemble (constant enthalpy and constant pressure ensemble) is a statistical mechanical ensemble that maintains constant enthalpy H H H\, and constant pressure P P P\, applied. It is also called the N p H N p H NpH -ensemble, where the number of particles N N N\, is also kept as a constant. It was developed by physicist H. C. Andersen in 1980.1 The ensemble adds another degree of freedom, which represents the variable volume V V V\, of a system to which the coordinates of all particles are relative. The volume V V V\, becomes a dynamical variable with potential energy and kinetic energy given by P V P V PV\, .2 The enthalpy H = E + P V H E P V H=E+PV\, is a conserved quantity.3

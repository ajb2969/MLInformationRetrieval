Conventions  There are several choices of signature and representation that are in common use in the physics literature. The Dirac matrices are typically written as    γ  μ     superscript  γ  μ    \scriptstyle\gamma^{\mu}   where   μ   μ   \scriptstyle\mu   runs from 0 to 3. In this notation, 0 corresponds to time, and 1 through 3 correspond to x, y, and z.  The + − − − signature is sometimes called the west coast metric, while the − + + + is the east coast metric. At this time the + − − − signature is in more common use, and our example will use this signature. To switch from one example to the other, multiply all    γ  μ     superscript  γ  μ    \scriptstyle\gamma^{\mu}   by   i   i   \scriptstyle i   .  After choosing the signature, there are many ways of constructing a representation in the 4×4 matrices, and many are in common use. In order to make this example as general as possible we will not specify a representation until the final step.

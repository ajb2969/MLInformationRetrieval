Euler–Poisson–Darboux equation

In mathematics, the Euler–Poisson–Darboux1 equation is the partial differential equation

u x , y + N ( u x + u y ) x + y = 0. subscript u x y N subscript u x subscript u y x y 0. u_{x,y}+\frac{N(u_{x}+u_{y})}{x+y}=0.

This equation is named for Siméon Poisson, Leonhard Euler, and Gaston Darboux. It plays important role in solving the classical wave equation.

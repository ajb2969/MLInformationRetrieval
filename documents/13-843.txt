The abscissa , line and half-plane of convergence of a Dirichlet series are analogous to radius , boundary and disk of convergence of a power series .  On the line of convergence, the question of convergence remains open as in the case of power series. However, if a Dirichlet series converges and diverges at different points on the same vertical line, then this line must be the line of convergence. The proof is implicit in the definition of abscissa of convergence.

Argument of latitude

In celestial mechanics, the argument of latitude ( u u u ) is an angular parameter that defines the position of a body moving along a Kepler orbit. It is the angle between the ascending node and the body.

It is the sum of the more commonly used true anomaly and argument of periapsis.

u = ν + ω u ν ω u=\nu+\omega

where u u u is the argument of latitude, ν ν \nu the true anomaly, and ω ω \omega the argument of periapsis.

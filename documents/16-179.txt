A GIT quotient may or may not be a geometric quotient: but both are categorical quotients, which is unique; in other words, one cannot have both types of quotients (without them being the same).  Relation to other quotients  A geometric quotient is a categorical quotient . This is proved in Mumford's geometric invariant theory.  A geometric quotient is precisely a good quotient whose fibers are orbits of the group.

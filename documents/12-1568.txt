   Papyrus 95      Papyrus 95   Papyrus 95 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by siglum    𝔓   𝔓   \mathfrak{P}    95 , is an early copy of the New Testament in Greek . It is a papyrus  manuscript of the Gospel of John . The surviving texts of John are verses 5:26-29,36-38. The manuscript palaeographically has been assigned to the early 3rd century. 1  The writing is in 35 lines per page. 2  Text  The Greek text of this manuscript is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type , Comfort ascribed it as proto-Alexandrian, though the extant portion is too fragmentary for certainty. 3 It has not yet been placed in any of the Categories of New Testament manuscripts . 4   Location   The manuscript is currently housed at the Biblioteca Medicea Laurenziana (PL II/31) at Florence . 5 6  Textual Variants   5:28: ακουσουσιν (shall hear (future tense)) becomes ακουσαντες (have heard (aorist tense, participle)).    5:36: omits second αυτα (these) through possible homoeoteleuton .

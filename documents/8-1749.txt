   Cylindric algebra      Cylindric algebra   The notion of cylindric algebra , invented by Alfred Tarski , arises naturally in the algebraization of first-order logic with equality . This is comparable to the role Boolean algebras play for propositional logic . Indeed, cylindric algebras are Boolean algebras equipped with additional cylindrification operations that model quantification and equality. They differ from polyadic algebras in that the latter do not model equality.

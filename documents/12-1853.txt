   Pettis integral      Pettis integral   In mathematics , the Pettis integral or Gelfand–Pettis integral , named after I. M. Gelfand and B. J. Pettis , extends the definition of the Lebesgue integral to vector-valued functions on a measure space , by exploiting duality . The integral was introduced by Gelfand for the case when the measure space is an interval with Lebesgue measure . The integral is also called the weak integral in contrast to the Bochner integral , which is the strong integral.

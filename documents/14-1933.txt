   Generalized Clifford algebra      Generalized Clifford algebra   In mathematics , a Generalized Clifford algebra (GCA) is an associative algebra that generalizes the Clifford algebra , and goes back to the work of Hermann Weyl , 1 who utilized and formalized these clock-and-shift operators introduced by J. J. Sylvester (1882), 2 and organized by Cartan (1898) 3 and Schwinger . 4  Clock and shift matrices find routine applications in numerous areas of mathematical physics, providing the cornerstone of quantum mechanical dynamics in finite-dimensional vector spaces . 5 6 7 The concept of a spinor can further be linked to these algebras. 8  The term Generalized Clifford Algebras can also refer to associative algebras that are constructed using forms of higher degree instead of quadratic forms. 9 10 11 12  Definition and properties  Abstract definition  The   n   n   n   -dimensional generalized Clifford algebra is defined as an associative algebra over a field   F   F   F   , generated by 13        e  j    e  k    =    ω   j  k     e  k     e  j            subscript  e  j    subscript  e  k       subscript  ω    j  k     subscript  e  k    subscript  e  j      e_{j}e_{k}=\omega_{jk}e_{k}e_{j}\,           ω   j  k     e  l    =    e  l     ω   j  k             subscript  ω    j  k     subscript  e  l       subscript  e  l    subscript  ω    j  k       \omega_{jk}e_{l}=e_{l}\omega_{jk}\,           ω   j  k     ω   l  m     =    ω   l  m      ω   j  k             subscript  ω    j  k     subscript  ω    l  m        subscript  ω    l  m     subscript  ω    j  k       \omega_{jk}\omega_{lm}=\omega_{lm}\omega_{jk}\,   and       e  j   N  j    =  1  =   ω   j  k    N  j    =    ω   j  k    N  k            superscript   subscript  e  j    subscript  N  j    1        superscript   subscript  ω    j  k     subscript  N  j          superscript   subscript  ω    j  k     subscript  N  k       e_{j}^{N_{j}}=1=\omega_{jk}^{N_{j}}=\omega_{jk}^{N_{k}}\,          ∀  j   ,  k  ,  l  ,  m   =  1   ,   …  ,  n      formulae-sequence       normal-∀  j   k  l  m   1    normal-…  n     ∀j,k,l,m=1,...,n   .

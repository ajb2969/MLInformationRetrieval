   Cook's distance      Cook's distance  In [[statistics]], '''Cook's distance''' or '''Cook's ''D''''' is a commonly used e stimate of the influence of a data point when performing least squares regression analysis . 1 In a practical ordinary least squares analysis, Cook's distance can be used in several ways: to indicate data points that are particularly worth checking for validity; to indicate regions of the design space where it would be good to be able to obtain more data points. It is named after the American statistician R. Dennis Cook , who introduced the concept in 1977. 2 3  Definition  Cook's distance measures the effect of deleting a given observation. Data points with large residuals ( outliers ) and/or high leverage may distort the outcome and accuracy of a regression. Points with a large Cook's distance are considered to merit closer examination in the analysis.

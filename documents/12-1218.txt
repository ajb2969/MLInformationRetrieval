   Multiplicative character      Multiplicative character   A multiplicative character (or linear character , or simply character ) on a group G is a group homomorphism from G to the multiplicative group of a field , usually the field of complex numbers . If G is any group, then the set Ch( G ) of these morphisms forms an abelian group under pointwise multiplication.  This group is referred to as the character group of G . Sometimes only unitary characters are considered (thus the image is in the unit circle ); other such homomorphisms are then called quasi-characters . Dirichlet characters can be seen as a special case of this definition.

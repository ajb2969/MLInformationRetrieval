Secondary supertonic chord

In music theory, the secondary supertonic chord or secondary second, is a secondary chord, but rather than being on the dominant it is on the supertonic scale degree and rather than tonicizing a degree other than the tonic, as does a secondary dominant, it creates a temporary dominant.1 Thus the progression i i V i i V \frac{ii}{V} - V V V V \frac{V}{V} - V ( I ) V I \frac{V}{(I)} .

Examples include ii7/III (Fmin.7, in C major).2

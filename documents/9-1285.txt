   List of space groups      List of space groups   There are 230 space groups in three dimensions, given by a number index, and a full name in Hermann–Mauguin notation , and a short name (international short symbol). The long names are given with spaces for readability. The groups each have a point groups of the unit cell.  Symbols  In Hermann–Mauguin notation , space groups are named by a symbol combining the point group identifier with the uppercase letters describing the lattice type . Translations within the lattice in the form of screw axes and glide planes are also noted, giving a complete crystallographic space group.  These are the Bravais lattices in three dimensions:   P primitive  I body centered (from the German "Innenzentriert")  F face centered (from the German "Flächenzentriert")  A centered on A faces only  B centered on B faces only  C centered on C faces only  R rhombohedral   A reflection plane m within the point groups can be replaced by a glide plane , labeled as a , b , or c depending on which axis the glide is along. There is also the n glide, which is a glide along the half of a diagonal of a face, and the d glide, which is along a quarter of either a face or space diagonal of the unit cell. The d glide is often called the diamond glide plane as it features in the diamond structure.      a   a   a   ,   b   b   b   , or   c   c   c   glide translation along half the lattice vector of this face     n   n   n   glide translation along with half a face diagonal     d   d   d   glide planes with translation along a quarter of a face diagonal.     e   e   e   two glides with the same glide plane and translation along two (different) half-lattice vectors.   A gyration point can be replaced by a screw axis is noted by a number, n , where the angle of rotation is     \color   B  l  a  c  k    360  ∘   n       \color  B  l  a  c  k     superscript  360    n     \color{Black}\tfrac{360^{\circ}}{n}   . The degree of translation is then added as a subscript showing how far along the axis the translation is, as a portion of the parallel lattice vector. For example, 2 1 is a 180° (twofold) rotation followed by a translation of ½ of the lattice vector. 3 1 is a 120° (threefold) rotation followed by a translation of ⅓ of the lattice vector.  The possible screw axis are: 2 1 , 3 1 , 3 2 , 4 1 , 4 2 , 4 3 , 6 1 , 6 2 , 6 3 , 6 4 , and 6 5 .

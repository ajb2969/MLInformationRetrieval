The importance of this for finance, is that option pricing problems, when extended beyond the standard assumptions (e.g. incorporating changing dividends), cannot be solved in closed form, but can be solved using this method. Note however, that for non-smooth final conditions (which happen for most financial instruments), the Crankâ€“Nicolson method is not satisfactory as numerical oscillations are not damped. For vanilla options , this results in oscillation in the gamma value around the strike price . Therefore, special damping initialization steps are necessary (e.g., fully implicit finite difference method).

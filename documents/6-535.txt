   Rogers–Ramanujan identities      Rogers–Ramanujan identities   In mathematics , the Rogers–Ramanujan identities are two identities related to basic hypergeometric series , first discovered and proved by . They were subsequently rediscovered (without a proof) by Srinivasa Ramanujan some time before 193. Ramanujan had no proof, but rediscovered Rogers's paper in 1917, and they then published a joint new proof .  independently rediscovered and proved the identities.

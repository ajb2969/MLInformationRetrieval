   Schwinger variational principle      Schwinger variational principle   Schwinger variational principle is a variational principle which expresses the scattering T-matrix as a functional depending on two unknown wave functions . The functional attains stationary value equal to actual scattering T-matrix. The functional is stationary if and only if the two functions satisfy the Lippmann-Schwinger equation . The development of the variational formulation of the scattering theory can be traced to works of L. Hultén and J.

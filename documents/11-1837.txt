The CIE 1960 color space ("CIE 1960 UCS", variously expanded Uniform Color Space , Uniform Color Scale , Uniform Chromaticity Scale , Uniform Chromaticity Space ) is another name for the chromaticity space devised by David MacAdam . 1  The CIE 1960 UCS does not define a luminance or lightness component, but the Y  tristimulus value of the XYZ color space or a lightness index similar to W * of the CIE 1964 color space are sometimes used. 2  Today, the CIE 1960 UCS is mostly used to calculate correlated color temperature , where the isothermal lines are perpendicular to the Planckian locus . As a uniform chromaticity space, it has been superseded by the CIE 1976 UCS .

Noise-equivalent flux density

In optics the noise-equivalent flux density (NEFD) or noise-equivalent irradiance (NEI) of a system is the level of flux density required to be equivalent to the noise present in the system.1 It is a measure used by astronomers in determining the accuracy of observations.2

The NEFD can be related to a light detector's Noise-equivalent power for a collection area A and a photon bandwidth ν ν \nu by:

N E F D = η N E P A ν N E F D η N E P A ν NEFD=\eta\frac{NEP}{A\nu} , where a factor η η \eta (often 2, in the case of switching between measuring a source and measuring off-source) accounts for the photon statistics for the mode of operation.

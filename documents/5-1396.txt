Like the Laplacian, the Laplaceâ€“Beltrami operator is defined as the divergence of the gradient , and is a linear operator taking functions into functions. The operator can be extended to operate on tensors as the divergence of the covariant derivative . Alternatively, the operator can be generalized to operate on differential forms using the divergence and exterior derivative . The resulting operator is called the Laplaceâ€“de Rham operator (named after Georges de Rham ).

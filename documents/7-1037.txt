Remark: It is not reasonable to define formally quadrics for "vector spaces" (strictly speaking, modules ) over genuine skew fields (division rings). Because one would get secants bearing more than 2 points of the quadric which is totally different from usual quadrics. The reason is the following statement. There are generalizations of quadrics: quadratic sets . A quadratic set is a set of points of a projective plane/space, which bears the same geometric properties as a quadric: any line intersects a quadratic set in no or 1 or two lines or is containt in the set.

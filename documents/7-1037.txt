   Quadric (projective geometry)      Quadric (projective geometry)   In projective geometry , a quadric is the set of points of a projective space where a certain quadratic form on the homogeneous coordinates becomes zero. We shall restrict ourself to the case of finite-dimensional projective spaces . Remark: It is not reasonable to define formally quadrics for "vector spaces" (strictly speaking, modules ) over genuine skew fields (division rings). Because one would get secants bearing more than 2 points of the quadric which is totally different from usual quadrics. The reason is the following statement.  Theorem: A division ring    K   K   K   is commutative if and only if any equation       x  2   +   a  x   +  b   =   0  ,  a    ,   b  ∈  K      formulae-sequence       superscript  x  2     a  x   b    0  a      b  K     x^{2}+ax+b=0,\ a,b\in K    has at most two solutions.  There are generalizations of quadrics: quadratic sets . A quadratic set is a set of points of a projective plane/space, which bears the same geometric properties as a quadric: any line intersects a quadratic set in no or 1 or two lines or is containt in the set.

Papyrus 105

Papyrus 105 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 105, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of Matthew. The surviving texts of Matthew are verses 27:62-64; 28:2-5, they are in a fragmentary condition. The manuscript has been paleographically estimated to date back to the 5th or 6th century CE.1

Text

The Greek text of the codex is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type.

Location

The manuscript is currently housed at the Sackler Library (Papyrology Rooms, P. Oxy. 4406) at Oxford.2

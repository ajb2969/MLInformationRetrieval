Kleinian integer

In mathematical cryptography, a Kleinian integer is a complex number of the form m + n 1 + - 7 2 m n 1 7 2 m+n\frac{1+\sqrt{-7}}{2} , with m and n rational integers. They are named after Felix Klein.

The Kleinian integers form a ring called the Kleinian ring, which is the ring of integers in the imaginary quadratic field ℚ ( - 7 ) ℚ 7 \mathbb{Q}(-7) . This ring is a unique factorization domain.

   Tschirnhausen cubic      Tschirnhausen cubic   In geometry , the Tschirnhausen cubic , or Tschirnhaus' cubic is a plane curve defined by the polar equation       r  =   a    sec  3    (   θ  /  3   )      .      r    a    superscript   3     θ  3       r=a\sec^{3}(\theta/3).     History  The curve was studied by von Tschirnhaus , de L'Hôpital , and Catalan . It was given the name Tschirnhausen cubic in a 1900 paper by R C Archibald, though it is sometimes known as de L'Hôpital's cubic or the trisectrix of Catalan.

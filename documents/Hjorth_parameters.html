Hjorth parameters

Hjorth Parameters are indicators of statistical properties used in signal processing in the time domain introduced by Bo Hjorth in 1970.1 The parameters are Activity, Mobility, and Complexity. They are commonly used in the analysis of electroencephalography signals for feature extraction.2 The Hjorth parameters are normalized slope descriptors (NSDs) usually used in sleep EEG processing for data reducing and/or automatic sleep stage scoring.3

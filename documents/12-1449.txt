Papyrus 36

Papyrus 36 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by siglum 

36, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of John, it contains only John 3:14-18.31-32.34-35. The manuscript palaeographically has been assigned to the 6th century.1
The Greek text of this codex is an eclectic. Aland placed it in Category III.2
The manuscript was examined by Pistelli, Carlini, and Horseley.
It is currently housed at the Laurentian Library (PSI 3) in Florence.34

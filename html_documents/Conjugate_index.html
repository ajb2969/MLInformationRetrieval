Conjugate index

In mathematics, two real numbers p , q > 1 p q 1 p,q>1 are called conjugate indices if

1 p + 1 q = 1. 1 p 1 q 1. \frac{1}{p}+\frac{1}{q}=1.

Formally, we will also define q = ∞ q q=\infty as conjugate to p = 1 p 1 p=1 and vice versa.

Conjugate indices are used in Hölder's inequality. Also, if p , q > 1 p q 1 p,q>1 are conjugate indices, the spaces Lp and Lq are dual to each other (see Lp space).

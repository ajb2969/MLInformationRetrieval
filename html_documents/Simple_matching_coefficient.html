Simple matching coefficient

The Simple Matching Coefficient (SMC) is a statistic used for comparing the similarity and diversity of sample sets.1

Given two objects, A and B, each with n binary attributes, SMC is defined as:

Where:

M 11 subscript M 11 M_{11} represents the total number of attributes where A and B both have a value of 1.

M 01 subscript M 01 M_{01} represents the total number of attributes where the attribute of A is 0 and the attribute of B is 1.

M 10 subscript M 10 M_{10} represents the total number of attributes where the attribute of A is 1 and the attribute of B is 0.

M 00 subscript M 00 M_{00} represents the total number of attributes where A and B both have a value of 0.

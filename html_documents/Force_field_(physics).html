Force field (physics)

In physics a force field is a vector field that describes a non-contact force acting on a particle at various positions in space. Specifically, a force field is a vector field F → ( x → ) normal-→ F normal-→ x \vec{F}(\vec{x}) , where F → normal-→ F \vec{F} is the force that a particle would feel if it were at the point x → normal-→ x \vec{x} .1

The gravitational force experienced by a particle of mass m is given by F → = m g → normal-→ F m normal-→ g \vec{F}=m\vec{g} . 23
  • An electric field E → normal-→ E \vec{E} is a vector field. It exerts a force on a point charge q given by F → = q E → normal-→ F q normal-→ E \vec{F}=q\vec{E} . 4
  • a gravitational force field is a model used to explain the influence that a massive body extends into the space around itself, producing a force on another massive body.,5

Restriction to position-dependent forces

Some forces, including friction, air drag, and the magnetic force on a charged particle, depend on the particle's velocity as well as its position. Therefore these forces are not characterized by a force field.

Work done by a force field

As a particle moves through a force field along a path C, the work done by the force is a line integral

For a conservative force field, it is also independent of the path itself, but depends only on the starting and ending points.

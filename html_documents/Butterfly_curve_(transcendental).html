Butterfly curve (transcendental)

The butterfly curve is a transcendental plane curve discovered by Temple H. Fay. The curve is given by the following parametric equations:

x = sin ( t ) ( e cos ( t ) - 2 cos ( 4 t ) - sin 5 ( t 12 ) ) x t superscript e t 2 4 t superscript 5 t 12 x=\sin(t)\left(e^{\cos(t)}-2\cos(4t)-\sin^{5}\left({t\over 12}\right)\right)

y = cos ( t ) ( e cos ( t ) - 2 cos ( 4 t ) - sin 5 ( t 12 ) ) y t superscript e t 2 4 t superscript 5 t 12 y=\cos(t)\left(e^{\cos(t)}-2\cos(4t)-\sin^{5}\left({t\over 12}\right)\right)

or by the following polar equation:

r = e sin θ - 2 cos ( 4 θ ) + sin 5 ( 2 θ - π 24 ) r superscript e θ 2 4 θ superscript 5 2 θ π 24 r=e^{\sin\theta}-2\cos(4\theta)+\sin^{5}\left(\frac{2\theta-\pi}{24}\right)

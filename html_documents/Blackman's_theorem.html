Blackman's theorem

Blackman's theorem is a general procedure for calculating the change in an impedance due to feedback in a circuit. It was published by R.B. Blackman in 1943,1 was connected to signal-flow analysis by John Choma, and was made popular in the extra element theorem by R. D. Middlebrook and the asymptotic gain model of Solomon Rosenstark.2345 Blackman's approach leads to the formula for the impedance Z between two selected terminals of a negative feedback amplifier as Blackman's formula:

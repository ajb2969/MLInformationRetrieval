Shift space

In symbolic dynamics and related branches of mathematics, a shift space or subshift is a set of infinite words that represent the evolution of a discrete system. In fact, shift spaces and symbolic dynamical systems are often considered synonyms.

Characterization and sofic subshifts

A subset S of A ‚Ñï superscript A ‚Ñï A^{\mathbb{N}} is a shift space if and only if there exists a set X of finite words such that S coincides with the set of all infinite words over A having no factor in X.

In particular, if X is finite then S is called a subshift of finite type and more generally when X is a regular language, the corresponding subshift is called sofic.

Infinite arithmetic series

In mathematics, an infinite arithmetic series is an infinite series whose terms are in an arithmetic progression. Examples are and . The general form for an infinite arithmetic series is

∑ n = 0 ∞ ( a n + b ) . superscript subscript n 0 a n b \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(an+b).

If a = b = 0, then the sum of the series is 0. If either a or b is nonzero while the other is, then the series diverges and has no sum in the usual sense.

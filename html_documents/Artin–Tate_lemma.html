Artin–Tate lemma

In algebra, the Artin–Tate lemma states:1

Let A be a Noetherian ring and B \sub C B \sub C B\sub C algebras over A. If C is of finite type over A and if C is finite over B, then B is of finite type over A.

(Here, "of finite type" means "finitely generated algebra" and "finite" means "finitely generated module".) The lemma was introduced by E. Artin and J. Tate in 19512 to give a proof of Hilbert's Nullstellensatz.

Papyrus 3

Papyrus 3, designated by 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 3 (in the numbering Gregory-Aland), is a small fragment of fifteen verses from the Gospel of Luke dating to the 6th/7th century.1 It is formed part of a lectionary. It is dated palaeographically to the 6th or 7th century.2

The Greek text-type of this codex is a mixed. Aland placed it in Category III.3

History

The text of the manuscript was published by Karl Wessely in 1882.4

The manuscript is housed at the Austrian National Library (Pap. G. 2323).5

The axiom of choice implies that every set can be wellordered, and therefore that every subset of every Polish space is ‚àû-Borel. Therefore the notion is interesting only in contexts where AC does not hold (or is not known to hold). Unfortunately, without the axiom of choice, it is not clear that the ‚àû-Borel sets are closed under wellordered union. This is because, given a wellordered union of ‚àû-Borel sets, each of the individual sets may have many ‚àû-Borel codes, and there may be no way to choose one code for each of the sets, with which to form the code for the union.

The assumption that every set of reals is ‚àû-Borel is part of AD+, an extension of the axiom of determinacy studied by Woodin.

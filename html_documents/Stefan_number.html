Stefan number

The Stefan number1 (St or Ste) is defined as the ratio of sensible heat to latent heat. It is given by the formula

Ste = c p Δ T L , Ste subscript c p normal-Δ T L \mathrm{Ste}=\frac{c_{p}\Delta T}{L},

where

  • cp is the specific heat,
  • ∆T is the temperature difference between phases,
  • L is the latent heat of melting.

It is a dimensionless parameter that is useful in analyzing a Stefan problem. The parameter was developed from Josef Stefan's calculations of the rate of phase change of water into ice on the polar ice caps.

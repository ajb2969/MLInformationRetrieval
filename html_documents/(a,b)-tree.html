(a,b)-tree

In computer science, an (a,b) tree is a kind of balanced search tree.

An (a,b)-tree has all of its leaves at the same depth, and all internal nodes except for the root have between a a a and b b b children, where a a a and b b b are integers such that 2 ≤ a ≤ ( b + 1 ) / 2 2 normal-≤ a normal-≤ b 1 2 2≤a≤(b+1)/2 . The root has, if it is not a leaf, between 2 and b b b children.

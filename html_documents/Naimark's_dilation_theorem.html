Naimark's dilation theorem

In operator theory, Naimark's dilation theorem is a result that characterizes positive operator valued measures. It can be viewed as a consequence of Stinespring's dilation theorem.

Note

In the mathematical literature, one may also find other results that bear Naimark's name.

Spelling

In the physics literature, it is common to see the spelling "Neumark" instead of "Naimark." This is due to translating between the Russian alphabet spelling and the spelling in European languages (namely English and German) using the Roman alphabet.

The theorem reads as follows: Let E be a positive L(H)-valued measure on X. There exists a Hilbert space K, a bounded operator V : K → H normal-: V normal-→ K H V:K\rightarrow H , and a self-adjoint, spectral L(K)-valued measure on X, F, such that

Proof

We now sketch the proof. The argument passes E to the induced map Φ E subscript normal-Φ E \Phi_{E} and uses Stinespring's dilation theorem. Since E is positive, so is Φ E subscript normal-Φ E \Phi_{E} as a map between C*-algebras, as explained above. Furthermore, because the domain of Φ E subscript normal-Φ E \Phi_{E} , C(X), is an abelian C*-algebra, we have that Φ E subscript normal-Φ E \Phi_{E} is completely positive. By Stinespring's result, there exists a Hilbert space K, a *-homomorphism π : C ( X ) → L ( K ) normal-: π normal-→ C X L K \pi:C(X)\rightarrow L(K) , and operator V : K → H normal-: V normal-→ K H V:K\rightarrow H such that

Φ E ( f ) = V π ( f ) V * . subscript normal-Φ E f V π f superscript V \;\Phi_{E}(f)=V\pi(f)V^{*}.

Since π is a *-homomorphism, its corresponding operator-valued measure F is spectral and self adjoint. It is easily seen that F has the desired properties.

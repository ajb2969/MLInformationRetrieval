Papyrus 4

Papyrus 4 ( ğ”“ ğ”“ \mathfrak{P} 4, part of Suppl. Gr. 1120) is an early New Testament papyrus of the Gospel of Luke in Greek. It is dated as being a late 2nd/early 3rd century manuscript.

Description

It is one of the earliest manuscripts (along with P75)1 of the Gospel of Luke and contains extensive sections of its first six chapters.2 It is currently housed in the BibliothÃ¨que nationale de France (Suppl. Gr. 1120) in Paris.

It contains texts of Luke: 1:58-59; 1:62-2:1; 2:6-7; 3:8-4:2; 4:29-32, 34-35; 5:3-8; 5:30-6:16

The Greek text-type of this codex is a representative of the Alexandrian. Aland placed it in Category I.3 There is agreement with Papyrus 75 in 93%.4

P4 was used as stuffing for the binding of "a codex of Philo, written in the later third century and found in a jar which had been walled up in a house at Coptos [in 250]."6

Philip Comfort and David Barret in their book Text of the Earliest NT Greek Manuscripts argue that P4 came from the same codex as P64/67, the Magdalen papyrus, and date the texts to 150-175.7 Willker tentatively agrees stating 'The [3rd century] dating given is that of NA. Some date it into the 2nd CE (e.g. Roberts and Comfort). This is quite probable considering the use as binding material for a 3rd CE codex'.8 Comfort and Barret also show that ğ”“ ğ”“ \mathfrak{P} 4 and [[Magdalen papyrus|P64+67]] have affinities with a number of late 2nd century papyri.9 Roberts (1979), Skeat (1997),10 Willker11 and Stanton12 also date the text to the late 2nd century, leading Gregory to conclude that '[t]here is good reason to believe that P4 ... may have been written late in the 2nd century...'.13 Most recently Charlesworth has concluded 'that P64+67 and P4, though written by the same scribe, are not from the same ... codex.'14

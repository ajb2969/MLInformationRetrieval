Since identities involving q-Pochhammer symbols so frequently involve products of many symbols, the standard convention is to write a product as a single symbol of multiple arguments:

q-series

A q-series is a series in which the coefficients are functions of q, typically expressions of ( a ; q ) n subscript a q n (a;q)_{n} .1 Early results are due to Euler, Gauss, and Cauchy. The systematic study begins with E. Heinle (1943).2

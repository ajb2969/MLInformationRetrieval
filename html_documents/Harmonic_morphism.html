Harmonic morphisms are therefore solutions to non-linear over-determined systems of partial differential equations, determined by the geometric data of the manifolds involved. For this reason, they are difficult to find and have no general existence theory, not even locally.

Complex Analysis

Minimal Surfaces

In differential geometry, one is interested in constructing minimal submanifolds of a given ambient space ( M , g ) M g (M,g) . Harmonic morphisms are useful tools for this purpose. This is due to the fact that every regular fibre ϕ - 1 ( { z 0 } ) superscript ϕ 1 subscript z 0 \phi^{-1}(\{z_{0}\}) of such a map ϕ : ( M , g ) → ( N 2 , h ) normal-: ϕ normal-→ M g superscript N 2 h \phi:(M,g)\to(N^{2},h) ,with values in a surface, is a minimal submanifold, of the domain, of codimension 2.3 This gives an attractive method for manifacturing whole families of minimal surfaces in 4-dimensional manifolds ( M 4 , g ) superscript M 4 g (M^{4},g) , in particular, homogeneous spaces, such as Lie groups and symmetric spaces.

Examples

  • Identity and constant maps are harmonic morphisms.
  • Holomorphic functions in the complex plane are harmonic morphisms.
  • Holomorphic functions in the complex vector space ℂ n superscript ℂ n \mathbb{C}^{n} are harmonic morphisms.
  • Holomorphic maps from Kähler manifolds with values in a Riemann surface are harmonic morphisms.
  • The Hopf maps ϕ : S 3 → S 2 normal-: ϕ normal-→ superscript S 3 superscript S 2 \phi:S^{3}\to S^{2} , ϕ : S 7 → S 4 normal-: ϕ normal-→ superscript S 7 superscript S 4 \phi:S^{7}\to S^{4} and ϕ : S 15 → S 8 normal-: ϕ normal-→ superscript S 15 superscript S 8 \phi:S^{15}\to S^{8} are harmonic morphisms.
  • For compact Lie groups K ⊂ H ⊂ G K H G K\subset H\subset G the standard Riemannian fibration ϕ : G / H → G / K normal-: ϕ normal-→ G H G K \phi:G/H\to G/K is a harmonic morphism.
  • Riemannian submersions with minimal fibres are harmonic morphisms.

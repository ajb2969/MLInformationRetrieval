Papyrus 98

Papyrus 98 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by ğ”“ ğ”“ \mathfrak{P} 98, is an early copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Book of Revelation. The manuscript palaeographically had been assigned to "100-200 (?)." 12

Description

The surviving text of Revelation are verses 1:13-2:1 in a fragmentary condition. The script is a well-formed and large.3 It was formed in a scroll. The biblical text is on the side verso. On the recto is another documentary text dated to the end of the 1st century or the beginning of the 2nd century.4 Side verso of the scroll was used for the biblical text at the end of the 2nd century.

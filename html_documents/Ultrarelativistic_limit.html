Ultrarelativistic limit

In physics, a particle is called ultrarelativistic when its speed is very close to the speed of light c c c .

Max Planck showed that the expression for the relativistic energy of a particle with rest mass m m m and momentum p p p is given by

The energy of an ultrarelativistic particle is almost completely due to its momentum (), and thus can be approximated by E = p c E p c E=pc . This can result from holding the mass fixed and increasing p p p to very large values (the usual case); or by holding the energy E E E fixed and shrinking the mass m m m to negligible values. The latter is used to derive orbits of massless particles such as the photon from those of massive particles (cf. Kepler problem in general relativity).

Parallelization (mathematics)

In mathematics, a parallelization1 of a manifold M M M\, of dimension n is a set of n global linearly independent vector fields.

Formal definition

Given a manifold M M M\, of dimension n, a parallelization of M M M\, is a set { X 1 , … , X n } subscript X 1 normal-… subscript X n \{X_{1},\dots,X_{n}\} of n vector fields defined on all of M M M\, such that for every p ∈ M p M p\in M\, the set { X 1 ( p ) , … , X n ( p ) } subscript X 1 p normal-… subscript X n p \{X_{1}(p),\dots,X_{n}(p)\} is a basis of T p M subscript T p M T_{p}M\, , where T p M subscript T p M T_{p}M\, denotes the fiber over p p p\, of the tangent vector bundle T M T M TM\, .

A manifold is called parallelizable whenever admits a parallelization.

Examples

  • Every Lie group is a parallelizable manifold.
  • The product of parallelizable manifolds is parallelizable.
  • Every affine space, considered as manifold, is parallelizable.

Implication(FCA)

In formal concept analysis (FCA) and closure system attributes are analyzed via their (simultaneous) occurrences (= objects). The implicational analysis deals with algorithms to find a minimal set of rules, by which attributes can be inferred from others (implications).

Definition

A pair of sets ( U , V ) U V (U,V) is an implication. U ⊆ A U A U\subseteq A and V ⊆ A V A V\subseteq A . U is the premise and V is the conclusion. A A A stands for attributes.

A set W respects U → V normal-→ U V U\rightarrow V , if U ⊈ W or V ⊆ W not-subset-of-nor-equals U W or V W U\nsubseteq W\text{ or }V\subseteq W .

Implications in a Concept Lattice

U → V normal-→ U V U\rightarrow V holds in a concept lattice, if every intent respects it. This is true if

  1. iff U ′ ⊆ V ′ superscript U normal-′ superscript V normal-′ U^{\prime}\subseteq V^{\prime} .
  1. iff V ⊆ U ′′ V superscript U ′′ V\subseteq U^{\prime\prime} .

An implication U → V normal-→ U V U\rightarrow V follows semantically from a set L of implications, if each subset of A A A respecting L also respects U → V normal-→ U V U\rightarrow V . L is closed, if it contains all implications that follow from L.

A set L of implications that hold in a context is called sound. It is called complete if every implications that holds in the context follows from L. L is non-redundant if implications that follow from L are not in L.

If L is a set of implications, then { X ⊆ A | X respects L } conditional-set X A X respects L \{X\subseteq A|X\;\text{respects}\;L\} is a closure system.

Implication Basis

Let X ↦ X ′′ maps-to X superscript X ′′ X\;\mapsto\;X^{\prime\prime} be a closure operator then P is pseudo-closed if

  1. P ≠ P ′′ P superscript P ′′ P\;\neq\;P^{\prime\prime}
  2. Q ⊂ P ⟹ Q ′′ ⊆ P Q P superscript Q ′′ P Q\subset P\implies Q^{\prime\prime}\subseteq P

The set { P ↦ P ′′ | P pseudo-closed } conditional-set maps-to P superscript P ′′ P pseudo-closed \{P\mapsto P^{\prime\prime}|P\;\text{pseudo-closed}\} is sound, complete and non-redundant. It is called Duquenne-Guiges-Basis (D-G-basis) or Stem basis.

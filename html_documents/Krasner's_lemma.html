Krasner's lemma

In number theory, more specifically in p-adic analysis, Krasner's lemma is a basic result relating the topology of a complete non-archimedean field to its algebraic extensions.

Statement

Applications

  • Krasner's lemma can be used to show that 𝔭 𝔭 \mathfrak{p} -adic completion and separable closure of global fields commute.3 In other words, given 𝔭 𝔭 \mathfrak{p} a prime of a global field L, the separable closure of the 𝔭 𝔭 \mathfrak{p} -adic completion of L equals the 𝔭 ¯ normal-¯ 𝔭 \overline{\mathfrak{p}} -adic completion of the separable closure of L (where 𝔭 ¯ normal-¯ 𝔭 \overline{\mathfrak{p}} is a prime of L ¯ normal-¯ L \overline{L} above 𝔭 𝔭 \mathfrak{p} ).
  • Another application is to proving that Cp — the completion of the algebraic closure of Qp — is algebraically closed.45

(Fructose-bisphosphate aldolase)-lysine N-methyltransferase

(Fructose-bisphosphate aldolase)-lysine N-methyltransferase (, rubisco methyltransferase, ribulose-bisphosphate-carboxylase/oxygenase N-methyltransferase, ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate carboxylase/oxygenase large subunit epsilonN-methyltransferase, S-adenosyl-L-methionine:[3-phospho-D-glycerate-carboxy-lyase (dimerizing)]-lysine 6-N-methyltransferase) is an enzyme with system name S-adenosyl-L-methionine:(fructose-bisphosphate aldolase)-lysine N6-methyltransferase.12 This enzyme catalyses the following chemical reaction

3 S-adenosyl-L-methionine + [fructose-bisphosphate aldolase]-L-lysine ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons 3 S-adenosyl-L-homocysteine + [fructose-bisphosphate aldolase]-N6,N6,N6-trimethyl-L-lysine

The enzyme methylates a conserved lysine in the C-terminal part of higher plant fructose-bisphosphate aldolase (EC 4.1.2.13).

Modal matrix

In linear algebra, the modal matrix is used in the diagonalization process involving eigenvalues and eigenvectors.1

Specifically the modal matrix M M M for the matrix A A A is the n n matrix formed with the eigenvectors of A A A as columns in M M M . It is utilized in the similarity transformation

D = M - 1 A M , D superscript M 1 A M D=M^{-1}AM,

where D D D is an n n diagonal matrix with the eigenvalues of A A A on the main diagonal of D D D and zeros elsewhere. The matrix D D D is called the spectral matrix for A A A . The eigenvalues must appear left to right, top to bottom in the same order as their corresponding eigenvectors are arranged left to right in M M M .2

Generalized modal matrix

Let A A A be an n n matrix. A generalized modal matrix M M M for A A A is an n n matrix whose columns, considered as vectors, form a canonical basis for A A A and appear in M M M according to the following rules:

  • All Jordan chains consisting of one vector (that is, one vector in length) appear in the first columns of M M M .
  • All vectors of one chain appear together in adjacent columns of M M M .
  • Each chain appears in M M M in order of increasing rank (that is, the generalized eigenvector of rank 1 appears before the generalized eigenvector of rank 2 of the same chain, which appears before the generalized eigenvector of rank 3 of the same chain, etc.).5

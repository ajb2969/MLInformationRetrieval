Eilenberg–Zilber theorem

In mathematics, specifically in algebraic topology, the Eilenberg–Zilber theorem is an important result in establishing the link between the homology groups of a product space X × Y X Y X\times Y and those of the spaces X X X and Y Y Y . The theorem first appeared in a 1953 paper in the American Journal of Mathematics.

Consequences

The Eilenberg–Zilber theorem is a key ingredient in establishing the Künneth theorem, which expresses the homology groups H * ( X × Y ) subscript H X Y H_{*}(X\times Y) in terms of H * ( X ) subscript H X H_{*}(X) and H * ( Y ) subscript H Y H_{*}(Y) . In light of the Eilenberg–Zilber theorem, the content of the Künneth theorem consists in analysing how the homology of the tensor product complex relates to the homologies of the factors.

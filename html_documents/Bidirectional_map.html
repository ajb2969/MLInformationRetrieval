Bidirectional map

In computer science, a bidirectional map, or hash bag, is an associative data structure in which the ( k e y , v a l u e ) k e y v a l u e (key,value) pairs form a one-to-one correspondence. Thus the binary relation is functional in each direction v a l u e v a l u e value can also act as a key to k e y k e y key . A pair ( a , b ) a b (a,b) thus provides a unique coupling between a a a and b b b so that b b b can be found when a a a is used as a key and a a a can be found when b b b is used as a key.

Douady rabbit

The Douady rabbit is any of various particular filled Julia sets associated with the parameter near the center period 3 buds of Mandelbrot set for complex quadratic map.

Name

Douady's rabbit or Douady rabbit is named for the French mathematician Adrien Douady.

Fat rabbit or chubby rabbit has c at the root of 1/3-limb of the Mandelbrot set. It has a parabolic fixed point with 3 petals.1

Mandelbrot and filled Julia sets

There are two planes associated with ℳ ℳ \mathcal{M} . One of these, the z z z (or w w w ) plane, will be called the mapping plane, since ℳ ℳ \mathcal{M} sends this plane into itself. The other, the γ γ \gamma (or μ μ \mu ) plane, will be called the control plane.

The nature of what happens in the mapping plane under repeated application of ℳ ℳ \mathcal{M} depends on where γ γ \gamma (or μ μ \mu ) is in the control plane. The filled Julia set consists of all points in the mapping plane whose images remain bounded under indefinitely repeated applications of ℳ ℳ \mathcal{M} . The Mandelbrot set consists of those points in the control plane such that the associated filled Julia set in the mapping plane is connected.

Figure 1 shows the Mandelbrot set when γ γ \gamma is the control parameter, and Figure 2 shows the Mandelbrot set when μ μ \mu is the control parameter. Since z z z and w w w are affine transformations of one another (a linear transformation plus a translation), the filled Julia sets look much the same in either the z z z or w w w planes.

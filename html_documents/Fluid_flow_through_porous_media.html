Fluid flow through porous media

Introduction

Many day to day processes involve the movement of fluids across porous medium. For example sponge, drying of wood, filtration of water by using sand and/or other porous materials. As commonly observed, some fluid flows through the media while some mass of the fluid is stored in the pores present in the media. The knowledge of how the fluid diffuses through these materials and what are the factors upon which the diffusion depends is really useful for Engineering practices.

Governing Law

The basic law governing the flow of fluids through porous media is Darcy's Law, which was formulated by the French civil engineer Henry Darcy in 1856 on the basis of his experiments on vertical water filtration through sand beds.1 According to which,

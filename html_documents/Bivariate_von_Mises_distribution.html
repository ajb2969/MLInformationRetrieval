Bivariate von Mises distribution

In probability theory and statistics, the bivariate von Mises distribution is a probability distribution describing values on a torus. It may be thought of as an analogue on the torus of the bivariate normal distribution. The distribution belongs to the field of directional statistics. The general bivariate von Mises distribution was first proposed by Kanti Mardia in 1975.12 One of its variants is today used in the field of bioinformatics to formulate a probabilistic model of protein structure in atomic detail.34

The Loss‚ÄìDiVincenzo quantum computer (or spin-qubit quantum computer) is a scalable semiconductor-based quantum computer proposed by Daniel Loss and David P. DiVincenzo in 1997.1 The proposal was to use as qubits the intrinsic spin-1/2 degree of freedom of individual electrons confined to quantum dots. This was done in a way that fulfilled DiVincenzo Criteria for a scalable quantum computer,2 namely:

  • identification of well-defined qubits;
  • reliable state preparation;
  • low decoherence;
  • accurate quantum gate operations and
  • strong quantum measurements.

A candidate for such a quantum computer is a lateral quantum dot system.

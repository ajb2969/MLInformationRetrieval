The simplest example of a pinch point is the hypersurface defined by the equation u 2 - v w 2 = 0 superscript u 2 v superscript w 2 0 u^{2}-vw^{2}=0 called Whitney umbrella.

The pinch point (in this case the origin) is a limit of normal crossings singular points (the v v v -axis in this case). These singular points are intimately related in the sense that in order to resolve the pinch point singularity one must blow-up the whole v v v -axis and not only the pinch point.

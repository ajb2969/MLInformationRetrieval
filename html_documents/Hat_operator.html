Estimated Value

In statistics, the hat is used to denote an estimator or an estimated value, as opposed to its theoretical counterpart. For example, in the context of errors and residuals, the "hat" over the letter Îµ indicates an observable estimate (the residuals) of an unobservable quantity called Îµ (the statistical errors).

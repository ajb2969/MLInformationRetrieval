Jacobi polynomials

In mathematics, Jacobi polynomials (occasionally called hypergeometric polynomials) P ( x ) P x P(x) are a class of classical orthogonal polynomials. They are orthogonal with respect to the weight on the interval − 1 , 11 normal-− 1 11 −1,11 . The Gegenbauer polynomials, and thus also the Legendre, Zernike and Chebyshev polynomials, are special cases of the Jacobi polynomials.1

The Jacobi polynomials were introduced by Carl Gustav Jacob Jacobi.

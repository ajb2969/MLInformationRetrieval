15 Arietis

15 Arietis is the Flamsteed designation for a single1 star in the northern constellation of Aries. It has an apparent visual magnitude of 5.74,2 which is just bright enough to be visible to the naked eye from dark suburban skies. An annual parallax shift of 5.84 mas3 corresponds to a physical distance of approximately from Earth. At that distance, the star's brightness is reduced by 0.334 in magnitude because of extinction from interstellar gas and dust.

This is a red giant star with a stellar classification of M3 III.5 The measured angular diameter of this star is .6 At the estimated distance of Delta Ophiuchi,7 this yields a physical size of about 67 times the radius of the Sun.8 It is a pulsating variable star with the variable star designation AV Arietis. The first pulsation period is 18.1 days with an amplitude of 0.028 magnitudes, while the second is 21.9 days and 0.030 in magnitude.9

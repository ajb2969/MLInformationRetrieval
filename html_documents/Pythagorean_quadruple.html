Pythagorean quadruple

A Pythagorean quadruple is a tuple of integers a, b, c and d, such that d > 0 and a 2 + b 2 + c 2 = d 2 superscript a 2 superscript b 2 superscript c 2 superscript d 2 a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}=d^{2} , and is often denoted ( a , b , c , d ) a b c d (a,b,c,d) . Geometrically, a Pythagorean quadruple ( a , b , c , d ) a b c d (a,b,c,d) defines a cuboid with integer side lengths |a|, |b|, and |c|, whose space diagonal has integer length d. Pythagorean quadruples are thus also called Pythagorean Boxes.1

Properties

The largest number that always divides the product abcd is 12.6 The quadruple with the minimal product is (1, 2, 2, 3).

In statistics, the inverse Wishart distribution, also called the inverted Wishart distribution, is a probability distribution defined on real-valued positive-definite matrices. In Bayesian statistics it is used as the conjugate prior for the covariance matrix of a multivariate normal distribution.

Note that when 𝐒 𝐒 \mathbf{S} is equal to an identity matrix, 𝒢 𝒲 - 1 ( 𝚿 , ν , 𝐒 ) = 𝒲 - 1 ( 𝚿 , ν ) 𝒢 superscript 𝒲 1 𝚿 ν 𝐒 superscript 𝒲 1 𝚿 ν \mathcal{GW}^{-1}(\mathbf{\Psi},\nu,\mathbf{S})=\mathcal{W}^{-1}(\mathbf{\Psi}% ,\nu) . A different type of generalization is the normal-inverse-Wishart distribution, essentially the product of a multivariate normal distribution with an inverse Wishart distribution.

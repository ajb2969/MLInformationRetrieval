Generalized Clifford algebra

In mathematics, a Generalized Clifford algebra (GCA) is an associative algebra that generalizes the Clifford algebra, and goes back to the work of Hermann Weyl,1 who utilized and formalized these clock-and-shift operators introduced by J. J. Sylvester (1882),2 and organized by Cartan (1898)3 and Schwinger.4

Clock and shift matrices find routine applications in numerous areas of mathematical physics, providing the cornerstone of quantum mechanical dynamics in finite-dimensional vector spaces.567 The concept of a spinor can further be linked to these algebras.8

The term Generalized Clifford Algebras can also refer to associative algebras that are constructed using forms of higher degree instead of quadratic forms.9101112

Book (graph theory)

In graph theory, a book graph (often written B p subscript B p B_{p}  ) may be any of several kinds of graph.

One kind, which may be called a quadrilateral book, consists of p quadrilaterals sharing a common edge (known as the "spine" or "base" of the book).1 A book of this type is the Cartesian product of a star and K2 .

A second type, which might be called a triangular book, is the complete tripartite graph K1,1,p. It is a graph consisting of p p p triangles sharing a common edge.2 A book of this type is a split graph. This graph has also been called a K e ( 2 , p ) subscript K e 2 p K_{e}(2,p) .3

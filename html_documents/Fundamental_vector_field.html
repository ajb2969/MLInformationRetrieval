Fundamental vector field

In the study of mathematics and especially differential geometry, fundamental vector fields are an instrument that describes the infinitesimal behaviour of a smooth Lie group action on a smooth manifold. Such vector fields find important applications in the study of Lie theory, symplectic geometry, and the study of Hamiltonian group actions.

Applications

Lie groups

The Lie algebra of a Lie group G G G may be identified with either the left- or right-invariant vector fields on G G G . It is a well known result9 that such vector fields are isomorphic to T e G subscript T e G T_{e}G , the tangent space at identity. In fact, if we let G G G act on itself via right-multiplication, the corresponding fundamental vector fields are precisely the left-invariant vector fields.

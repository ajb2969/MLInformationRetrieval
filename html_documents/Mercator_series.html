Complex series

The complex power series

∑ n = 1 ∞ z n n = z + z 2 2 + z 3 3 + z 4 4 + ⋯ superscript subscript n 1 superscript z n n z superscript z 2 2 superscript z 3 3 superscript z 4 4 normal-⋯ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{z^{n}}{n}=z\,+\,\frac{z^{2}}{2}\,+\,\frac{z^{3}}{3}\,% +\,\frac{z^{4}}{4}\,+\,\cdots

is the Taylor series for -log(1 - z), where log denotes the principal branch of the complex logarithm. This series converges precisely for all complex number |z| ≤ 1, z ≠ 1.

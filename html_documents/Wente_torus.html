Wente torus

In differential geometry, a Wente torus is an immersed torus in ℝ 3 superscript ℝ 3 \mathbb{R}^{3} of constant mean curvature, discovered by . It is a counterexample to the conjecture of Heinz Hopf that every closed, compact, constant-mean-curvature surface is a sphere (though this is true if the surface is embedded). There are similar examples known for every positive genus.

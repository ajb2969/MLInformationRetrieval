Carathéodory's criterion

Carathéodory's criterion is a result in measure theory that was formulated by Greek mathematician Constantin Carathéodory. Its statement is as follows: Let λ * superscript λ \lambda^{*} denote the Lebesgue outer measure on ℝ n superscript ℝ n \mathbb{R}^{n} , and let E ⊆ ℝ n E superscript ℝ n E\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n} . Then E E E is Lebesgue measurable if and only if λ * ( A ) = λ * ( A ∩ E ) + λ * ( A ∩ E c ) superscript λ A superscript λ A E superscript λ A superscript E c \lambda^{*}(A)=\lambda^{*}(A\cap E)+\lambda^{*}(A\cap E^{c}) for every A ⊆ ℝ n A superscript ℝ n A\subseteq\mathbb{R}^{n} . Notice that A A A is not required to be a measurable set.

Examples

  • Algebraically closed fields and separably closed fields are always PAC.
  • Pseudo-finite fields and hyper-finite fields are PAC.
  • A non-principal ultraproduct of distinct finite fields is (pseudo-finite and hence3) PAC.4 Ax deduces this from the Riemann hypothesis for curves over finite fields.5
  • Infinite algebraic extensions of finite fields are PAC.6
  • The PAC Nullstellensatz. The absolute Galois group G G G of a field K K K is profinite, hence compact, and hence equipped with a normalized Haar measure.   • Let K be the maximal totally real Galois extension of the rational numbers and i the square root of -1. Then K(i) is PAC.

Properties

  • The Brauer group of a PAC field is trivial,8 as any Severi‚ÄìBrauer variety has a rational point.9
  • The absolute Galois group of a PAC field is a projective profinite group; equivalently, it has cohomological dimension at most 1.10
  • A PAC field of characteristic zero is C1.11

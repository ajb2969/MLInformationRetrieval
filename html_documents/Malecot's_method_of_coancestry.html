Malecot's method of coancestry

Malecot's coancestry coefficient, f f f , refers to an indirect measure of genetic similarity of two individuals which was initially devised by the French mathematician Gustave Malécot.

f f f is defined as the probability that any two alleles, sampled at random (one from each individual), are identical copies of an ancestral allele. In species with well-known lineages (such as domesticated crops), f f f can be calculated by examining detailed pedigree records. Modernly, f f f can be estimated using genetic marker data.

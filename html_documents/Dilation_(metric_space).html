Dilation (metric space)

In mathematics, a dilation is a function f f f from a metric space into itself that satisfies the identity

d ( f ( x ) , f ( y ) ) = r d ( x , y ) d f x f y r d x y d(f(x),f(y))=rd(x,y) for all points ( x , y ) x y (x,y) , where d ( x , y ) d x y d(x,y) is the distance from x x x to y y y and r r r is some positive real number.1

In Euclidean space, such a dilation is a similarity of the space.2 Dilations change the size but not the shape of an object or figure.

Every dilation of a Euclidean space that is not a congruence has a unique fixed point3 that is called the center of dilation.4 Some congruences have fixed points and others do not.5

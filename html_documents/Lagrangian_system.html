Lagrangian system

In mathematics, a Lagrangian system is a pair ( Y , L ) Y L (Y,L) of a smooth fiber bundle Y → X Y normal-→ X Y→X and a Lagrangian density L L L which yields the Euler–Lagrange differential operator acting on sections of Y → X Y normal-→ X Y→X .

In classical mechanics, many dynamical systems are Lagrangian systems. The configuration space of such a Lagrangian system is a fiber bundle Q → ℝ Q normal-→ normal-ℝ Q→ℝ over the time axis ℝ normal-ℝ ℝ . In particular, Q = ℝ × M Q normal-ℝ normal-× M Q=ℝ×M if a reference frame is fixed. In classical field theory, all field systems are the Lagrangian ones.

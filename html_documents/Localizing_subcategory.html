Localizing subcategory

In mathematics, Serre and localizing subcategories form important classes of subcategories of an abelian category. Localizing subcategories are certain Serre subcategories. They are strongly linked to the notion of a quotient category.

Serre subcategories

Let 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} be an abelian category. A non-empty full subcategory 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} is called a Serre subcategory (or also a dense subcategory), if for every short exact sequence 0 → A ′ → A → A ′′ → 0 normal-→ 0 superscript A normal-′ normal-→ A normal-→ superscript A ′′ normal-→ 0 0\rightarrow A^{\prime}\rightarrow A\rightarrow A^{\prime\prime}\rightarrow 0 in 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} the object A A A is in 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} if and only if the objects A ′ superscript A normal-′ A^{\prime} and A ′′ superscript A ′′ A^{\prime\prime} belong to 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} . In words: 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} is closed under subobjects, quotient objects and extensions.

The importance of this notion stems from the fact that kernels of exact functors between abelian categories have this property, and that one can build (for locally small 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} ) the quotient category (in the sense of Gabriel, Grothendieck, Serre) 𝒜 / 𝒞 𝒜 𝒞 \mathcal{A}/\mathcal{C} , which has the same objects as 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} , is abelian, and comes with an exact functor (called the quotient functor) T : 𝒜 → 𝒜 / 𝒞 normal-: T normal-→ 𝒜 𝒜 𝒞 T\colon\mathcal{A}\rightarrow\mathcal{A}/\mathcal{C} whose kernel is 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} .

Localizing subcategories

Let 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} be locally small. The Serre subcategory 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} is called localizing, if the quotient functor T : 𝒜 → 𝒜 / 𝒞 normal-: T normal-→ 𝒜 𝒜 𝒞 T\colon\mathcal{A}\rightarrow\mathcal{A}/\mathcal{C} has a right adjoint S : 𝒜 / 𝒞 → 𝒜 normal-: S normal-→ 𝒜 𝒞 𝒜 S\colon\mathcal{A}/\mathcal{C}\rightarrow\mathcal{A} . Since then T T T , as a left adjoint, preserves colimits, each localizing subcategory is closed under colimits. The functor T T T (or sometimes S T S T ST ) is also called the localization functor, and S S S the section functor. The section functor is left-exact and fully faithful.

If the abelian category 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} is moreover cocomplete and has injective hulls (e.g. if it is a Grothendieck category), then a Serre subcategory 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} is localizing if and only if 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} is closed under arbitrary coproducts (a.k.a. direct sums). Hence the notion of a localizing subcategory is equivalent to the notion of a hereditary torsion class.

If 𝒜 𝒜 \mathcal{A} is a Grothendieck category and 𝒞 𝒞 \mathcal{C} a localizing subcategory, then the quotient category 𝒜 / 𝒞 𝒜 𝒞 \mathcal{A}/\mathcal{C} is again a Grothendieck category.

The Gabriel-Popescu theorem implies that every Grothendieck category is the quotient category of a module category Mod ( R ) Mod R \operatorname{Mod}(R) (with R R R a suitable ring) modulo a localizing subcategory.

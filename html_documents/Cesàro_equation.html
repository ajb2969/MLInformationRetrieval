Cesàro equation

In geometry, the Cesàro equation of a plane curve is an equation relating the curvature ( κ κ \kappa ) at a point of the curve to the arc length ( s s s ) from the start of the curve to the given point. It may also be given as an equation relating the radius of curvature ( R R R ) to arc length. (These are equivalent because R = 1 / κ R 1 κ R=1/\kappa .) Two congruent curves will have the same Cesàro equation. Cesàro equations are named after Ernesto Cesàro.

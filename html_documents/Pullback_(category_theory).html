Weak pullbacks

A weak pullback of a cospan X → Z ← Y X normal-→ Z normal-← Y X→Z←Y is a cone over the cospan that is only weakly universal, that is, the mediating morphism u : Q → P normal-: u Q normal-→ P u:Q→P above is not required to be unique.

Pullback and product

The pullback is similar to the product, but not the same. One may obtain the product by "forgetting" that the morphisms f f f and g g g exist, and forgetting that the object Z Z Z exists. One is then left with a discrete category containing only the two objects X X X and Y Y Y , and no arrows between them. This discrete category may be used as the index set to construct the ordinary binary product. Thus, the pullback can be thought of as the ordinary (Cartesian) product, but with additional structure. Instead of "forgetting" Z Z Z , f f f , and g g g , one can also "trivialize" them by specializing Z Z Z to be the terminal object (assuming it exists). f f f and g g g are then uniquely determined and thus carry no information, and the pullback of this cospan can be seen to be the product of X X X and Y Y Y .

Fiber bundles

Another example of a pullback comes from the theory of fiber bundles: given a bundle map π : E → B normal-: π E normal-→ B π:E→B and a continuous map f : X → B normal-: f X normal-→ B f:X→B , the pullback is a fiber bundle over X X X called the pullback bundle. The associated commutative diagram is a morphism of fiber bundles.

Poinsot's spirals

In mathematics, Poinsot's spirals are two spirals represented by the polar equations

r = a csch ( n θ ) r a csch n θ r=a\ \operatorname{csch}(n\theta)

r = a sech ( n θ ) r a sech n θ r=a\ \operatorname{sech}(n\theta) where csch is the hyperbolic cosecant, and sech is the hyperbolic secant.1 They are named after the French mathematician Louis Poinsot.

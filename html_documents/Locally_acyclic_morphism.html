Locally acyclic morphism

In algebraic geometry, a morphism f : X → S normal-: f normal-→ X S f:X\to S of schemes is said to be locally acyclic if, roughly, any sheaf on S and its restriction to X through f have the same étale cohomology, locally. For example, a smooth morphism is universally locally acyclic.

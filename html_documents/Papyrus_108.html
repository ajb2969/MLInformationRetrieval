Papyrus 108

Papyrus 108 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 108, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of John, containing verses 17:23-24 & 18:1-5 in a fragmentary condition. The manuscript has been paleographically assigned to the late 2nd or early 3rd Century CE.1 The manuscript is currently housed at the Papyrology Rooms (P. Oxy. 4447) of the Sackler Library at Oxford University.2

Description

The original manuscript would've been around 14.5 cm x 18.5 cm, with 23 lines per page. The handwriting script is representative of the reformed Documentary style.3 The text is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type. Although small, the manuscript concurs with Codex Sinaiticus.4 It has itacistic error in John 17:23 (γεινωσκη instead of γινωσκη).5

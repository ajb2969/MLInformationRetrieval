Rogers–Ramanujan identities

In mathematics, the Rogers–Ramanujan identities are two identities related to basic hypergeometric series, first discovered and proved by . They were subsequently rediscovered (without a proof) by Srinivasa Ramanujan some time before 193. Ramanujan had no proof, but rediscovered Rogers's paper in 1917, and they then published a joint new proof . independently rediscovered and proved the identities.

The Rogers-Ramanujan identities could be now interpreted in the following way. Let n n n be a non-negative integer.

  1. The number of partitions of n n n such that the adjacent parts differ by at least 2 is the same as the number of partitions of n n n such that each part is congruent to either 1 or 4 modulo 5.
  2. The number of partitions of n n n such that the adjacent parts differ by at least 2 and such that the smallest part is at least 2 is the same as the number of partitions of n n n such that each part is congruent to either 2 or 3 modulo 5.

Applications

The Rogers–Ramanujan identities appeared in Baxter's solution of the hard hexagon model in statistical mechanics.

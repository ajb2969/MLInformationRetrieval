Laplace formula

The Laplace formula is a result in fluid mechanics that gives the surface pressure between two continuous media. The surface of separation is really a transitional layer, but it is so thin that in classical mechanics it can be considered a dividing surface between the two media.

Definition

Consider two continuous media (top and bottom) whose surface of separation undergoes a small displacement. If r t subscript r t r_{t} and r b subscript r b r_{b} are the principal radii of curvature of the surface in some sample region, then the difference of pressures is

where α α \alpha is the surface tension coefficient.

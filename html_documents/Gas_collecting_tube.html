Gas collecting tube

The characterization gas collecting tube describes an oblong gas-tight container with one valve at either end. Usually such a container has a gauged volume, has a cylindrical shape and is made of glass. Gas collecting tubes are used for science-related purposes; for taking samples of gases.

Measurement of the Mass Density of a Gas - Aspirator Method 1

The mass density of a gas can be measured by using a gas collecting tube, an analytical balance and an aspirator. The mass and volume of a displaced amount of gas are determined: At atmospheric pressure p p p , the gas collecting tube is filled with the gas to be investigated and the overall mass m f u l l subscript m f u l l m_{full} is measured. Then the aspirator sucks out much of the gas yielding a second overall mass m s u c k e d subscript m s u c k e d m_{sucked} measurement. (The difference of masses represents the mass ( m = m f u l l - m s u c k e d m subscript m f u l l subscript m s u c k e d m=m_{full}-m_{sucked} ) of the extracted amount of gas.) Finally, the nearly evacuated gas collecting tube is allowed to suck in an outgassed liquid, usually previously heated water, which is offered under atmospheric pressure p p p . The gas collecting tube is weighed for a third and last time containing the liquid yielding the value m c o n t a i n i n g l i q u i d subscript m c o n t a i n i n g l i q u i d m_{containingliquid} . (The difference of masses of the nearly evacuated tube and the liquid-containing tube gives the mass ( m l i q u i d = m c o n t a i n i n g l i q u i d - m s u c k e d subscript m l i q u i d subscript m c o n t a i n i n g l i q u i d subscript m s u c k e d m_{liquid}=m_{containingliquid}-m_{sucked} ) of the sucked-in liquid, that took the place of the extracted amount of gas.)

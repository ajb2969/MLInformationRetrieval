Volume integral

In mathematics—in particular, in multivariable calculus—a volume integral refers to an integral over a 3-dimensional domain, that is, it is a special case of multiple integrals. Volume integrals are especially important in physics for many applications, for example, to calculate flux densities.

In coordinates

It can also mean a triple integral within a region D in R3 of a function f ( x , y , z ) , f x y z f(x,y,z), and is usually written as:

∭ D f ( x , y , z ) d x d y d z . subscript triple-integral D f x y z d x d y d z \iiint\limits_{D}f(x,y,z)\,dx\,dy\,dz.

A volume integral in cylindrical coordinates is

∭ D f ( r , θ , z ) r d r d θ d z , subscript triple-integral D f r θ z r d r d θ d z \iiint\limits_{D}f(r,\theta,z)\,r\,dr\,d\theta\,dz,

and a volume integral in spherical coordinates (using the convention for angles with θ θ \theta as the azimuth and ϕ ϕ \phi measured from the polar axis (see more on conventions)) has the form

∭ D f ( r , θ , ϕ ) r 2 sin ϕ d r d θ d ϕ . subscript triple-integral D f r θ ϕ superscript r 2 ϕ d r d θ d ϕ \iiint\limits_{D}f(r,\theta,\phi)\,r^{2}\sin\phi\,dr\,d\theta\,d\phi.

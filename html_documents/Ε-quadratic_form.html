Refinements

An intuitive way to understand an Œµ-quadratic form is to think of it as a quadratic refinement of its associated Œµ-symmetric form.

For instance, in defining a Clifford algebra over a general field or ring, one quotients the tensor algebra by relations coming from the symmetric form and the quadratic form: vw + wv = 2B(v,w) and v 2 = Q ( v ) superscript v 2 Q v v^{2}=Q(v) .

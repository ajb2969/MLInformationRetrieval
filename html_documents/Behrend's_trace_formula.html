Behrend's trace formula

In algebraic geometry, Behrend's formula is a generalization of the Grothendieck‚ÄìLefschetz trace formula to a smooth algebraic stack over a finite field, conjectured in 1993 1 and proven in 2003 2 by Kai Behrend. Unlike the classical one, the formula counts points in the "stacky way"; it takes into account the presence of nontrivial automorphisms.

The desire for the formula comes from the fact that it applies to the moduli stack of principal bundles on a curve over a finite field (in some instances indirectly, via the Harder‚ÄìNarasimhan stratification, as the moduli stack is not of finite type.34) See the moduli stack of principal bundles and references therein for the precise formulation in this case.

Deligne found an example5 that shows the formula may be interpreted as a sort of the Selberg trace formula.

A proof of the formula in the context of the six operations formalism developed by Laszlo and Olsson6 is given by Shenghao Sun.7

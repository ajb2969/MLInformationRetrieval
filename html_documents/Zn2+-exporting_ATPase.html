Zn2+-exporting ATPase

In enzymology, a Zn2+-exporting ATPase () is an enzyme that catalyzes the chemical reaction

ATP + H2O + Zn2+in ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons ADP + phosphate + Zn2+out

The 3 substrates of this enzyme are ATP, H2O, and Zn2+, whereas its 3 products are ADP, phosphate, and Zn2+.

This enzyme belongs to the family of hydrolases, specifically those acting on acid anhydrides to catalyse transmembrane movement of substances. The systematic name of this enzyme class is ATP phosphohydrolase (Zn2+-exporting). Other names in common use include Zn(II)-translocating P-type ATPase, P1B-type ATPase, and AtHMA4.

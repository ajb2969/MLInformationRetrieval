Maximum common edge subgraph problem

Given two graphs G G G and G ′ superscript G normal-′ G^{\prime} , the maximum common edge subgraph problem is the problem of finding a graph H H H with as many edges as possible which is isomorphic to both a subgraph of G G G and a subgraph of G ′ superscript G normal-′ G^{\prime} .

The maximum common edge subgraph problem on general graphs is NP-complete as it is a generalization of subgraph isomorphism: a graph H H H is isomorphic to a subgraph of another graph G G G if and only if the maximum common edge subgraph of G G G and H H H has the same number of edges as H H H . Unless the two inputs G G G and G ′ superscript G normal-′ G^{\prime} to the maximum common edge subgraph problem are required to have the same number of vertices, the problem is APX-hard.1

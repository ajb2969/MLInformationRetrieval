Graphical timeline of the Big Bang

This timeline of the Big Bang shows the sequence of events as predicted by the Big Bang theory, from the beginning of time to the end of the Dark Ages.

It is a logarithmic scale that shows 10 ⋅ log 10 normal-⋅ 10 subscript 10 10\cdot\log_{10} second instead of second. For example, one microsecond is 10 ⋅ log 10 0.000001 = 10 ⋅ ( - 6 ) = - 60 normal-⋅ 10 subscript 10 0.000001 normal-⋅ 10 6 60 10\cdot\log_{10}0.000001=10\cdot(-6)=-60 . To convert -30 read on the scale to second calculate 10 - 30 10 = 10 - 3 = 0.001 superscript 10 30 10 superscript 10 3 0.001 10^{-\frac{30}{10}}=10^{-3}=0.001 second = one millisecond. On a logarithmic time scale a step lasts ten times longer than the previous step.

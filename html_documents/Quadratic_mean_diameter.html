Quadratic mean diameter

In Forestry, Quadratic mean diameter or QMD is a measure of Central tendency which is considered more appropriate than Arithmetic mean for characterizing the group of trees which have been measured. For n trees, QMD is calculated as:

∑ D i 2 n superscript subscript D i 2 n \sqrt{\frac{\sum{D_{i}}^{2}}{n}}

where D i subscript D i {D_{i}} is the Diameter at breast height of the ith tree. Compared to the arithmetic mean, QMD assigns greater weight to larger trees - QMD is always greater than or equal to arithmetic mean for a given set of trees. QMD can be used in timber cruises to estimate the standing volume of timber in a forest, because it has the practical advantage of being directly related to Basal area, which in turn is directly related to volume. 1 QMD can also be calculated as:

B A k * n B A k n \sqrt{\frac{BA}{k*n}}

where BA is stand basal area, n is the number of trees, and k is a constant based on measurement units - for BA in ft2 and DBH in inches, k=0.005454; for BA in m2 and DBH in cm, k=0.0000785.

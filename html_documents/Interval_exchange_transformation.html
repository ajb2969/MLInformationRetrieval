Interval exchange transformation

In mathematics, an interval exchange transformation1 is a kind of dynamical system that generalises circle rotation. The phase space consists of the unit interval, and the transformation acts by cutting the interval into several subintervals, and then permuting these subintervals.

Generalizations

Two and higher-dimensional generalizations include polygon exchanges, polyhedral exchanges and piecewise isometries.4

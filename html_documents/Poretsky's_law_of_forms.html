Poretsky's law of forms

In Boolean algebra, Poretsky's law of forms shows that the single Boolean equation f ( X ) = 0 f X 0 f(X)=0 is equivalent to g ( X ) = h ( X ) g X h X g(X)=h(X) if and only if g = f ⊕ h g direct-sum f h g=f\oplus h , where ⊕ direct-sum \oplus represents exclusive or.

The law of forms was discovered by Platon Poretsky.

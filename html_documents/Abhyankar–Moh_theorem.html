Abhyankar–Moh theorem

In mathematics, the Abhyankar–Moh theorem states that if L L L is a complex line in the complex affine plane ℂ 2 superscript ℂ 2 \mathbb{C}^{2} , then every embedding of L L L into ℂ 2 superscript ℂ 2 \mathbb{C}^{2} extends to an automorphism of the plane. It is named after Shreeram Shankar Abhyankar and T.-T. Moh, who published it in 1975. More generally, the same theorem applies to lines and planes over any algebraically closed field of characteristic zero, and to certain well-behaved subsets of higher-dimensional complex affine spaces.

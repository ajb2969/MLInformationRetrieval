Fundamental tools

The cyclotomic polynomials are monic polynomials with integer coefficients that are irreducible over the field of the rational numbers. Except for n equal to 1 or 2, they are palindromics of even degree.

The degree of Φ n subscript normal-Φ n \Phi_{n} , or in other words the number of nth primitive roots of unity, is φ ( n ) φ n \varphi(n) , where φ φ \varphi is Euler's totient function.

The fact that Φ n subscript normal-Φ n \Phi_{n} is an irreducible polynomial of degree φ ( n ) φ n \varphi(n) in the ring ℤ [ x ] ℤ delimited-[] x \mathbb{Z}[x] is a nontrivial result due to Gauss.2 Depending on the chosen definition, it is either the value of the degree or the irreducibility which is a nontrivial result. The case of prime n is easier to prove than the general case, thanks to Eisenstein's criterion.

Cobyrinate a,c-diamide synthase

Cobyrinate a,c-diamide synthase (, cobyrinic acid a,c-diamide synthetase, CbiA (gene)) is an enzyme.1 This enzyme catalyses the following chemical reaction

2 ATP + cobyrinate + 2 L-glutamine + 2 H2O ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons 2 ADP + 2 phosphate + cobyrinate a,c-diamide + 2 L-glutamate (overall reaction)
(1a) ATP + cobyrinate + L-glutamine + H2O ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons ADP + phosphate + cobyrinate c-monamide + L-glutamate
(1b) ATP + cobyrinate c-monamide + L-glutamine + H2O ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons ADP + phosphate + cobyrinate a,c-diamide + L-glutamate

This enzyme is the first glutamine amidotransferase that participates in the anaerobic biosynthetic pathway of adenosylcobalamin.

Seminormal subgroup

In mathematics, in the field of group theory, a subgroup A A A of a group G G G is termed seminormal if there is a subgroup B B B such that A B = G A B G AB=G , and for any proper subgroup C C C of B B B , A C A C AC is a proper subgroup of G G G .

This definition of seminormal subgroups is due to Xiang Ying Su.12

Every normal subgroup is seminormal. For finite groups, every quasinormal subgroup is seminormal.

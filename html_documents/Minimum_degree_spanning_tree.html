Minimum degree spanning tree

In graph theory, for a connected graph G G G , a spanning tree T T T is a subgraph of G G G with the least number of edges that still spans G G G . A number of properties can be proved about T T T . T T T is acyclic, has ( | V | - 1 V 1 |V|-1 ) edges where V V V is the number of vertices in G G G etc.

A minimum degree spanning tree T ′ superscript T normal-′ T^{\prime} is a spanning tree which has the least maximum degree. The vertex of maximum degree in T ′ superscript T normal-′ T^{\prime} is the least among all possible spanning trees of G G G .

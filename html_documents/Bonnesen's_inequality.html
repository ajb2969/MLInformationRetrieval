Bonnesen's inequality

Bonnesen's inequality is an inequality relating the length, the area, the radius of the incircle and the radius of the circumcircle of a Jordan curve. It is a strengthening of the classical isoperimetric inequality.

More precisely, consider a planar simple closed curve of length L L L bounding a domain of area A A A . Let r r r and R R R denote the radii of the incircle and the circumcircle. Bonnesen proved the inequality

π 2 ( R - r ) 2 ≤ L 2 - 4 π A . superscript π 2 superscript R r 2 superscript L 2 4 π A \pi^{2}(R-r)^{2}\leq L^{2}-4\pi A.\,

The term π 2 ( R - r ) 2 superscript π 2 superscript R r 2 \pi^{2}(R-r)^{2} in the left hand side is known as the isoperimetric defect.

Loewner's torus inequality with isosystolic defect is a systolic analogue of Bonnesen's inequality.

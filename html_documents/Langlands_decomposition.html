Langlands decomposition

In mathematics, the Langlands decomposition writes a parabolic subgroup P of a semisimple Lie group as a product P = M A N P M A N P=MAN of a reductive subgroup M, an abelian subgroup A, and a nilpotent subgroup N.

Applications

A key application is in parabolic induction, which leads to the Langlands program: if G G G is a reductive algebraic group and P = M A N P M A N P=MAN is the Langlands decomposition of a parabolic subgroup P, then parabolic induction consists of taking a representation of M A M A MA , extending it to P P P by letting N N N act trivially, and inducing the result from P P P to G G G .

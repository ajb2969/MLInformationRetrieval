Connection (algebraic framework)

Geometry of quantum systems (e.g., noncommutative geometry and supergeometry) is mainly phrased in algebraic terms of modules and algebras. Connections on modules are generalization of a linear connection on a smooth vector bundle E → X normal-→ E X E\to X written as a Koszul connection on the C ∞ ( X ) superscript C X C^{\infty}(X) -module of sections of E → X normal-→ E X E\to X .1

Graded commutative algebra

The notion of a connection on modules over commutative rings is straightforwardly extended to modules over a graded commutative algebra.3 This is the case of superconnections in supergeometry of graded manifolds and supervector bundles. Superconnections always exist.

Noncommutative algebra

If A A A is a noncommutative ring, connections on left and right A A A -modules are defined similarly to those on modules over commutative rings.4 However these connections need not exist.

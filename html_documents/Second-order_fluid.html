Second-order fluid

A second-order fluid is a fluid where the stress tensor is the sum of all tensors that can be formed from the velocity field with up to two derivatives. So that the total stress tensor can be written

where A A A is the strain tensor, A u subscript A u A_{u} is the upper convected derivative of A A A and A l subscript A l A_{l} is the lower convected derivative of A A A . The coefficients C i subscript C i C_{i} are functions of the tensor invariants of A A A . An Euler fluid is a zeroth order fluid and a Newtonian fluid is a first order one.

For a steady flow this can be written

where B is the second Rivlin-Ericksen tensor, and C 5 subscript C 5 C_{5} is a new constant.

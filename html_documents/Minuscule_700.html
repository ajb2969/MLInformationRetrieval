Minuscule 700

Minuscule 700 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), Îµ 133 (Soden),1 is a Greek minuscule manuscript of the Gospels. Formerly it was labelled as 604 in all catalogues (Scrivener, Hoskier), Gregory gave the number 700 to it.2 It is dated palaeographically to the 11th century.3

Description

The codex contains a complete text of the Gospels on 297 parchment leaves (14.8Â cm by 11.7Â cm). The text is written in one column per page, 19 lines per page in minuscule letters.4 The initial letters are in gold. Simple headpieces and tailpieces are in gold.

It contains the Epistula ad Carpianum, Eusebian Canon tables at the beginning of the codex, subscriptions at the end of each Gospel, illustrations of the evangelists, lectionary markings at the margin in gold.5 It lacks Mark 11:26.6

History

The author of the codex is unknown. Probably it was written in Constantinople.18

The manuscript was bought in 1882 for the British Library.19

It was examined by Burgon, described and collated by Simcox, and Scrivener. The manuscript is located now in British Library, Egerton 2610 in London.20

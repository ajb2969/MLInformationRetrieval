YDbDr

YDbDr, sometimes written YDBDR, is the colour space used in the SÉCAM analog terrestrial colour television broadcasting standard, which is used in France and some countries of the former Eastern Bloc. It is very close to YUV (PAL) and its related colour spaces such as YIQ (NTSC), YPbPr and YCbCr.

YDbDr is composed of three components - Y Y Y , D B subscript D B D_{B} and D R subscript D R D_{R} . Y Y Y is the luminance, D B subscript D B D_{B} and D R subscript D R D_{R} are the chrominance components, representing the red and blue colour differences.

Formulas

The three component signals are created from an original RGB (red, green and blue) source. The weighted values of R R R , G G G and B B B are added together to produce a single Y Y Y signal, representing the overall brightness, or luminance, of that spot. The D B subscript D B D_{B} signal is then created by subtracting the Y Y Y from the blue signal of the original RGB, and then scaling; and D R subscript D R D_{R} by subtracting the Y Y Y from the red, and then scaling by a different factor.

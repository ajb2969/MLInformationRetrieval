Inequalities in information theory

Lower bounds for the Kullback‚ÄìLeibler divergence

A great many important inequalities in information theory are actually lower bounds for the Kullback‚ÄìLeibler divergence. Even the Shannon-type inequalities can be considered part of this category, since the bivariate mutual information can be expressed as the Kullback‚ÄìLeibler divergence of the joint distribution with respect to the product of the marginals, and thus these inequalities can be seen as a special case of Gibbs' inequality.

On the other hand, it seems to be much more difficult to derive useful upper bounds for the Kullback‚ÄìLeibler divergence. This is because the Kullback‚ÄìLeibler divergence DKL(P||Q) depends very sensitively on events that are very rare in the reference distribution Q. DKL(P||Q) increases without bound as an event of finite non-zero probability in the distribution P becomes exceedingly rare in the reference distribution Q, and in fact DKL(P||Q) is not even defined if an event of non-zero probability in P has zero probability in Q. (Hence the requirement that P be absolutely continuous with respect to Q.)

Gibbs' inequality

This fundamental inequality states that the Kullback‚ÄìLeibler divergence is non-negative.

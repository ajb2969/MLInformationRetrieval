Shoolery's rule

Shoolery's rule, which is named after James Nelson Shoolery, is a good approximation about Chemical shift δ of Methylene group in Nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy. We can calculate shift of A and B (A–CH2–B) .

δ = 0.23 ppm + S A + S B δ 0.23 ppm subscript S normal-A subscript S normal-B \delta=0.23\,\mathrm{ppm}+S_{\mathrm{A}}+S_{\mathrm{B}} The increments S is calculated by empirical ways.

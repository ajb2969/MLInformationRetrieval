Monoterpene epsilon-lactone hydrolase

Monoterpene epsilon-lactone hydrolase (, MLH) is an enzyme with system name isoprop(en)ylmethyloxepan-2-one lactonohydrolase.1 This enzyme catalyses the following chemical reaction

(1) isoprop(en)ylmethyloxepan-2-one + H2O ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons 6-hydroxyisoprop(en)ylmethylhexanoate (general reaction)
(2) 4-isopropenyl-7-methyloxepan-2-one + H2O ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons 6-hydroxy-3-isopropenylheptanoate
(3) 7-isopropyl-4-methyloxepan-2-one + H2O ⇌ normal-⇌ \rightleftharpoons 6-hydroxy-3,7-dimethyloctanoate

The enzyme catalyses the ring opening of epsilon-lactones in Gram-positive bacterium Rhodococcus erythropolis DCL14.

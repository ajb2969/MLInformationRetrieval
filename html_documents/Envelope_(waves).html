Envelope (waves)

In physics and engineering, the envelope function of an oscillating signal is a smooth curve outlining its extremes.1 The envelope thus generalizes the concept of a constant amplitude. The figure illustrates a modulated sine wave varying between an upper and a lower envelope. The envelope function may be a function of time, space, angle, or indeed of any variable.

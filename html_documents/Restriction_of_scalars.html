Restriction of scalars

In abstract algebra, restriction of scalars is a procedure of creating a module over a ring R R R from a module over another ring S S S , given a homomorphism f : R → S normal-: f normal-→ R S f:R\to S between them. Intuitively speaking, the resulting module "remembers" less information than the initial one, hence the name.

In algebraic geometry, the term "restriction of scalars" is often used as a synonym for Weil restriction.

The case of fields

When both R R R and S S S are fields, f f f is necessarily a monomorphism, and so identifies R R R with a subfield of S S S . In such a case an S S S -module is simply a vector space over S S S , and naturally over any subfield thereof. The module obtained by restriction is then simply a vector space over the subfield R ⊂ S R S R\subset S .

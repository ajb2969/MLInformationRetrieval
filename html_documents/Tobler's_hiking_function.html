Tobler's hiking function

Tobler's hiking function is an exponential function determining the hiking speed, taking into account the slope angle.123 It was formulated by Waldo Tobler.

Formula

Walking velocity:

W = 6 e - 3.5 | d h d x + 0.05 | W 6 superscript e 3.5 d h d x 0.05 W=6e^{\displaystyle-3.5\left|\frac{dh}{dx}+0.05\right|}

where

dh = elevation difference,
dx = distance,
S = slope,
Θ = angle of slope (inclination).

On flat terrain this formula works out to 5 km/h. For off-path travel should be multiplied by 3/5, for horseback by 5/4.4

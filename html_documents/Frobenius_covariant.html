Frobenius covariant

In matrix theory, the Frobenius covariants of a square matrix A A A are special polynomials of it, namely projection matrices Ai associated with the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of A A A .1 They are named after the mathematician Ferdinand Frobenius.

Each covariant is a projection on the eigenspace associated with the eigenvalue . Frobenius covariants are the coefficients of Sylvester's formula, which expresses a function of a matrix f ( A ) f A f(A) as a matrix polynomial, namely a linear combination of that function's values on the eigenvalues of A A A .

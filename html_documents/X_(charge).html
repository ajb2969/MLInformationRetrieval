X (charge)

In particle physics, the X-charge (or simply X) is a conserved quantum number associated with the SO(10) grand unification theory.

X is related to the difference between the baryon number B and the lepton number L (that is B − L), and the weak hypercharge YW via the relation:

X = 5 ( B - L ) - 2 Y W X 5 B L 2 subscript Y W X=5(B-L)-2Y_{W}\, .

Papyrus 106

Papyrus 106 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), designated by ğ”“ ğ”“ \mathfrak{P} 106, is a copy of the New Testament in Greek. It is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of John, containing verses 1:29-35 & 1:40-46 in a fragmentary condition. The manuscript has been paleographically assigned to the early 3rd century.1 The manuscript is currently housed at the Sackler Library (Papyrology Rooms, shelf number P. Oxy. 4445) at Oxford.2

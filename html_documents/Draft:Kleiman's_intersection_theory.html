This is the intersection theory introduced by Snapper in 1959-60 and developed later by Cartier and Kleiman. The basic idea is to intersect cycles with Cartier divisors (in fact line bundles), which results in technical simplification.

Equivariant Chow ring

Let X be an equidimensional smooth scheme with the action of a linear algebraic group G. We note:

  1. A universal G-bundle EG on X admits an approximation by algebraic bundles of arbitrary large codimension. (Note E G E G EG is an ind-scheme.)
  2. X can be covered by invariant quasi-projective open subsets.

(1. is always true and 2. holds by Sumihiro's theorem as X is normal.) Then one defines the equivariant Chow group to be

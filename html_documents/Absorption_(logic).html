Absorption (logic)

Absorption is a valid argument form and rule of inference of propositional logic.12 The rule states that if P P P implies Q Q Q , then P P P implies P P P and Q Q Q . The rule makes it possible to introduce conjunctions to proofs. It is called the law of absorption because the term Q Q Q is "absorbed" by the term P P P in the consequent.3 The rule can be stated:

where the rule is that wherever an instance of " P → Q normal-→ P Q P\to Q " appears on a line of a proof, " P → ( P and Q ) normal-→ P P italic- and Q P\to(P\and Q) " can be placed on a subsequent line.

Examples

If it will rain, then I will wear my coat.
Therefore, if it will rain then it will rain and I will wear my coat.

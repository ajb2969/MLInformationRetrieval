Fractional supersymmetry

In theoretical physics, fractional supersymmetry is a generalization of the notion of supersymmetry in which the minimal positive amount of spin does not have to be 1 / 2 1 2 1/2 but can be an arbitrary 1 / N 1 N 1/N for integer value of N. Such a generalization is possible in two or less spacetime dimensions.

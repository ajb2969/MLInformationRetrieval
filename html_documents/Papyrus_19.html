Papyrus 19

Papyrus 19 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), signed by 𝔓 𝔓 \mathfrak{P} 19, is an early copy of the New Testament in Greek. The manuscript paleographically has been assigned to the 4th or 5th century.1

The papyrus is currently housed at the Bodleian Library, Gr. bibl. d. 6 (P) at the University of Oxford.23

Description

Papyrus 19 is a papyrus manuscript of the Gospel of Matthew, containing text for Matt 10:32-11:5. The leaf is complete at the top and bottom, but broken at the sides.4

The Greek text of this codex is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type. Aland placed it in Category II.5

Notable Readings

Matthew 10:34 has the variant ουν νομίσητε (Therefore, youpl think) instead of μη νομίσητε (Do not think).6

Matthew 10:37b (and the person loving their son or daughter more than me is not worthy of me) is omitted, as in B* D 983 syrh Codex Schøyen

Matthew 10:38 is omitted, as in M*

Matthew 10:37b-38 is also omitted in the Hebrew Shem Tov Matthew manuscript.7

Kostant's convexity theorem

In mathematics, Kostant's convexity theorem, introduced by , states that the projection of every coadjoint orbit of a connected compact Lie group into the dual of a Cartan subalgebra is a convex set. It is a special case of a more general result for symmetric spaces. Kostant's theorem is a generalization of a result of , and for hermitian matrices.

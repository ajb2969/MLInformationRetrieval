F. and M. Riesz theorem

In mathematics, the F. and M. Riesz theorem is a result of the brothers Frigyes Riesz and Marcel Riesz, on analytic measures. It states that for a measure μ on the circle, any part of μ that is not absolutely continuous with respect to the Lebesgue measure dθ can be detected by means of Fourier coefficients. The original statements are rather different (see Zygmund, Trigonometric Series, VII.8). The formulation here is as in Rudin, Real and Complex Analysis, p.335. The proof given uses the Poisson kernel and the existence of boundary values for the Hardy space H1.

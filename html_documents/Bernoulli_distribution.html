In probability theory and statistics, the Bernoulli distribution, named after Swiss scientist Jacob Bernoulli, is the probability distribution of a random variable which takes value 1 with success probability p p p and value 0 with failure probability q = 1 - p q 1 p q=1-p . It can be used, for example, to represent the toss of a (not necessarily fair) coin, where "1" is defined to mean "heads" and "0" is defined to mean "tails" (or vice versa).

The Bernoulli distribution is a special case of the two-point distribution, for which the two possible outcomes need not be 0 and 1.

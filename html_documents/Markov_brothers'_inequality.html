Markov brothers' inequality

In [[mathematics]], the '''Markov brothers' inequality''' is an [[inequality (mathematics)|inequality]] proved in the 1890s by [[Andrey Markov]] and [[Vladimir Andreevich Markov|Vladimir Markov]], two Russian mathematicians and brothers. This inequality bounds the maximum of the [[derivative]]s of a polynomial on an interval in terms of the maximum of the polynomial.{{cite book|last=A

Applications

Markov's inequality is used to obtain lower bounds in computational complexity theory via the so-called "Polynomial Method".

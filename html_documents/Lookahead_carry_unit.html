Lookahead carry unit

A lookahead carry unit (LCU) is a logical unit in digital circuit design used to decrease calculation time in adder units and used in conjunction with carry look-ahead adders (CLAs).

64-bit adder

By combining 4 CLAs and an LCU together creates a 16-bit adder. Four of these units can be combined to form a 64-bit adder. An additional (second-level) LCU is needed that accepts the propagate ( P L C U subscript P L C U P_{LCU} ) and generate ( G L C U subscript G L C U G_{LCU} ) from each LCU and the four carry outputs generated by the second-level LCU are fed into the first-level LCUs.

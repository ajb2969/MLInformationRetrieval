Coadjoint representation

In mathematics, the coadjoint representation K K K of a Lie group G G G is the dual of the adjoint representation. If 𝔤 𝔤 \mathfrak{g} denotes the Lie algebra of G G G , the corresponding action of G G G on 𝔤 * superscript 𝔤 \mathfrak{g}^{*} , the dual space to 𝔤 𝔤 \mathfrak{g} , is called the coadjoint action. A geometrical interpretation is as the action by left-translation on the space of right-invariant 1-forms on G G G .

The importance of the coadjoint representation was emphasised by work of Alexandre Kirillov, who showed that for nilpotent Lie groups G G G a basic role in their representation theory is played by coadjoint orbit. In the Kirillov method of orbits, representations of G G G are constructed geometrically starting from the coadjoint orbits. In some sense those play a substitute role for the conjugacy classes of G G G , which again may be complicated, while the orbits are relatively tractable.

Semipermutable subgroup

In mathematics, in algebra, in the realm of group theory, a subgroup H H H of a finite group G G G is said to be semipermutable if H H H commutes with every subgroup K K K whose order is relatively prime to that of H H H .

Clearly, every permutable subgroup of a finite group is semipermutable. The converse, however, is not necessarily true.

Ritz proposed a new equation, derived from the principles of the ballistic theory of electromagnetic waves. This equation relates the force between two charged particles with a radial separation r relative velocity v and relative acceleration a, where k is an undetermined parameter from the general form of Ampere's force law as proposed by Maxwell. This equation obeys Newton's third law and forms the basis of Ritz's electrodynamics.

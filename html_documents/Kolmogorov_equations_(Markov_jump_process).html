Background

The original derivation of the equations by Kolmogorov 1 starts with the Chapman-Kolmogorov equation (Kolmogorov called it Fundamental equation) for time-continuous and differentiable Markov processes on a finite, discrete state space. In this formulation, it is assumed that the probabilities P ( i , s ; j , t ) P i s j t P(i,s;j,t) are continuous and differentiable functions of t > s t s t>s . Also adequate limit properties for the derivatives are assumed. Feller 2 derives the equations under slightly different conditions, starting with the concept of purely discontinuous Markov process and formulating them for more general state spaces. Feller 3 proves the existence of solutions of probabilistic character to the Kolmogorov forward equations and Kolmogorov backward equations under natural conditions.

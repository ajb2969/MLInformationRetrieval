Schrank

The surname Schrank is an occupational surname of Germanic descent (old high German: scranc). The word schrank loosely translates to "cabinet" (e.g. kühlschrank (compound word meaning literally "cold-cabinet" or refrigerator)) or "closet" (e.g. wäscheschrank or linen-closet). Schränk also translates to "saw." The name "Schrank" may refer to:

  • Franz Paula von Schrank (1747–1835), German botanist and entomologist
  • John F. Schrank (1876–1943), failed American assassin
  • Peter Schrank (born 1952), cartoonist

Other uses:

  • Schrank, a tall wardrobe-like German chest with double doors. Often used as hope chests, these were constructed so as to partially dismantle for transport to the matrimonial home.
  • The term "schrank" is a slang term for a grey crew neck sweat shirt. The expression's usage is limited to neighborhoods in the North East part of the United States where there was once a high concentration of German immigrants and is falling out of use today.

Nikos Aliagas ( / Níkos Aliágas; born Νικόλαος Αλιάγας / Nikólaos Aliágas on May 13, 1969 in Paris) is a French-Greek journalist and entertainer, known for being the host of the French reality program named Star Academy.

One of his parents came from the area of Stamna in Aetolia-Acarnania in Greece. He lived between France and Greece during childhood.

He was a guest star on a Greek program Koita ti Ekanes in late-2003 and featured clips from Star Academy. He has published a book called "I was born Greek: The mythology or The school of life" (). He was the presenter of a show on Alpha TV called "Gros Plan" where he met international stars like Celine Dion, Jean Paul Gaultier, Sylvester Stallone and the singer Helena Paparizou.1 He's the French presenter of The Voice: la plus belle voix.

He speaks five languages (as of 2003) including English.

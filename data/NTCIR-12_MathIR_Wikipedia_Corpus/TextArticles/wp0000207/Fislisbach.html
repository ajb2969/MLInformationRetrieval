Fislisbach

Fislisbach is a municipality in the district of Baden in the canton of Aargau in Switzerland.

History

Fislisbach is first mentioned in 1184 as Vicelisbach.1

Geography

Fislisbach has an area, , of . Of this area, 47.5% is used for agricultural purposes, while 30.6% is forested. The rest of the land, (22%) is settled.2

The municipality is located in the Baden district at the foot of the Heitersberg. It consists of the linear village of Fislisbach and scattered single and multi-family housing developments.

The blazon of the municipal coat of arms is Azure a Cross Lorraine pattee fitchy patonce Argent3

Economy

, Fislisbach had an unemployment rate of 2.48%. , there were 102 people employed in the primary economic sector and about 15 businesses involved in this sector. 229 people are employed in the secondary sector and there are 30 businesses in this sector. 788 people are employed in the tertiary sector, with 129 businesses in this sector.14 there were 2,751 total workers who lived in the municipality. Of these, 2,321 or about 84.4% of the residents worked outside Fislisbach while 582 people commuted into the municipality for work. There were a total of 1,012 jobs (of at least 6 hours per week) in the municipality.15
